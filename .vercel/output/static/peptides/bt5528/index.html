BT5528

Also known as: Bicycle Toxin Conjugate 5528, EphA2-BTC

A first-in-class Bicycle Toxin Conjugate (BTC) targeting EphA2-expressing tumors, developed by Bicycle Therapeutics. Combines a constrained bicyclic peptide targeting moiety with the cytotoxic payload MMAE. Phase 1/2 dose expansion ongoing with 67% ORR reported in EphA2-positive urothelial cancer patients. Represents a novel therapeutic modality bridging small molecules and antibody-drug conjugates.

  • Developer: Bicycle Therapeutics (Cambridge, UK/USA)

What is a Bicycle Toxin Conjugate?

Bicycle Toxin Conjugates represent a novel therapeutic modality that combines:

  1. Bicyclic peptide — A chemically constrained peptide that forms two interlocking rings, creating a highly stable, compact structure (~1.5-2 kDa) that binds targets with antibody-like affinity
  2. Cleavable linker — Connects the targeting peptide to the cytotoxic payload; designed to release the payload inside tumor cells
  3. Cytotoxic payload — In BT5528, this is MMAE, a potent microtubule-disrupting agent

Why this matters: BTCs bridge the gap between small molecules and antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs):

  • Smaller than antibodies (~7 kDa vs ~150 kDa) — enables deeper tumor penetration
  • Faster clearance — potentially reduced systemic toxicity
  • Antibody-like specificity — selective targeting of tumor-expressed antigens
  • No Fc-mediated effects — avoids some ADC-associated toxicities

Why EphA2 as a Target?

EphA2 (Ephrin type-A receptor 2) is an attractive oncology target because:

  1. Overexpressed in multiple solid tumors:

    • Urothelial/bladder cancer: 50-70% of tumors
    • Non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC): 60-80%
    • Ovarian cancer: 70-90%
    • Pancreatic cancer: 70-80%
    • Gastric/esophageal cancer: 60-70%
    • Triple-negative breast cancer: 50-60%

  2. Limited expression in normal tissues:

    • Low levels in normal epithelium
    • Provides therapeutic window for targeted therapy

  3. Associated with aggressive disease:

    • High EphA2 expression correlates with poor prognosis
    • Associated with metastasis and treatment resistance

  4. No approved EphA2-directed therapies:

    • BT5528 is first-in-class
    • Prior EphA2-targeting approaches (ADCs, CAR-T) had limited success

Mechanism of Action

How BT5528 Works (Plain English)

BT5528 works like a “smart missile” that finds cancer cells and delivers a toxic payload directly to them:

  1. Finding the target — The bicyclic peptide portion of BT5528 circulates through your bloodstream until it encounters EphA2, a protein that is abnormally abundant on the surface of many cancer cells. The peptide binds tightly and specifically to EphA2.

  2. Getting inside — Once bound, the cancer cell swallows the BT5528 molecule through a process called receptor-mediated endocytosis. This is like the cell grabbing and engulfing the drug.

  3. Releasing the payload — Inside the cell, the linker connecting the peptide to the toxin is cleaved by cellular enzymes (cathepsins), releasing the MMAE payload.

  4. Killing the cancer cell — Free MMAE disrupts microtubules, which are essential structures cells need to divide. Without functional microtubules, the cancer cell cannot divide and dies.

Why small size matters: Because BT5528 is much smaller than antibody-drug conjugates (~7 kDa vs ~150 kDa), it can penetrate solid tumors more effectively. Think of it like the difference between a motorcycle and a truck navigating through traffic — the smaller vehicle can reach places the larger one cannot.

MMAE-Related Toxicities

As BT5528 uses MMAE as its cytotoxic payload, certain toxicities are expected based on experience with other MMAE-conjugates (brentuximab vedotin, enfortumab vedotin):

1. Peripheral Neuropathy

  • Mechanism: MMAE disrupts microtubules essential for nerve function
  • Presentation: Numbness, tingling, pain in hands/feet
  • Management: Dose modification, supportive care
  • Usually reversible with treatment interruption

2. Neutropenia

  • Mechanism: MMAE affects rapidly dividing bone marrow cells
  • Management: Dose delays, G-CSF support if needed
  • Monitoring: Regular CBC required

3. Skin Reactions

  • May be partially target-related (EphA2 expression in skin)
  • Generally mild and manageable

Comparison to EphA2-Targeting ADCs

Prior EphA2-targeting approaches using traditional antibody-drug conjugates showed limited clinical success due to:

  • Poor tumor penetration of large antibodies
  • High systemic exposure leading to toxicity
  • Insufficient therapeutic window

BT5528 was designed to address these limitations through:

  • Smaller size enabling better tumor penetration
  • Faster clearance reducing systemic exposure
  • Chemical synthesis enabling consistent manufacturing
