# Researcher Identification Criteria

*What makes content worth covering*
*Created: 2026-01-27*

---

## Core Question

**Before covering any research, ask:**
> "Will this help our audience make more informed decisions about peptides?"

If no → skip.
If yes → evaluate using criteria below.

---

## Research Selection Criteria

| Factor | Weight | Questions to Ask |
|--------|--------|------------------|
| **Evidence Quality** | High | Is there human data? RCTs? Meta-analyses? Peer-reviewed? |
| **Relevance** | High | Does this affect peptide users directly? Is it actionable? |
| **Timeliness** | Medium | Is this new research? Breaking news? Trend? |
| **Uniqueness** | Medium | Are competitors covering this? Can we add value? |
| **Traffic Potential** | Low | Are people searching for this topic? |

---

## Evidence Quality Scoring

| Score | Description | Examples |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **5 - Strong** | Multiple RCTs, meta-analyses, FDA approval | Semaglutide weight loss trials |
| **4 - Moderate** | Single RCT, strong observational studies | Tirzepatide Phase 3 data |
| **3 - Emerging** | Human pilot studies, multiple animal studies | BPC-157 gut studies |
| **2 - Early** | Single animal studies, case reports | Novel peptide discoveries |
| **1 - Theoretical** | In-vitro only, mechanism speculation | "May work because..." |

**Threshold:** Score 2+ to cover. Score 1 only if exceptionally relevant/timely.

---

## Red Flags (Skip)

Automatically skip content if:

| Red Flag | Why |
|----------|-----|
| **Only in-vitro data** | No clinical relevance yet |
| **Single animal study** | Not replicated, could be noise |
| **Press release without paper** | Can't verify claims |
| **Conflicts of interest not disclosed** | Potential bias |
| **Published in predatory journal** | Quality concerns |
| **Dosing/protocol focus** | Against editorial policy |
| **Vendor/sourcing angle** | Against editorial policy |
| **Celebrity endorsement as "evidence"** | Not research |

**Exception:** Cover for myth-busting if the claim is widespread and harmful.

## Content Types by Evidence Level

| Evidence Level | Appropriate Content |
|----------------|---------------------|
| **Known** (Strong evidence) | Full dossier, definitive blog posts, comparisons |
| **Suggestive** (Moderate evidence) | Blog posts with caveats, "what we know" format |
| **Early** (Emerging evidence) | "Promising but unproven" articles, myth-busting |
| **Unknown** (Insufficient evidence) | FAQ answers, "we don't know yet" content |

## Questions to Ask Before Covering

### For New Research
1. Is this peer-reviewed?
2. Sample size?
3. Study design (RCT > observational > case report)?
4. Conflicts of interest?
5. Does it replicate previous findings?
6. What are the limitations?
7. Can we cite the original paper?

### For News/Announcements
1. Is this verified by multiple sources?
2. Is there an official statement?
3. What's the actual regulatory implication?
4. Is this actionable for our audience?

### For Trends/Questions
1. Are people actually asking this?
2. Can we provide evidence-based answer?
3. Is this in our wheelhouse?
4. Can we add value beyond Google results?

## Competitive Differentiation

**What we cover that others don't:**
- Honest evidence grading (not hype)
- Animal vs. human study distinction
- Regulatory context
- What we DON'T know

**What we DON'T cover that others do:**
- Dosing protocols
- Sourcing information
- Anecdotal reports as evidence
- Celebrity endorsements

## Researcher Workflow

### When New Study Drops

1. **Assess (5 min)**
   - Check journal quality
   - Read abstract
   - Note study type, sample size
   - Score evidence quality

2. **Decide (2 min)**
   - Use decision matrix above
   - If skip → log reason
   - If cover → determine content type

3. **Queue (3 min)**
   - Add to content backlog
   - Note priority and deadline
   - Assign content type

### Weekly Research Review

1. Review PubMed alerts
2. Check ClinicalTrials.gov
3. Scan competitor content
4. Update backlog priorities

## Example Evaluations

### Example 1: Strong Coverage
**Study:** "Tirzepatide vs Semaglutide: 72-week RCT (n=2,000)"
- Evidence: 5/5 (large RCT)
- Relevance: High (direct comparison)
- Timeliness: New
**Decision:** Immediate blog post + comparison update + newsletter feature

### Example 2: Moderate Coverage
**Study:** "BPC-157 and gut healing in rats (n=40)"
- Evidence: 2/5 (animal only)
- Relevance: High (popular topic)
- Timeliness: Moderate
**Decision:** Add to dossier, mention in carousel about BPC-157 evidence

### Example 3: Skip
**News:** "Celebrity says peptides changed their life"
- Evidence: 0/5 (anecdote)
- Relevance: Low (not actionable)
- Timeliness: High
**Decision:** Skip entirely. Don't contribute to hype.

### Example 4: Myth-Bust
**Claim:** "BPC-157 is FDA approved" (spreading on social)
- Evidence: 0/5 (false claim)
- Relevance: High (common misconception)
- Timeliness: Ongoing
**Decision:** Create myth-busting carousel/story
