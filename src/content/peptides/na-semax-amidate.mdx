## Overview

### Quick Summary

- **Modified version of Semax** with N-acetylation and C-terminal amidation
- **No independent clinical research** — all claims extrapolated from parent Semax studies
- **Not approved** by any regulatory agency (Russia approved unmodified Semax in 1990s)
- **Marketed claims:** Enhanced stability, prolonged effects, improved bioavailability
- **Evidence base:** Extremely limited; modifications based on peptide chemistry theory
- **Status:** Research chemical only

**Evidence Strength: Very Low** — Semax has moderate evidence from extensive Russian research, but N-Acetyl Semax Amidate specifically has no published research. Claims are theoretical rather than demonstrated.

## Mechanism of Action

### Semax Background (Parent Compound)

Semax is derived from ACTH(4-7), the melanocortin fragment with central nervous system activity, extended with Pro-Gly-Pro for stability:

```
ACTH → ACTH(4-7) → Semax (Met-Glu-His-Phe-Pro-Gly-Pro)
         ↓
   No hormonal activity retained
   Only CNS/nootropic effects
```
