HMG

Also known as: Human Menopausal Gonadotropin, Menotropins, hMG, Menopur, Pergonal, Repronex, HP-hMG

A urinary-derived glycoprotein hormone preparation containing both FSH and LH in approximately 1:1 ratio, FDA-approved for female infertility (ovulation induction, ART) and male hypogonadotropic hypogonadism. First used clinically in 1961 by Bruno Lunenfeld, HMG enabled the birth of the first American IVF baby in 1981 and remains a cornerstone of fertility medicine with over 60 years of clinical experience. Highly purified preparations (HP-hMG) demonstrate comparable or slightly superior live birth rates compared to recombinant FSH in IVF.

Overview

Quick Summary

  • FDA-approved urinary gonadotropin preparation containing both FSH and LH for ovulation induction, assisted reproductive technology (ART), and male hypogonadotropic hypogonadism
  • Historical significance: First successful clinical use in 1961 by Bruno Lunenfeld; instrumental in the first American IVF baby (Elizabeth Carr, 1981)
  • Strong evidence base: Multiple Cochrane systematic reviews, Phase 3 RCTs, international fertility guidelines (ASRM, ESHRE), and over 60 years of clinical experience
  • Current formulations: Highly purified hMG (HP-hMG) preparations like Menopur; earlier products Pergonal and Repronex have been discontinued
  • Comparable to recombinant FSH: Meta-analyses show HP-hMG achieves similar or slightly better live birth rates in IVF, particularly in conventional IVF vs ICSI
  • Key safety concern: Ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome (OHSS) in 1-5% of cycles; multiple pregnancy risk with ovulation induction

Evidence Strength: HIGH — Supported by decades of clinical use, multiple Cochrane systematic reviews, FDA approval for specific indications, and endorsement by international fertility societies. One of the foundational treatments in reproductive endocrinology.

Molecular Composition

HMG is a mixture of glycoprotein hormones extracted and purified from the urine of postmenopausal women:

FSH (Follicle-Stimulating Hormone):

  • Heterodimer: Alpha subunit (92 aa) + Beta subunit (111 aa)
  • Molecular weight: ~30,000 Da
  • Primary function: Stimulates follicular development in ovaries; supports spermatogenesis in testes

LH (Luteinizing Hormone):

  • Heterodimer: Alpha subunit (92 aa) + Beta subunit (121 aa)
  • Molecular weight: ~28,000 Da
  • Primary function: Triggers ovulation; stimulates steroidogenesis

Important note on LH activity: In HP-hMG preparations, the LH bioactivity is partially derived from supplementary hCG added during manufacturing. This is because purification processes cause preferential loss of native LH activity, which is compensated with pharmacologically significant quantities of hCG that binds the same receptor.

Why HMG Was Developed

Natural FSH and LH are pituitary hormones essential for reproduction:

  • In women: FSH recruits and develops ovarian follicles; LH triggers ovulation and supports the corpus luteum
  • In men: FSH supports Sertoli cells and spermatogenesis; LH stimulates Leydig cells to produce testosterone

The clinical need: Women with hypothalamic-pituitary dysfunction (hypogonadotropic hypogonadism) cannot produce adequate FSH and LH, preventing ovulation. Men with this condition have impaired testosterone production and spermatogenesis.

Why postmenopausal urine: After menopause, the ovaries no longer respond to FSH and LH. The pituitary continues secreting these hormones, which accumulate in urine and can be extracted. This provided an abundant source before recombinant technology.

Mechanism of Action

How HMG Works (Plain English)

HMG provides both of the “master hormones” needed for reproduction. Think of it as giving the body the signals it needs to:

  1. Grow eggs (in women) — The FSH component tells the ovaries to develop multiple follicles, each containing an egg. Without FSH, follicles don’t mature properly.

  2. Prepare for ovulation — The LH component helps follicles mature fully and prepares them for release. LH also stimulates estrogen production, which prepares the uterus.

  3. Support egg quality — Having both FSH and LH (rather than FSH alone) may improve oocyte quality and the cells surrounding the egg, potentially leading to better embryos.

  4. Restore testosterone production (in men) — The LH activity stimulates the testicles to produce testosterone, and both hormones together support sperm production.

Why both hormones matter: The ovary needs a “dialogue” between FSH and LH. FSH alone can develop follicles, but the addition of LH activity appears to improve steroidogenesis (hormone production) and may enhance oocyte quality in some patient populations.

The “Two-Cell, Two-Gonadotropin” Model

Ovarian steroidogenesis requires coordinated FSH and LH action:

  1. LH acts on theca cells → Produces androgens (androstenedione)
  2. Androgens diffuse to granulosa cells
  3. FSH acts on granulosa cells → Activates aromatase
  4. Aromatase converts androgens to estrogens

This explains why HMG (with both FSH and LH) may provide more physiologic stimulation than FSH alone in some patients.

Supported Mechanisms (Human Evidence)

Follicular development:

  • Multiple follicle growth demonstrated in IVF [Cochrane PMID: 21328277]
  • Dose-dependent oocyte yield established in clinical trials
  • Optimal starting doses: 150-225 IU/day in most protocols

Steroidogenesis:

  • Estradiol production correlates with follicle development
  • LH activity supports androgen substrate for estrogen synthesis
  • HP-hMG may produce different steroid profiles than rFSH alone

Male spermatogenesis:

  • 75-85% success rate in inducing spermatogenesis in hypogonadotropic males [PMID: 3928676]
  • Combined hCG + HMG therapy more effective than hCG alone initially

Key Meta-Analysis Findings: HMG vs Recombinant FSH

Cochrane Review 2011 [PMID: 21328277]:

  • 42 RCTs with 9,606 women
  • HMG/HP-hMG associated with significantly higher live birth rates than rFSH
  • Odds ratio 0.84 (95% CI 0.72-0.99) favoring HMG
  • ~4% absolute increase in live birth rate with HMG

Al-Inany et al. 2008/2009 [PMID: 18056719; PMID: 19340668]:

  • HP-hMG associated with 3-4% higher live birth rate vs rFSH in long agonist protocols
  • Benefit most pronounced in IVF (not ICSI) cycles
  • Statistical significance reached in pooled analysis

Coomarasamy et al. 2008 [PMID: 18056719]:

  • 7 RCTs, 2,159 women
  • Relative risk for live birth: 1.18 (95% CI 1.02-1.38) favoring HMG
  • Fewer gonadotropin ampoules required with HMG

IVF Outcomes with HP-hMG (Menopur)

Andersen et al. 2006 [PMID: 16873892]:

  • 731 patients across 37 centers in Europe and Israel
  • HP-hMG vs rFSH in GnRH agonist long protocol
  • HP-hMG: 11.3% top-quality embryos vs rFSH: 9.0%
  • Ongoing pregnancy rate higher in HP-hMG IVF subgroup (31% vs 20%)

MEGASET-HR Trial [PMID: 29345163]:

  • 620 predicted high-responder women
  • HP-hMG: 35.5% ongoing pregnancy rate vs rFSH: 30.7%
  • Lower OHSS incidence with HP-hMG
  • Lower total treatment cost per live birth

In Vitro Studies

Receptor binding:

  • FSH binds FSHR with high affinity (Kd ~1-10 nM)
  • LH/hCG binds LHCGR with high affinity
  • Both receptors couple to Galphas/cAMP pathway

Steroidogenesis models:

  • Theca-granulosa co-culture confirms two-cell model
  • FSH alone produces less estradiol than FSH + LH
  • HP-hMG stimulates physiologic steroid ratios

Limitations of Preclinical Data

  • Rodent ovarian physiology differs significantly from humans
  • Primate studies limited but most relevant
  • Urinary-derived product complexity difficult to model
  • Species differences in receptor expression patterns

Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome: Detailed Assessment

What is OHSS: OHSS is the most significant complication of gonadotropin therapy, characterized by:

  • Ovarian enlargement with multiple cysts
  • Capillary leak leading to third-space fluid accumulation
  • Ascites, pleural effusion, hemoconcentration
  • In severe cases: renal failure, ARDS, thromboembolism

OHSS Classification:

GradeSymptomsManagement
MildBloating, mild discomfort, ovaries <8 cmOutpatient monitoring
ModerateNausea, vomiting, ultrasound evidence of ascites, ovaries 8-12 cmClose monitoring, hydration
SevereTense ascites, oliguria, hemoconcentration, ovaries >12 cmHospitalization
CriticalRenal failure, ARDS, thromboembolismICU care

Risk factors for OHSS with HMG:

  1. Young age (<35 years)
  2. Low body weight/BMI
  3. Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
  4. High antral follicle count (>24)
  5. Elevated AMH levels
  6. High estradiol during stimulation
  7. Large number of developing follicles
  8. Use of hCG trigger (vs GnRH agonist)
  9. Previous OHSS history

OHSS incidence data:

  • SHOview study [PMID: 28461237]: 2.4% moderate-severe OHSS with HP-hMG
  • General IVF population: 1-5% moderate; <1% severe
  • PCOS population: Up to 12.5% OHSS in some studies

Prevention strategies:

  • Individualized starting doses based on AFC/AMH
  • Careful monitoring with estradiol and ultrasound
  • GnRH antagonist protocols (allow agonist trigger)
  • GnRH agonist trigger in high-risk patients
  • Coasting (withhold gonadotropins if E2 excessive)
  • Freeze-all strategy (no fresh transfer)
  • Lower total gonadotropin exposure

Multiple Pregnancy Risk

Multiple pregnancy is an expected consequence of multi-follicular development:

SettingTwin RateHigher-Order Multiple Rate
Ovulation induction (non-IVF)15-25%3-5%
IVF with single embryo transfer<5%<1%
IVF with double embryo transfer20-30%1-2%

Mitigation: Single embryo transfer policies in IVF have dramatically reduced multiple pregnancy rates while maintaining acceptable pregnancy rates.

Contraindications

  1. Primary ovarian failure (elevated FSH/low AMH)
  2. Uncontrolled thyroid or adrenal dysfunction
  3. Sex hormone-dependent tumors (breast, ovarian, uterine, testicular)
  4. Abnormal uterine bleeding of undetermined origin
  5. Ovarian cyst or enlargement not due to PCOS
  6. Pregnancy
  7. Known allergy to menotropins or components

Clinical Dosing

Ovulation induction (non-IVF):

  • Starting dose: 75-150 IU/day
  • Route: Subcutaneous or intramuscular
  • Adjustment: Increase by 37.5-75 IU increments every 7 days if no response
  • Maximum: 225-450 IU/day
  • Duration: Until follicle(s) mature (typically 7-12 days)
  • Trigger: hCG 5,000-10,000 IU when lead follicle ≥18mm

IVF/ART protocols:

  • Starting dose: 150-225 IU/day (individualized based on AFC, AMH, age)
  • GnRH agonist protocol: Start after pituitary down-regulation
  • GnRH antagonist protocol: Start on cycle day 2-3
  • Monitoring: Estradiol + ultrasound every 2-3 days
  • Duration: 8-14 days typically
  • Trigger: hCG or GnRH agonist when ≥2-3 follicles ≥17mm

Male hypogonadotropic hypogonadism:

  • hCG: 500-2500 IU 2-3 times weekly (establish testosterone first)
  • HMG: 75-150 IU 2-3 times weekly (add after 3-6 months hCG)
  • Duration: 12-24 months for spermatogenesis induction
  • Monitoring: Testosterone levels, semen analysis

Administration

  • Reconstitution: HP-hMG supplied as lyophilized powder; reconstitute with supplied diluent
  • Injection volume: 0.5-1 mL per injection
  • Subcutaneous sites: Abdomen, thigh (rotate sites)
  • Intramuscular sites: Upper outer quadrant of gluteus or lateral thigh
  • Storage: Reconstituted solution stable 28 days refrigerated (product-specific)

FDA-Approved Indications (Menopur)

Women:

  1. Development of multiple follicles in ovulatory patients as part of an Assisted Reproductive Technology (ART) cycle
  2. Induction of ovulation and pregnancy in anovulatory infertile patients in whom the cause of infertility is functional and not due to primary ovarian failure

Men:

  1. Induction of spermatogenesis in men with primary or secondary hypogonadotropic hypogonadism in whom the cause of infertility is not due to primary testicular failure

Off-Label Uses

The following are not FDA-approved but may be encountered:

  • Male fertility preservation during testosterone therapy (usually hCG + HMG)
  • Controlled ovarian hyperstimulation in surrogacy
  • Egg donation protocols

WADA Status

HMG/menotropins are not specifically listed by WADA as prohibited substances. However:

  • FSH and LH are endogenous hormones
  • Use in males could theoretically affect testosterone production
  • Athletes should verify with sports medicine physicians

Key points for this dossier:

  • HMG is a foundational fertility treatment with over 60 years of clinical experience
  • FDA-approved for ovulation induction, ART, and male hypogonadotropic hypogonadism
  • Contains both FSH and LH activity in approximately 1:1 ratio
  • Cochrane reviews show slight advantage over recombinant FSH for live birth rates
  • Primary safety concern is OHSS, which is preventable with appropriate monitoring
  • Current formulation (Menopur/HP-hMG) is highly purified with consistent quality
  • Historical significance: enabled first American IVF baby (1981)
