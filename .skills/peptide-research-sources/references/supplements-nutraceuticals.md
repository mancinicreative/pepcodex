# Supplement & Nutraceutical Databases

Comprehensive guide to databases focused on dietary supplements, nutraceuticals, and natural products.

---

## NIH Office of Dietary Supplements (ODS)

**URL:** https://ods.od.nih.gov
**Trust:** 5/5 | **Access:** Free | **Authority:** U.S. National Institutes of Health

### What It Is
The NIH Office of Dietary Supplements provides authoritative, evidence-based information about dietary supplements. Gold standard for supplement research in the United States.

### Key Resources

#### Fact Sheets
**URL:** https://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/list-all/

Evidence-based summaries for vitamins, minerals, and popular supplements. Available in two versions:
- **Health Professional:** Comprehensive, cited, technical
- **Consumer:** Simplified, accessible language

```
Example topics: Vitamin D, Omega-3 Fatty Acids, Probiotics,
Iron, Magnesium, Zinc, Selenium, Vitamin B12, Folate
```

#### DSLD (Dietary Supplement Label Database)
**URL:** https://dsld.od.nih.gov

Database of dietary supplement product labels sold in the United States.
- Product names and manufacturers
- Ingredient lists with amounts
- Serving sizes
- Label claims
- Historical label versions

**Search Examples:**
```
Search by ingredient: "BPC-157" or "Thymosin"
Search by brand: Product name
Browse by category: Amino Acids, Peptides
Compare products: Same ingredient, different brands
```

**Use Cases:**
- Verify what's in commercial products
- Compare formulations across brands
- Track ingredient dosing in products
- Research market availability

#### DSID (Dietary Supplement Ingredient Database)
**URL:** https://dsid.od.nih.gov

Compares actual nutrient content vs. labeled amounts based on analytical testing.
- Validation of label accuracy
- Nutrient variability data
- Adult and children's multivitamins
- Omega-3 products

**Key Insight:** Reveals discrepancies between what labels claim and what products actually contain.

#### CARDS (Computer Access to Research on Dietary Supplements)
**URL:** https://ods.od.nih.gov/Research/CARDS_Database.aspx

Specialized literature database for dietary supplement research.
- Curated supplement citations
- Links to PubMed abstracts
- Research funding information
- Conference proceedings

### When to Use ODS
- Starting supplement research (Fact Sheets)
- Verifying commercial product contents (DSLD)
- Checking label accuracy studies (DSID)
- Finding supplement-specific literature (CARDS)
- Establishing baseline evidence for ingredients

### Tips
- Fact Sheets updated regularly - check revision dates
- DSLD covers products sold in U.S. only
- Cross-reference Fact Sheet citations in PubMed
- Use DSID to understand typical label accuracy ranges

### Trust Rationale
NIH-maintained, expert-reviewed, regularly updated. No commercial bias. The most authoritative free supplement resource available.

## Natural Medicines Database

**URL:** https://naturalmedicines.therapeuticresearch.com
**Trust:** 5/5 | **Access:** Subscription (institutional/professional)
**Coverage:** 1,300+ natural ingredients

### What It Is
Comprehensive clinical decision support tool for natural medicines, formerly Natural Medicines Comprehensive Database. Used by healthcare professionals, pharmacists, and researchers.

### Key Features

#### Professional Monographs
Detailed ingredient profiles including:
- Scientific names and synonyms
- Uses and efficacy ratings
- Mechanism of action
- Adverse effects
- Drug interactions
- Dosing information
- Safety ratings (pregnancy, lactation)

#### Effectiveness Grades
Evidence-based effectiveness ratings:
- **Effective:** Strong scientific evidence
- **Likely Effective:** Good evidence
- **Possibly Effective:** Some evidence
- **Possibly Ineffective:** Evidence against
- **Likely Ineffective:** Good evidence against
- **Ineffective:** Strong evidence against
- **Insufficient Evidence:** Unknown

#### Interaction Checker
- Drug-supplement interactions
- Supplement-supplement interactions
- Severity ratings
- Clinical significance
- Management recommendations

#### Additional Tools
- Brand name products database
- Patient handouts
- Continuing education modules
- Adverse event reports

### Example Searches
```
Ingredient: Curcumin, CoQ10, Melatonin, DHEA
Condition: "diabetes supplements", "cognitive enhancement"
Interaction: Warfarin + supplements
```

### Access Options
- Individual professional subscription
- Institutional access (universities, hospitals)
- Some content via integration with clinical tools

### When to Use
- Clinical decision-making on supplements
- Checking drug-supplement interactions
- Evidence grades for effectiveness
- Patient counseling resources
- Professional-level monographs

### Tips
- Check the "Bottom Line" section for quick summaries
- Interaction checker is highly comprehensive
- Download patient handouts for education
- Compare effectiveness grades across conditions

### Trust Rationale
Peer-reviewed by PharmD-level experts, updated continuously, widely used in clinical practice. Industry gold standard for natural medicine information.

## ConsumerLab

**URL:** https://www.consumerlab.com
**Trust:** 4/5 | **Access:** Subscription ($59/year typical)
**Coverage:** 1,000+ products tested

### What It Is
Independent laboratory that tests supplement products for quality, purity, and label accuracy. Consumer-focused but valuable for research.

### Key Features

#### Product Testing Reports
- Actual ingredient amounts vs. label claims
- Contamination testing (heavy metals, microbes)
- Disintegration/dissolution testing
- Cost comparisons (cost per dose)

#### Quality Ratings
- **Approved:** Passed all tests
- **Not Approved:** Failed one or more tests
- Conditional approvals with notes

#### Categories Tested
```
Vitamins & minerals, Omega-3s, Probiotics, Protein powders,
Herbs (curcumin, ashwagandha, etc.), Sports nutrition,
Amino acids, Specialty supplements
```

#### Ingredient Reviews
- Evidence summaries
- Optimal dosing
- Form comparisons (e.g., curcumin types)
- Quality considerations

### Limitations
- Primarily U.S. products
- Not all products/brands covered
- Consumer-oriented (less technical)
- Testing methodology not always detailed

### When to Use
- Evaluating specific product quality
- Comparing brands for the same ingredient
- Checking for contamination issues
- Cost-effectiveness analysis
- Consumer recommendations

### Tips
- Check "Top Picks" for category leaders
- Read full reports for methodology
- Note test dates (products change)
- Use ingredient reviews for dosing guidance

### Trust Rationale
Independent, no manufacturer funding, consistent methodology. Well-regarded but some limitations on transparency. Good for product-level quality data.

## NSF International

**URL:** https://www.nsf.org
**Product Search:** https://www.nsf.org/consumer-resources/product-listings
**Trust:** 5/5 | **Access:** Free (verification) | **Authority:** ANSI-accredited

### What It Is
Independent, accredited organization that tests and certifies products for safety and quality. NSF certification is widely recognized and respected.

### Key Certification Programs

#### Certified for Sport
**URL:** https://www.nsfsport.com

Products tested for banned substances per WADA (World Anti-Doping Agency) and professional sports leagues.
- Over 270+ banned substances screened
- Label claim verification
- Contaminant testing
- Lot-by-lot testing

**Used by:** NFL, MLB, NHL, PGA, LPGA, CCES, Taylor Hooton Foundation

#### GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice) Certification
Facility audits for manufacturing quality:
- Production processes
- Quality control systems
- Facility conditions
- Documentation practices

#### Dietary Supplement Certification
Product-level verification:
- Label accuracy
- Contaminant testing
- Facility GMP compliance

### Verification Search
```
Search certified products: nsf.org/certified-products-systems
Certified for Sport list: nsfsport.com/certified-products
```

### When to Use
- Verifying product certifications
- Athletes checking for banned substances
- Evaluating manufacturer quality systems
- Recommending vetted products

### Tips
- Always verify current certification status
- "Certified for Sport" is athlete-specific
- GMP certification indicates facility quality
- Certification scopes vary - check details

### Trust Rationale
ANSI-accredited third party, no commercial bias, rigorous testing standards. Among the most trusted certifications in the supplement industry.

## USP (United States Pharmacopeia)

**URL:** https://www.usp.org
**Verified Products:** https://www.quality-supplements.org
**Trust:** 5/5 | **Access:** Free (verification), Reference Standards (purchase)

### What It Is
Independent, scientific nonprofit that sets quality standards for medicines, supplements, and food ingredients. USP standards are recognized globally and referenced by FDA.

### Key Programs

#### USP Verified Mark
Voluntary verification program for dietary supplements:
- **Identity:** Contains listed ingredients
- **Potency:** Amounts match label
- **Purity:** No harmful contaminants
- **Performance:** Will properly dissolve

**Verification Process:**
- Product testing against USP standards
- Manufacturing facility audits
- Ongoing monitoring
- Annual re-verification

#### Dietary Supplement Compendium
Monographs with quality specifications:
- Identity tests
- Assay methods
- Purity limits
- Dissolution requirements

#### Reference Standards
Authenticated materials for laboratory testing:
- Chemical reference standards
- Biological reference standards
- Used by manufacturers and labs

### Verified Product Search
```
URL: quality-supplements.org/verified-products
Search by brand, ingredient, or product name
```

### When to Use
- Verifying product quality marks
- Setting quality specifications
- Laboratory method development
- Regulatory compliance research

### Tips
- USP Verified is premium certification
- Check verification expiration dates
- USP standards are publicly accessible
- Reference standards available for purchase

### Trust Rationale
Independent nonprofit, 200+ year history, globally recognized. USP Verified is among the highest quality certifications available for supplements.

## Examine.com

**URL:** https://examine.com
**Trust:** 4/5 | **Access:** Free (basic), Subscription (premium ~$29/month)
**Coverage:** 400+ supplements, 800+ health topics

### What It Is
Independent research organization providing evidence-based analysis of supplements and nutrition. Known for accessible, well-referenced summaries.

### Key Features

#### Free Content
- Supplement guides (basic information)
- Quick summaries
- References to primary literature
- Human Effect Matrix (summary tables)

#### Premium Content
- **Research Digests:** Monthly summaries of new research
- **Supplement Guides:** In-depth analysis (50-100+ pages)
- **Study Summaries:** Detailed breakdowns
- **Examine+:** Full database access

### Human Effect Matrix
Visual summary showing:
- Health outcomes studied
- Effect magnitude
- Level of evidence
- Number of studies

```
Example: Creatine
├── Power Output: Notable increase (Strong evidence)
├── Lean Mass: Minor increase (Strong evidence)
├── Fatigue: Minor decrease (Moderate evidence)
└── Cognition: Minor increase (Limited evidence)
```

### Supplement Stack Guides
Condition-specific recommendations:
- Fat Loss
- Muscle Gain
- Mood & Depression
- Sleep Quality
- Joint Health

### Example Searches
```
Ingredients: Ashwagandha, Berberine, NMN, Creatine
Conditions: "testosterone", "sleep", "anxiety"
Topics: "examine creatine" (Google often faster)
```

### Limitations
- Some content behind paywall
- Not all peptides covered
- Consumer-oriented (may oversimplify)
- Updates can lag for newer compounds

### When to Use
- Quick evidence summaries
- Starting point for supplement research
- Dosing guidance
- Understanding common supplements
- Finding primary literature references

### Tips
- Check "Scientific Research" section for citations
- Human Effect Matrix for quick overview
- Free content often sufficient for basics
- Cross-reference citations in PubMed

### Trust Rationale
Independent, no advertising or affiliate income from supplement companies. Well-referenced but consumer-focused. Good starting point, verify with primary literature.

## EFSA (European Food Safety Authority)

**URL:** https://www.efsa.europa.eu
**Trust:** 5/5 | **Access:** Free | **Authority:** European Union regulatory body

### What It Is
EU agency providing scientific advice on food and feed safety, including dietary supplements, functional foods, and health claims.

### Key Resources

#### EU Register of Health Claims
**URL:** https://ec.europa.eu/food/safety/labelling-and-nutrition/nutrition-and-health-claims/eu-register-health-claims_en

Database of permitted and rejected health claims:
- **Authorized claims:** Approved wording for labels
- **Non-authorized claims:** Rejected with reasons
- **Under evaluation:** Pending assessment

**Search Examples:**
```
"vitamin D" → Approved claim: "contributes to normal immune function"
"coenzyme Q10" → Most claims not authorized
"glucosamine" → Joint health claims rejected
```

#### Scientific Opinions
Detailed risk assessments and evaluations:
- Novel food applications
- Supplement safety assessments
- Maximum intake levels
- Health claim evaluations

**URL:** https://www.efsa.europa.eu/en/publications

**Categories:**
```
Novel foods: New ingredients seeking EU approval
Health claims: Article 13/14 claim evaluations
Tolerable upper intakes: Safety thresholds
Contaminants: Heavy metals, toxins in supplements
```

#### Novel Food Assessments
Evaluation of new/non-traditional ingredients:
- Safety data requirements
- Approved novel foods list
- Pending applications
- Traditional use assessments

### EFSA vs. FDA Approach
| Aspect | EFSA | FDA |
|--------|------|-----|
| Claims | Pre-market approval | Post-market enforcement |
| Novel foods | Mandatory assessment | Generally Recognized as Safe (GRAS) |
| Evidence standard | Generally stricter | Variable |

### When to Use
- Researching EU regulatory status
- Understanding health claim evidence
- Novel ingredient safety data
- Setting upper intake limits
- Comparing EU vs. U.S. approaches

### Tips
- Health claims register searchable by nutrient
- Scientific opinions are highly detailed
- Novel food status affects EU marketability
- Rejected claims often cite evidence gaps

### Trust Rationale
Official EU regulatory body, rigorous scientific review process, no commercial bias. Very high evidence standards, though EU-focused.

## Labdoor

**URL:** https://labdoor.com
**Trust:** 3/5 | **Access:** Free (basic), some paid content
**Coverage:** 700+ products tested

### What It Is
Independent testing company that ranks supplements based on laboratory analysis. Consumer-focused with visual rankings.

### Testing Categories
- **Label Accuracy:** Actual vs. claimed amounts
- **Product Purity:** Contaminant testing
- **Nutritional Value:** Ingredient quality
- **Projected Efficacy:** Based on dosing
- **Value:** Cost per effective dose

### Ranking System
Products scored 0-100 across categories:
- A+ to F grades
- Overall rank within category
- Detailed breakdown available

### Categories Covered
```
Protein powders, Omega-3s, Multivitamins, Vitamin D,
Pre-workouts, BCAAs, Creatine, Probiotics,
Fish oil, Greens powders
```

### Example Search
```
labdoor.com/rankings/protein → Top protein powders
labdoor.com/rankings/fish-oil → Omega-3 rankings
Individual product pages → Detailed test results
```

### Limitations
- Funding model questions (some manufacturer partnerships)
- Not all categories/products covered
- Testing methodology details limited
- Update frequency unclear

### When to Use
- Quick product quality comparisons
- Finding top-rated products in category
- Basic contaminant screening info
- Consumer recommendations

### Tips
- Check test dates on reports
- Read methodology when available
- Use as one data point, not sole source
- Compare with ConsumerLab results

### Trust Rationale
Independent testing, but less transparency than ConsumerLab or NSF. Useful for quick rankings but verify with other sources for important decisions.

## Search Strategy by Question Type

### "Is this ingredient effective for X?"
1. **Examine.com** - Quick summary and Human Effect Matrix
2. **Natural Medicines** - Professional effectiveness grade
3. **NIH ODS Fact Sheets** - Authoritative summary
4. **EFSA Health Claims** - EU regulatory view
5. **Primary literature** via PubMed

### "What's the quality of product X?"
1. **ConsumerLab** - Independent testing results
2. **NSF/USP** - Certification verification
3. **DSLD** - Label information
4. **Labdoor** - Quick ranking check

### "What dose should be used?"
1. **Natural Medicines** - Clinical dosing
2. **Examine.com** - Research-based dosing
3. **NIH ODS** - RDA/UL values
4. **Primary literature** for specific applications

### "What are the interactions/safety concerns?"
1. **Natural Medicines** - Interaction checker
2. **NIH ODS** - Safety information
3. **EFSA** - Upper intake levels
4. **DrugBank** - Drug interaction data

### "What products contain ingredient X?"
1. **DSLD** - U.S. product labels
2. **NSF/USP** - Certified products only
3. **ConsumerLab** - Tested products
