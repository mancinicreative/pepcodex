### DOSSIER: Mazdutide

#### 1) Snapshot
- **What it is:** Long-acting GLP-1 and glucagon receptor dual agonist peptide
- **What it targets:** GLP-1R (metabolic/satiety) and GCGR (hepatic lipid metabolism)
- **What it's for:** Obesity, type 2 diabetes, MASH (metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis)
- **Where it is:** Phase 3 clinical trials (GLORY-1/2, DREAMS-3)
- **Most important result:** 14.0% weight loss at 48 weeks; 49.5% achieved ≥15% weight reduction

#### 2) Identity & Nomenclature
- **Names/Synonyms:** Mazdutide, IBI362
- **Sponsor:** Innovent Biologics (China), partnered with Eli Lilly
- **INN:** Mazdutide (proposed)
- **Modality:** Acylated peptide, once-weekly subcutaneous injection
- **Sequence/Modifications:** UNKNOWN - proprietary; includes fatty acid acylation for albumin binding

#### 3) Mechanism of Action
- **Primary targets:**
  - GLP-1R: Enhances glucose-dependent insulin secretion, reduces appetite, slows gastric emptying
  - GCGR: Stimulates hepatic fatty acid oxidation, inhibits lipogenesis, increases energy expenditure
- **Downstream effects:** Dual receptor engagement provides additive weight loss + direct liver fat reduction
- **Competitive comparison:** Similar to survodutide (Boehringer) but different receptor balance; more glucagon activity than tirzepatide (GLP-1/GIP)

#### 4) Evidence Map
- **Preclinical:** UNKNOWN - limited public preclinical data
- **Clinical Trials:**
  - **GLORY-1** (NCT05506774): Phase 3, obesity, 48wk, N=610; -11.0% (4mg), -14.0% (6mg) vs placebo
  - **GLORY-2** (NCT05609448): Phase 3, T2D + obesity
  - **DREAMS-3** (NCT05265689): Phase 3, head-to-head vs semaglutide in T2D + obesity; expected completion early 2026
- **Regulatory:** NMPA (China) submission expected 2026; FDA path TBD

#### 5) Safety & Tolerability
- **Most common AEs:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea (GI class effects)
- **Serious AEs:** Not yet fully characterized in Phase 3
- **Confidence level:** MEDIUM - Phase 3 safety data emerging

#### 6) PK/PD & Delivery
- **Route:** Subcutaneous injection
- **Half-life strategy:** Fatty acid acylation (albumin binding)
- **Dosing:** Once weekly; 4mg and 6mg doses in Phase 3

#### 7) Manufacturing / CMC Notes
- Peptide synthesis with lipidation; standard for GLP-1 class
- Partner with Lilly provides manufacturing expertise

#### 8) Competitive Landscape
- **Closest alternatives:** Survodutide (Boehringer), retatrutide (Lilly triple agonist)
- **Advantages:** First head-to-head data vs semaglutide; potential MASH benefits from glucagon component
- **Risks:** Crowded obesity market; differentiation vs other dual/triple agonists

#### 9) Open Questions / Unknowns
- Full Phase 3 safety profile
- DREAMS-3 head-to-head results vs semaglutide (pivotal)
- MASH/liver histology outcomes
- US/EU regulatory path and timeline
- Pricing and market positioning outside China

#### 10) References
- [NEJM - GLORY-1 Results](https://www.nejm.org/doi/abs/10.1056/NEJMoa2411528)

### DOSSIER: CagriSema

#### 1) Snapshot
- **What it is:** Fixed-dose combination of cagrilintide (amylin analog) + semaglutide (GLP-1 agonist)
- **What it targets:** Amylin receptors (CTR/RAMP) + GLP-1R
- **What it's for:** Chronic weight management in obesity
- **Where it is:** NDA filed to FDA December 2025
- **Most important result:** 22.7% weight loss at 68 weeks (REDEFINE-1)

#### 2) Identity & Nomenclature
- **Names/Synonyms:** CagriSema, cagrilintide/semaglutide
- **Sponsor:** Novo Nordisk
- **INN:** Cagrilintide + Semaglutide (combination)
- **Modality:** Peptide combination, once-weekly subcutaneous injection
- **Sequence/Modifications:** Cagrilintide is acylated amylin analog; semaglutide is acylated GLP-1

#### 3) Mechanism of Action
- **Primary targets:**
  - Amylin receptors: Induces satiety, slows gastric emptying, suppresses glucagon
  - GLP-1R: Glucose-dependent insulin secretion, appetite suppression
- **Downstream effects:** Complementary satiety pathways; potentially additive weight loss
- **Competitive comparison:** First amylin + GLP-1 combination; differentiated mechanism from dual/triple incretins

#### 4) Evidence Map
- **Clinical Trials:**
  - **REDEFINE-1** (NCT05567796): Phase 3, obesity, 68wk, N=3417; -22.7% (CagriSema) vs -16.1% (semaglutide) vs -11.8% (cagrilintide) vs -2.3% (placebo)
  - **REDEFINE-2** (NCT05601350): Phase 3, T2D + obesity
  - **REDEFINE-3**: Event-driven CV outcomes trial ongoing
- **Regulatory:** NDA filed to FDA December 2025

#### 5) Safety & Tolerability
- **Most common AEs:** GI events (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation) - mostly mild-moderate, improved over time
- **Serious AEs:** Comparable to components; no unexpected signals in Phase 3
- **Confidence level:** HIGH - extensive Phase 3 data; >5 years exposure data

#### 6) PK/PD & Delivery
- **Route:** Subcutaneous injection (pre-filled pen)
- **Half-life strategy:** Both peptides use fatty acid acylation
- **Dosing:** Once weekly; 2.4mg cagrilintide / 2.4mg semaglutide

#### 7) Manufacturing / CMC Notes
- Novo Nordisk established manufacturing; fixed-ratio combination pen device

#### 8) Competitive Landscape
- **Closest alternatives:** Tirzepatide (Lilly), retatrutide (Lilly triple)
- **Advantages:** Novel amylin mechanism; potentially different patient profile responders
- **Risks:** GI tolerability concerns; pricing in crowded market

#### 9) Open Questions / Unknowns
- FDA approval timeline (expected 2026)
- CV outcomes data from REDEFINE-3
- Head-to-head vs tirzepatide
- Insurance coverage/access

### DOSSIER: Pemvidutide

#### 1) Snapshot
- **What it is:** GLP-1 and glucagon receptor dual agonist peptide
- **What it targets:** GLP-1R + GCGR (with emphasis on liver effects)
- **What it's for:** Obesity, MASH, alcohol use disorder (AUD), alcohol-associated liver disease (ALD)
- **Where it is:** Phase 2b completed (MOMENTUM, IMPACT); Phase 3 planning
- **Most important result:** 59.1% MASH resolution without fibrosis worsening at 24 weeks

#### 2) Identity & Nomenclature
- **Names/Synonyms:** Pemvidutide, ALT-801
- **Sponsor:** Altimmune
- **INN:** Pemvidutide (proposed)
- **Modality:** Peptide, once-weekly subcutaneous injection
- **Sequence/Modifications:** UNKNOWN - proprietary dual agonist

#### 3) Mechanism of Action
- **Primary targets:**
  - GLP-1R: Appetite suppression, metabolic regulation
  - GCGR: Direct hepatic action - stimulates fatty acid oxidation, inhibits lipogenesis
- **Downstream effects:** Weight loss + direct liver fat reduction; potentially superior liver effects vs GLP-1 monotherapy
- **Key differentiator:** Glucagon agonism provides liver-directed mechanism beyond weight loss alone

#### 4) Evidence Map
- **Clinical Trials:**
  - **MOMENTUM** (NCT04895462): Phase 2, obesity, 48wk, N=391; -15.6% weight loss; 78% fat mass, 22% lean mass
  - **IMPACT** (NCT05006885): Phase 2b, MASH F2/F3, 24wk, N=212; 59.1% MASH resolution (1.8mg), 34.5% fibrosis improvement
  - **RECLAIM**: Phase 2, AUD, initiated May 2025
  - **RESTORE**: Phase 2, ALD, initiated July 2025
- **Regulatory:** FDA Fast Track for MASH; End of Phase 2 meeting Q4 2025

#### 5) Safety & Tolerability
- **Most common AEs:** Nausea, diarrhea, vomiting (GI class effects)
- **Notable finding:** Class-leading lean mass preservation (only 21.9% of weight loss from lean mass)
- **Confidence level:** MEDIUM-HIGH - robust Phase 2 data

#### 6) PK/PD & Delivery
- **Route:** Subcutaneous injection
- **Dosing:** Once weekly; 1.2mg, 1.8mg, 2.4mg studied

#### 7) Manufacturing / CMC Notes
- Standard peptide synthesis; Altimmune partnership for manufacturing

#### 8) Competitive Landscape
- **Closest alternatives:** Survodutide (Boehringer), mazdutide (Innovent)
- **Advantages:** Strong MASH data; lean mass preservation; AUD/ALD expansion
- **Risks:** Smaller company resources vs Big Pharma competitors

#### 9) Open Questions / Unknowns
- 48-week IMPACT histology data (expected Q4 2025)
- Phase 3 trial design and timeline
- Competitive positioning vs survodutide in MASH
- Long-term safety

- **What it is:** Bicycle Drug Conjugate (BDC) targeting Nectin-4
- **What it targets:** Nectin-4 (cell adhesion molecule overexpressed in tumors)
- **What it's for:** Metastatic urothelial cancer, NSCLC, breast cancer
- **Where it is:** Phase 2/3 registrational trial (Duravelo-2)

#### 2) Identity & Nomenclature
- **Names/Synonyms:** Zelenectide pevedotin, BT8009
- **Sponsor:** Bicycle Therapeutics
- **INN:** Zelenectide pevedotin (proposed)
- **Modality:** Bicycle Drug Conjugate - constrained bicyclic peptide + cytotoxic payload (MMAE)
- **Structure:** Two-loop bicyclic peptide scaffold with small molecule constraint

#### 3) Mechanism of Action
- **Primary target:** Nectin-4 (overexpressed in urothelial, breast, lung cancers)
- **Mechanism:** Bicycle peptide binds Nectin-4 with high affinity → internalization → MMAE payload release → tubulin inhibition → cell death
- **Competitive comparison:** Alternative to Padcev (ADC); smaller molecule with better tissue penetration

#### 4) Evidence Map
- **Clinical Trials:**

  - **NSCLC expansion**: Initiated Q3 2025
- **Key results:** 45% ORR, 11.1mo median DoR in mUC monotherapy

#### 5) Safety & Tolerability
- **Most common AEs:** Generally well-tolerated; specific AE profile UNKNOWN from search
- **Confidence level:** MEDIUM - Phase 1/2 safety emerging

#### 6) PK/PD & Delivery
- **Route:** Intravenous infusion
- **Half-life:** Bicycle molecules have rapid clearance (feature for reduced off-target exposure)
- **Dosing:** UNKNOWN - multiple dose levels in expansion

#### 7) Manufacturing / CMC Notes

#### 8) Competitive Landscape

### DOSSIER: Oveporexton (TAK-861)

#### 1) Snapshot
- **What it is:** Oral orexin receptor 2 (OX2R) selective agonist
- **What it targets:** OX2R (restores orexin signaling)
- **What it's for:** Narcolepsy type 1 (excessive daytime sleepiness, cataplexy)
- **Where it is:** Phase 3 completed; regulatory submission expected
- **Most important result:** Significant improvements in EDS, cataplexy, disease severity vs placebo

#### 2) Identity & Nomenclature
- **Names/Synonyms:** Oveporexton, TAK-861
- **Sponsor:** Takeda
- **INN:** Oveporexton (proposed)
- **Modality:** Small molecule (non-peptide) - but targets neuropeptide system
- **Note:** While not a peptide itself, it restores orexin peptide signaling

#### 3) Mechanism of Action
- **Primary target:** Orexin receptor 2 (OX2R) agonism
- **Downstream effects:** Restores orexin signaling lost due to orexin neuron destruction in NT1
- **Key insight:** NT1 is caused by autoimmune destruction of orexin-producing neurons; OX2R agonism bypasses this

#### 4) Evidence Map
- **Clinical Trials:**
  - **Phase 2b** (NCT04091438): Published in NEJM; significant improvements across all doses
  - **Phase 3 Program**: World Sleep 2025 presentation; program completed
- **Regulatory:** Breakthrough Therapy designation (FDA); Priority review (China NMPA)

#### 5) Safety & Tolerability
- **Most common AEs:** UNKNOWN from search - generally well-tolerated per reports
- **Confidence level:** HIGH - Phase 3 completed

#### 6) PK/PD & Delivery
- **Route:** Oral
- **Dosing:** Once or twice daily (specific regimen UNKNOWN)

#### 7) Manufacturing / CMC Notes
- Small molecule; standard chemical synthesis

#### 8) Competitive Landscape
- **Closest alternatives:** Alixorexton (Alkermes), traditional stimulants, sodium oxybate
- **Advantages:** First-in-class mechanism addressing root cause; oral delivery
- **Risks:** Novel mechanism; long-term safety unknown

#### 9) Open Questions / Unknowns
- BLA filing timeline
- Long-term efficacy maintenance
- Effects on other orexin-related functions (appetite, reward)
- Pricing and access
