## Overview

<h2 id="overview" class="sr-only">Overview</h2>

### Quick Summary

- **Dual GLP-1/glucagon receptor agonist** with balanced activity at both receptors
- **Phase 2b completed** for obesity (MOMENTUM trial) and MASH (IMPACT trial)
- **15.6% weight loss at 48 weeks** in the MOMENTUM trial (2.4mg dose)
- **59.1% MASH resolution** without fibrosis worsening in IMPACT trial
- **Unique lean mass preservation** - 78% of weight lost was fat mass (vs typical 65-70%)
- **FDA Fast Track designation** granted for MASH indication (November 2022)
- **Developed by Altimmune Inc.** - Phase 3 trials planned for 2025-2026
- **Once-weekly subcutaneous injection** - convenient dosing schedule

**Evidence Strength: High** - Consistent Phase 2b results across two well-designed trials (MOMENTUM and IMPACT) with robust efficacy and safety data. Differentiated by superior lean mass preservation. Phase 3 program in planning.

<h2 id="mechanism-of-action">Mechanism of Action</h2>

### How Pemvidutide Works (Plain English)

Pemvidutide activates TWO natural hormone receptors simultaneously, creating a powerful combination for weight loss and liver health:

1. **GLP-1 receptor activation** - Like Ozempic and Wegovy, pemvidutide tells your brain "you're full" and slows stomach emptying. This reduces appetite and calorie intake naturally.

2. **Glucagon receptor activation** - This is pemvidutide's special addition. Glucagon tells your liver to burn fat for energy and increases your metabolic rate. It's like turning up your internal furnace.

**Why the dual approach matters:**

- **The liver connection:** The liver is packed with glucagon receptors but has almost no GLP-1 receptors. This is why pemvidutide may be especially effective for fatty liver disease (MASH) - it directly tells liver cells to burn fat.

- **Muscle preservation:** The unique balance of GLP-1 and glucagon in pemvidutide appears to preserve lean muscle mass better than GLP-1-only drugs. In trials, 78% of weight lost was fat, compared to the typical 65-70% with other medications.

- **Metabolic boost:** Glucagon increases energy expenditure (calories burned at rest). Combined with reduced appetite from GLP-1, this creates weight loss from both sides - eating less AND burning more.

**The safety balance:** Adding glucagon might raise blood sugar, but the GLP-1 component counteracts this by boosting insulin release. The net effect is stable or improved blood sugar alongside significant weight loss.

### The Science: Balanced Dual Agonism

Pemvidutide is a **pegylated 30-amino acid peptide** with balanced activity at both GLP-1 and glucagon receptors.

### Receptor Potency Profile

| Receptor | Activity | Clinical Relevance |
|----------|----------|-------------------|
| **GLP-1R** | Full agonist | Appetite suppression, insulin secretion |
| **GCGR** | Full agonist | Hepatic fat oxidation, thermogenesis |
| **Ratio** | Balanced (~1:1) | Differentiates from GLP-1-biased compounds |

### Why Glucagon Matters for MASH

**Critical insight:** The liver has abundant glucagon receptors but minimal GLP-1 receptors.

```
Glucagon Receptor Activation in Liver
    |
    ├── ↑ Hepatic lipid oxidation (fat burning)
    ├── ↓ De novo lipogenesis (fat creation)
    ├── ↓ Hepatic steatosis (fatty liver)
    ├── ↓ Oxidative stress
    └── Anti-inflammatory and anti-fibrotic effects
```

This direct liver action explains pemvidutide's impressive 82% reduction in liver fat content observed in trials.

### Tissue-Specific Signaling

```
Pemvidutide administration
    |
├── GLP-1R PATHWAY
│   ├── Brain (hypothalamus): Satiety ↑, hunger ↓
│   ├── Pancreas (β-cells): Insulin secretion ↑
│   ├── Pancreas (α-cells): Glucagon suppression
│   └── GI tract: Gastric emptying delayed
│
└── GCGR PATHWAY
    ├── Liver: Fat oxidation ↑, lipogenesis ↓
    ├── White adipose: Lipolysis ↑
    ├── Brown adipose: Thermogenesis ↑
    └── Energy expenditure: Estimated 10-15% increase
```

### Why Lean Mass Preservation?

Pemvidutide showed **78% fat mass loss** (22% lean mass) compared to typical 65-70% fat mass with GLP-1-only drugs.

**Proposed mechanisms:**
1. **Glucagon-driven lipolysis:** Preferentially targets fat stores
2. **Increased energy expenditure:** May reduce metabolic adaptation
3. **Balanced receptor activity:** Optimized ratio for body composition
4. **Protein-sparing effects:** Glucagon may have anabolic signaling in muscle

**Why this matters:** Preserving lean mass during weight loss maintains metabolic rate, reduces weight regain risk, and improves functional outcomes - especially important in older adults and those with sarcopenic obesity.

<h2 id="key-human-trials">Key Human Trials</h2>

### MOMENTUM Trial (Phase 2b - Obesity)

**ClinicalTrials.gov:** NCT05295875
**Study design:** 48-week, double-blind, placebo-controlled, dose-ranging RCT
**Population:** Adults with obesity (BMI ≥30 or ≥27 with comorbidity) without diabetes
**N = 320** randomized to pemvidutide 1.2mg, 1.8mg, 2.4mg or placebo

| Dose | Weight Loss (24 wk) | Weight Loss (48 wk) | Fat Mass % |
|------|---------------------|---------------------|------------|
| 1.2 mg | -7.2% | -11.2% | 77% |
| 1.8 mg | -10.3% | -14.3% | 78% |
| **2.4 mg** | **-10.7%** | **-15.6%** | **78%** |
| Placebo | -2.2% | -2.3% | — |

**Key findings:**
- **15.6% weight loss at 48 weeks** at highest dose (2.4mg)
- **78% of weight lost was fat mass** - superior to GLP-1 class average
- Weight loss trajectory not yet plateaued at 48 weeks (continued benefit expected)
- Consistent efficacy across dose levels
- Well-tolerated with manageable GI adverse events

**Body composition substudy:**
- Assessed by DEXA at baseline and 48 weeks
- Lean mass preservation significantly better than historical GLP-1 comparators
- May reduce metabolic adaptation and weight regain

### IMPACT Trial (Phase 2b - MASH)

**ClinicalTrials.gov:** NCT05454839
**Study design:** 48-week, double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT with liver biopsy endpoints
**Population:** Adults with biopsy-confirmed MASH (NAS ≥4) and fibrosis stage F1-F3
**N = 188** randomized to pemvidutide 1.8mg, 2.4mg or placebo

| Endpoint | Pemvidutide 2.4mg | Placebo | P-value |
|----------|-------------------|---------|---------|
| **MASH resolution without fibrosis worsening** | **59.1%** | 16.7% | &lt;0.001 |
| **Fibrosis improvement (≥1 stage)** | **43.6%** | 12.5% | &lt;0.001 |
| **Both endpoints achieved** | 34.5% | 6.3% | &lt;0.001 |
| Liver fat reduction (MRI-PDFF) | -78.3% | -12.1% | &lt;0.001 |

**Key findings:**
- **59.1% achieved MASH resolution** without fibrosis worsening (primary endpoint)
- **43.6% achieved fibrosis improvement** of at least one stage
- **82% reduction in liver fat content** by MRI-PDFF
- Significant improvements in liver enzymes (ALT, AST, GGT)
- Results compare favorably to resmetirom (first approved MASH therapy)

**Histological outcomes (NAS components):**
- Steatosis: Improved in 78% of pemvidutide group
- Inflammation: Improved in 65% of pemvidutide group
- Ballooning: Improved in 72% of pemvidutide group

### Phase 1 Studies

**Study:** Single and multiple ascending dose
**N = 48** healthy volunteers
**Key findings:**
- Half-life approximately 65-70 hours (supports weekly dosing)
- Linear pharmacokinetics across dose range
- No serious adverse events
- Dose-dependent effects on glucose and weight

<h2 id="safety-profile">Safety Profile</h2>

### Common Adverse Events (Pooled Phase 2 Data)

| Event | Incidence | Severity | Time Course |
|-------|-----------|----------|-------------|
| **Nausea** | 25-35% | Mild-moderate | Improves over 4-8 weeks |
| **Diarrhea** | 15-20% | Usually mild | Variable |
| **Vomiting** | 10-15% | Mild-moderate | More common during titration |
| **Constipation** | 8-12% | Mild | May persist |
| **Decreased appetite** | 15-20% | Expected | Part of mechanism |
| **Injection site reactions** | 5-8% | Mild | Localized, transient |

**GI tolerability note:** Adverse events are dose-dependent and improve with continued therapy. Slow dose titration (as used in trials) significantly reduces discontinuation rates.

### Discontinuation Rates

| Trial | Pemvidutide | Placebo | Due to AEs |
|-------|-------------|---------|------------|
| MOMENTUM | 12.5% | 8.3% | 4.2% |
| IMPACT | 10.8% | 6.5% | 3.9% |

Discontinuation rates are comparable to or lower than other GLP-1 class medications.

### Serious Adverse Events

| Risk | Incidence | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Pancreatitis** | &lt;1% | Standard GLP-1 class monitoring |
| **Gallbladder events** | 2-3% | Associated with rapid weight loss |
| **Hypoglycemia** | Rare (&lt;1%) | Low risk; no severe events reported |
| **Thyroid** | Monitoring ongoing | GLP-1 class theoretical concern |

### Cardiovascular Parameters

| Parameter | Effect | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| **Heart rate** | +2-4 bpm | Similar to GLP-1 class |
| **Blood pressure** | -3 to -5 mmHg (systolic) | Consistent with weight loss |
| **Lipids** | Improved profile | LDL ↓, TG ↓, HDL ↑ |

### Contraindications (Anticipated)

Based on class effects and trial protocols:
- Personal/family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma
- Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN2)
- Known hypersensitivity to pemvidutide
- Pregnancy (contraindicated)

| **Metabolism** | Proteolytic degradation | Not CYP-dependent |

### Dosing in Clinical Trials

**MOMENTUM titration schedule:**
- Week 1-2: 0.6 mg once weekly
- Week 3-4: 1.2 mg once weekly (maintenance for some)
- Week 5-6: 1.8 mg once weekly (maintenance for some)
- Week 7+: 2.4 mg once weekly (maximum dose)

Slow titration minimizes gastrointestinal adverse events.
