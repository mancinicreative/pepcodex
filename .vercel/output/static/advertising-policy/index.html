Advertising Policy

How we handle sponsorships and partnerships while maintaining editorial independence.

Our Approach to Advertising

Peptide Library accepts limited sponsorships and partnerships to support our research and publishing operations. We maintain strict separation between editorial content and commercial relationships.

What We Accept

  • Founding Partner sponsorships — Companies that support our mission with no editorial influence
  • Newsletter sponsorships — Clearly labeled sponsored sections in our newsletter
  • Educational partnerships — Collaborations with research institutions and medical education providers

What We Don't Accept

  • Advertising for peptide vendors or sources
  • Sponsored content disguised as editorial
  • Advertising that promotes specific dosing or protocols
  • Pay-for-play coverage or reviews
  • Advertising that contradicts our editorial standards

Disclosure Standards

All sponsored content and partnerships are clearly disclosed:

  • Sponsor logos appear only in designated sponsor sections
  • Sponsored newsletter content is labeled "Sponsored"
  • Partner pages are clearly identified as partner profiles
  • We list all current sponsors on this page

Editorial Firewall

Sponsors and partners have zero influence over our editorial content:

  • They don't review content before publication
  • They don't influence evidence grades or conclusions
  • They don't determine which peptides we cover
  • They can't request removal of unfavorable information

Current Sponsors

We are currently accepting founding partners. If you're interested in supporting evidence-based peptide education, learn more about partnership opportunities.

Questions?

If you have questions about our advertising policies or want to report a concern about a sponsorship, please contact us.
