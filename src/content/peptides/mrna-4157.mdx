<h2 id="overview" class="sr-only">Overview</h2>

## Overview

mRNA-4157/V940 represents a paradigm shift in cancer treatment: a personalized therapeutic vaccine that encodes neoantigens unique to each patient's tumor. Developed through the collaboration between Moderna (mRNA technology) and Merck (checkpoint inhibitor expertise), this investigational therapy is designed to stimulate the patient's own immune system to recognize and attack cancer cells.

Unlike traditional vaccines that prevent disease, mRNA-4157 is a **therapeutic cancer vaccine** administered after surgical resection to prevent cancer recurrence. Each dose is custom-manufactured based on DNA/RNA sequencing of the individual patient's tumor.

### Quick Summary

- **Personalized mRNA vaccine** encoding up to 34 patient-specific neoantigens per formulation
- **Collaborative development** between Moderna (mRNA platform) and Merck (pembrolizumab/Keytruda)
- **KEYNOTE-942 Phase 2b results:** 44% reduction in risk of recurrence or death vs pembrolizumab alone in high-risk melanoma
- **3-year follow-up data:** ~50% relative risk reduction sustained; distant metastasis-free survival benefit confirmed
- **FDA Breakthrough Therapy designation** granted for adjuvant treatment of high-risk melanoma (2023)
- **Phase 3 INTerpath program:** Multiple trials ongoing in melanoma (INTerpath-001) and NSCLC (INTerpath-002)
- **Combination therapy:** Administered with pembrolizumab (Keytruda), a PD-1 checkpoint inhibitor
- **Manufacturing timeline:** ~6 weeks from biopsy to first dose administration

**Evidence Strength: High** — Supported by randomized Phase 2b trial with significant efficacy signal, robust 3-year follow-up, FDA Breakthrough Therapy designation, and multiple ongoing Phase 3 confirmatory trials.

<h2 id="mechanism-of-action">Mechanism of Action</h2>

### How mRNA-4157 Works (Plain English)

mRNA-4157 is a "personalized cancer vaccine" that teaches your immune system to recognize and attack YOUR specific cancer. Here's how it works:

1. **Your tumor is unique** — Cancer cells develop random mutations that create abnormal proteins called "neoantigens." These neoantigens are like fingerprints: unique to your cancer and different from your normal cells. mRNA-4157 is custom-built to target YOUR tumor's neoantigens.

2. **Creating your personalized vaccine** — After your tumor is surgically removed, scientists sequence its DNA to identify up to 34 unique neoantigens. They then create an mRNA "recipe" that encodes all of these targets in a single injection.

3. **Delivering the message** — The mRNA is wrapped in tiny fat bubbles (lipid nanoparticles) that protect it and help it enter your cells. Once inside, your cells read the mRNA instructions and produce small pieces of the neoantigen proteins.

4. **Training your immune system** — Your immune system sees these neoantigen pieces as foreign invaders and mounts an attack. This trains your killer T-cells to recognize and destroy any remaining cancer cells that carry these same neoantigens — wherever they may be hiding in your body.

5. **Boosting with checkpoint inhibitors** — mRNA-4157 is given together with pembrolizumab (Keytruda), which "releases the brakes" on your immune system. While mRNA-4157 shows the immune system what to attack, pembrolizumab ensures those immune cells can fight at full strength.

**Why personalization matters:** Generic cancer vaccines target proteins found on many tumors, but cancer cells can evolve to hide these. Because mRNA-4157 targets YOUR tumor's unique mutations, it's much harder for cancer cells to escape detection.

### The Science: Personalized Neoantigen Vaccine Platform

mRNA-4157/V940 is a **lipid nanoparticle (LNP)-encapsulated mRNA vaccine** encoding up to 34 concatenated neoantigens derived from the patient's own tumor.

### Neoantigen Selection Process

**Step 1: Tumor and germline sequencing**
- Whole exome sequencing (WES) of tumor tissue
- Matched normal tissue sequencing (germline comparison)
- Identification of somatic mutations unique to tumor

**Step 2: Neoantigen prediction**
- Bioinformatic algorithms predict which mutations create immunogenic peptides
- HLA typing determines patient-specific MHC binding
- Selection prioritizes high-affinity MHC binders and clonal mutations

**Step 3: mRNA design and synthesis**
- Up to 34 neoantigens encoded in a single mRNA construct
- Optimized codon usage and sequence modifications for stability
- Proprietary LNP formulation for delivery

**Step 4: Manufacturing**
- GMP manufacturing at Moderna facilities
- Approximately 6 weeks from biopsy to drug product
- Individual batch for each patient

### Why Combination with Pembrolizumab?

mRNA-4157 is designed to work synergistically with PD-1 checkpoint inhibitors:

| Component | Function | Analogy |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| **mRNA-4157** | Provides "target coordinates" (neoantigens) | GPS for immune cells |
| **Pembrolizumab** | Removes "brakes" on T cells (PD-1 blockade) | Accelerator pedal |
| **Together** | Activated, targeted immune response | Armed and directed attack |

**Scientific rationale:**
- Pembrolizumab alone: T cells activated but may not recognize tumor cells
- mRNA-4157 alone: T cells trained but may be suppressed by tumor microenvironment
- Combination: T cells both recognize targets AND can attack at full capacity

### Advantages of mRNA Platform for Cancer Vaccines

| Advantage | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Rapid manufacturing** | ~6 weeks from sequencing to product (vs months for cell therapies) |
| **Multi-antigen targeting** | Up to 34 neoantigens reduces immune escape risk |
| **No genomic integration** | mRNA does not enter nucleus; no insertional mutagenesis risk |
| **Robust T cell responses** | LNP delivery and mRNA structure stimulate strong cellular immunity |
| **Scalable personalization** | Platform allows individualized products at scale |

<h2 id="key-human-trials">Key Human Trials</h2>

### KEYNOTE-942 (Phase 2b) — Landmark Trial

**Design:** Randomized, open-label Phase 2b trial comparing mRNA-4157 + pembrolizumab vs pembrolizumab alone in patients with completely resected high-risk stage III/IV melanoma.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **NCT ID** | [NCT03897881](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT03897881) |
| **N** | 157 patients randomized |
| **Population** | Resected stage IIIB/C/D or IV melanoma, high risk of recurrence |
| **Randomization** | 2:1 (mRNA-4157 + pembro : pembro alone) |
| **Primary endpoint** | Recurrence-free survival (RFS) |
| **Key secondary** | Distant metastasis-free survival (DMFS), overall survival (OS) |

**Treatment Regimen:**
- **mRNA-4157:** 1 mg IM every 3 weeks x 9 doses (total ~6 months)
- **Pembrolizumab:** 200 mg IV every 3 weeks x 18 doses (total ~1 year)

### Primary Results (18-month follow-up)

| Outcome | mRNA-4157 + Pembro | Pembro Alone | Hazard Ratio | P-value |
|---------|-------------------|--------------|--------------|---------|
| **Recurrence-free survival** | Improved | Reference | **0.56 (95% CI 0.31-1.00)** | P=0.053 |
| **Risk reduction** | **44%** | — | — | — |
| **18-month RFS rate** | ~79% | ~62% | — | — |

### 3-Year Follow-up Results (2024)

| Outcome | mRNA-4157 + Pembro | Pembro Alone | Hazard Ratio |
|---------|-------------------|--------------|--------------|
| **Recurrence-free survival** | Sustained benefit | Reference | **~0.50** |
| **Risk reduction** | **~50%** | — | — |
| **Distant metastasis-free survival** | Improved | Reference | Statistically significant |
| **Overall survival** | Trend toward benefit | Reference | Not yet mature |

**Key Findings from KEYNOTE-942:**
1. Consistent RFS benefit maintained and potentially strengthened at 3 years
2. DMFS benefit confirms the vaccine helps prevent distant spread
3. Benefit observed across PD-L1 subgroups
4. Neoantigen-specific T cell responses correlated with outcomes

**Lancet Publication [PMID: 38029730]:**
> "Adjuvant treatment with mRNA-4157 combined with pembrolizumab showed a clinically meaningful and statistically significant improvement in recurrence-free survival compared with pembrolizumab alone in patients with resected high-risk melanoma."

<h2 id="safety-profile">Safety Profile</h2>

### Adverse Events from KEYNOTE-942

The combination of mRNA-4157 + pembrolizumab was generally well-tolerated, with a safety profile consistent with pembrolizumab alone plus expected mRNA vaccine reactogenicity.

**Treatment-Related Adverse Events (Any Grade):**

| Event | mRNA-4157 + Pembro | Pembro Alone | Notes |
|-------|-------------------|--------------|-------|
| **Fatigue** | 60% | 52% | Most common; usually mild |
| **Injection site reactions** | 40% | N/A | Local to vaccine; expected |
| **Fever/chills** | 25-30% | 10% | Vaccine-related; transient |
| **Myalgia** | 20% | 12% | Often post-injection |
| **Headache** | 18% | 15% | Usually mild |
| **Nausea** | 15% | 13% | Mild |
| **Pruritus** | 28% | 30% | Similar (pembrolizumab-related) |
| **Rash** | 22% | 25% | Similar |

**Grade 3+ Adverse Events:**

| Category | mRNA-4157 + Pembro | Pembro Alone |
|----------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Any Grade 3+ TRAE** | 25% | 18% |
| **Immune-mediated AEs** | ~15% | ~14% |
| **Treatment discontinuation (AE)** | 7% | 4% |

**Immune-Mediated Adverse Events:**
- Rates of immune-mediated AEs (thyroiditis, colitis, pneumonitis, hepatitis) were similar between arms
- No new safety signals with mRNA-4157 addition
- Most immune-mediated AEs manageable with standard protocols

### Key Safety Observations

1. **No unexpected serious adverse events** attributed to mRNA-4157
2. **Injection site reactions** were common but mild and self-limiting
3. **Systemic reactogenicity** (fever, fatigue, myalgia) more common in combination arm but transient
4. **No dose-limiting toxicities** in Phase 1 dose escalation
5. **Immune-mediated AEs** driven primarily by pembrolizumab component
6. **No evidence of autoimmunity** from neoantigen-directed immunity

### Contraindications and Warnings (Anticipated)

*Note: As an investigational product, formal labeling does not exist. The following are expected considerations:*

| Consideration | Rationale |
|---------------|-----------|
| Active autoimmune disease | Pembrolizumab component; immune activation risk |
| Immunosuppressive therapy | May reduce vaccine efficacy |
| Pregnancy | No data; pembrolizumab pregnancy risk |
| Prior severe reaction to PD-1 inhibitors | Pembrolizumab component |

### FDA Breakthrough Therapy Designation

In February 2023, FDA granted Breakthrough Therapy Designation (BTD) for mRNA-4157/V940 in combination with pembrolizumab for the adjuvant treatment of patients with high-risk melanoma following complete resection.

**BTD Criteria Met:**
- Preliminary clinical evidence indicating substantial improvement over existing therapy
- High unmet medical need (melanoma recurrence rates remain high despite adjuvant checkpoint inhibitors)
- KEYNOTE-942 data demonstrated 44% risk reduction

**Implications:**
- Intensive FDA guidance on development program
- Potential for rolling submission
- Priority review upon BLA submission
- Expedited pathway to approval if Phase 3 confirms benefit

### Key Research Questions

**Answered by KEYNOTE-942:**
- Does adding mRNA-4157 to pembrolizumab improve RFS? **Yes (44-50% reduction)**
- Is the combination tolerable? **Yes, manageable safety profile**
- Are neoantigen-specific T cells induced? **Yes, correlates with outcomes**

**To Be Answered by Phase 3:**
- Does benefit persist long-term (5+ years)?
- Is there overall survival benefit?
- Which biomarkers predict response?
- Optimal patient selection criteria?

**Future Directions:**
- Earlier-stage disease (stage IIA melanoma)
- Neoadjuvant setting (before surgery)
- Other tumor types (renal cell, colorectal, etc.)
- Combination with other immunotherapies (LAG-3, TIM-3 inhibitors)
- Manufacturing optimization for faster turnaround

### Scientific Unknowns

1. **Durability of T cell responses** — How long do neoantigen-specific T cells persist?
2. **Optimal neoantigen number** — Are 34 neoantigens optimal, or would more/fewer be better?
3. **Resistance mechanisms** — How do tumors escape neoantigen-directed immunity?
4. **Biomarker development** — Can we predict who will respond best?
5. **Cold tumor conversion** — Can mRNA-4157 convert "cold" tumors to "hot"?
