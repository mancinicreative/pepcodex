Also known as: Anti-Obesity Drug 9604, Tyr-hGH Fragment 177-191

Overview

Overview

Quick Summary

  • Modified fragment of human growth hormone (amino acids 177-191)
  • Not FDA approved - clinical development discontinued
  • Failed Phase 2b/3 trials for obesity did not meet primary endpoints
  • No diabetogenic effects of full HGH (theoretical advantage)
  • Marketed illegally despite lack of efficacy evidence

Evidence Strength: Low - Clinical trials failed to demonstrate efficacy for weight loss. Development discontinued.

Mechanism of Action

How AOD-9604 Was Supposed to Work

AOD-9604 was designed to capture the fat-burning effects of growth hormone while avoiding its downsides (like raising blood sugar). Here’s the concept:

  1. A piece of growth hormone - Researchers identified that amino acids 177-191 of growth hormone seemed to be responsible for its fat-burning effects, separate from its growth-promoting effects.

  2. Fat cells have a “burn” switch - Fat cells have receptors called beta-3 adrenergic receptors. When activated, these receptors tell fat cells to break down stored fat and release it for energy.

  3. The theory - AOD-9604 was supposed to activate these beta-3 receptors, causing fat cells to release their stored fat without the side effects of full growth hormone (like insulin resistance or tissue growth).

What actually happened: In mice, it worked. In humans, it didn’t. The Phase 2b trial with 536 people showed no significant weight loss compared to placebo. The drug was abandoned in 2007.

Molecular Target: Beta-3-Adrenergic Receptor Pathway

AOD-9604 represents amino acids 177-191 of human growth hormone with a tyrosine substitution at the N-terminus. Unlike full-length GH, it does not bind to the GH receptor and does not increase IGF-1.

Primary Signaling Cascade:

AOD-9604 → β3-Adrenergic Receptor Activation

Adenylyl Cyclase Stimulation
    ↓
↑ Intracellular cAMP
    ↓
Protein Kinase A (PKA) Activation
    ↓
Hormone-Sensitive Lipase Phosphorylation
    ↓

This cascade essentially tells fat cells to break down stored fat (triglycerides) into components that can be burned for energy.

Key Mechanistic Findings:

  • Preferentially accumulates in adipose (fat) tissue
  • Upregulates beta-3 adrenergic receptor expression over time
  • Beta-3-AR knockout mice are unresponsive to AOD-9604’s effects, proving this receptor is essential
  • Stimulates lipolysis (fat breakdown) and inhibits lipogenesis (fat storage)
  • In animal studies, released fat specifically from obese fat cells, not lean cells

Cyclized Structure for Stability

AOD-9604 features a disulfide bridge between two cysteine residues, creating a ring-shaped structure that:

  • Increases resistance to digestive enzymes
  • Enhances stability in the bloodstream
  • Distinguishes it from the simpler linear hGH Fragment 176-191

Why Development Failed

Despite promising animal data, human trials failed:

  • Effects did not translate - What worked in mice did not work in humans
  • Species differences - Beta-3 receptor biology differs between rodents and humans
  • Bioavailability issues - The drug may not have reached fat tissue effectively in humans
  • Modest effect size - Even if there was a small effect, the trial may have been too small to detect it

The bottom line: over 900 people participated in AOD-9604 trials, but the drug never showed significant weight loss in the pivotal study.
