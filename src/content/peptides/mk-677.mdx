<h2 id="overview" class="sr-only">Overview</h2>

## Quick Summary

- **Orally-active, non-peptide growth hormone secretagogue** that mimics ghrelin at GHS-R1a
- **NOT a peptide** — small molecule (spiroindoline) but commonly searched alongside peptides
- **Not FDA approved** for any indication; Phase 2 trials completed but development discontinued
- **Sustained IGF-1 elevation** — maintains elevated levels for 24+ hours with daily dosing
- **Extensive human data** — multiple RCTs in elderly, obese, GH-deficient, and hip fracture populations
- **Sleep improvement** demonstrated — increases REM and Stage 4 sleep duration
- **WADA prohibited** — banned in all sports as a GH secretagogue

**Evidence Strength: MODERATE** — Multiple Phase 2 randomized controlled trials demonstrate GH/IGF-1 elevation and effects on body composition, bone density, and sleep. Development discontinued due to lack of Phase 3 advancement, not safety concerns.

<h2 id="identity-origins">Identity & Origins</h2>

### What Makes MK-677 Different

Unlike GHRP-6, ipamorelin, and other GH secretagogues in this library, **MK-677 is not a peptide**. It's a small molecule that can be taken orally — a significant pharmacokinetic advantage over injectable peptides.

**Why it's included here:** MK-677 is consistently searched alongside peptides and acts on the same ghrelin receptor (GHS-R1a) as peptide secretagogues. Users researching GHRP-6 or ipamorelin often compare to MK-677 due to the oral bioavailability advantage.

### Why MK-677 Was Developed

Merck recognized that injectable peptide GH secretagogues had limited commercial potential due to:

1. **Poor oral bioavailability** — Peptides are destroyed in the GI tract
2. **Patient convenience** — Daily injections less acceptable than oral dosing
3. **Manufacturing complexity** — Peptides more expensive to produce

MK-677 was designed to maintain ghrelin receptor agonism in a small molecule scaffold with oral bioavailability, solving the "peptide problem" while preserving the physiological advantages of secretagogue-induced GH release over exogenous GH.

<h2 id="mechanism-of-action">Mechanism of Action</h2>

### How MK-677 Works

MK-677 tells your body to release more growth hormone by mimicking ghrelin, the "hunger hormone." Here's what makes it work:

1. **Binds to the ghrelin receptor** — Your body has a receptor called GHS-R1a in the pituitary and hypothalamus. When ghrelin (or MK-677) binds to it, growth hormone is released.

2. **Oral bioavailability** — Unlike peptide secretagogues that must be injected, MK-677 survives stomach acid and enters the bloodstream through the gut. This is its main advantage.

3. **Long-lasting effects** — A single daily dose elevates IGF-1 (the downstream marker of GH activity) for the entire 24-hour period. Peptide secretagogues provide shorter pulses.

4. **Mimics natural GH release** — Rather than providing synthetic GH directly, MK-677 triggers your pituitary to produce and release its own GH. This maintains the body's normal feedback mechanisms.

**Why oral dosing matters:** This allows for easier chronic dosing in clinical trials and real-world use. The Phase 2 trials dosed once daily for up to 2 years.

**Development status:** Despite multiple successful Phase 2 trials, Merck discontinued development. The specific reasons are not public, but may relate to commercial viability rather than safety concerns.

### Receptor Pharmacology

**Primary Target: Ghrelin Receptor (GHS-R1a)**

**Receptor Classification (IUPHAR/BPS Guide to Pharmacology):**
- **Official name:** Ghrelin receptor (GHSR)
- **Gene:** GHSR (human)
- **Class:** Class A G protein-coupled receptor
- **Endogenous ligand:** Ghrelin (pKd 8.6-9.4)

- **Constitutive activity:** Yes — receptor maintains basal activity without ligand

### Signaling Cascade

```
MK-677 oral administration
    ↓
GI absorption → systemic circulation
    ↓
Binds GHS-R1a on pituitary somatotrophs
    ↓
Gαq/11 → PLC → IP3 + DAG
    ↓
Intracellular Ca²⁺ release
    ↓
GH vesicle exocytosis
    ↓
Hepatic IGF-1 synthesis
    ↓
Sustained IGF-1 elevation (24h)
```

### What MK-677 Does vs Doesn't Do

**What it does:**
- Increases GH pulse amplitude (not frequency)
- Elevates IGF-1 to sustained, higher levels
- Increases appetite (ghrelin effect)
- Modestly increases cortisol and prolactin
- Improves sleep architecture

**What it doesn't do:**
- Provide exogenous GH (you make your own)
- Bypass pituitary feedback mechanisms
- Cause GH receptor downregulation
- Significantly affect GH pulsatility pattern

### Landmark Trial: Elderly GH Restoration (1998)

**Chapman et al.** [PMID: 9771850](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9771850/)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Design** | Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled |
| **N** | 32 healthy elderly (64-81 years) |
| **Duration** | 2 months |
| **Dose** | 25 mg once daily oral |
| **Primary outcome** | IGF-1, IGFBP-3 levels |

**Key Results:**
- IGF-1 increased to young adult levels (equivalent to 30-year-olds)
- IGFBP-3 increased proportionally
- GH pulse amplitude increased; frequency unchanged
- Effects maintained throughout 2-month treatment
- Minimal tachyphylaxis observed

**Clinical significance:** Demonstrated that oral secretagogue could restore age-related decline in GH/IGF-1 axis without pituitary exhaustion.

### Sleep Study (1997)

**Copinschi et al.** [PMID: 9349662](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9349662/)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Design** | Randomized, double-blind, crossover |
| **N** | 8 healthy young men |
| **Intervention** | Single dose MK-677 25 mg vs placebo |
| **Outcome** | Polysomnography sleep architecture |

**Key Results:**
- **REM sleep duration:** 50% increase
- **Stage 4 (deep) sleep:** 50% increase
- Sleep latency improved
- Total sleep time unchanged
- High-frequency EEG activity increased
- No morning grogginess reported

**Mechanism:** GH secretagogues have known effects on sleep architecture; MK-677's sustained action produced more robust effects than short-acting peptides.

- Usually remains in normal range

### Why Oral Bioavailability Matters

MK-677's ability to be absorbed orally provides:

1. **Patient compliance** — Daily pill vs multiple injections
2. **Clinical trial feasibility** — Easier long-term studies

4. **Commercial viability** — Broader market potential

This oral bioavailability is why MK-677 reached Phase 2 trials while most peptide secretagogues never advanced beyond Phase 1.

### WADA Status

**MK-677 is PROHIBITED** under the World Anti-Doping Agency code.

- Listed under S2: Peptide Hormones, Growth Factors, Related Substances and Mimetics
- Specifically: "Growth Hormone Secretagogues (GHS)"
- Banned both in-competition and out-of-competition
- Detection methods available via mass spectrometry
- Detectable in urine for extended periods

### Why Development Was Discontinued

Despite positive Phase 2 results, Merck did not advance MK-677 to Phase 3. Possible reasons:

1. **Commercial considerations** — GH market dynamics
2. **Regulatory pathway uncertainty** — Unclear endpoints for approval
3. **Competition** — Other GHS molecules in development
4. **Strategic portfolio decisions** — Merck priorities shifted
5. **NOT safety** — 2-year data showed acceptable tolerability

### Current Availability

- Available from research chemical suppliers
- Quality/purity varies significantly
- Not manufactured to GMP standards
- Legal status varies by jurisdiction
- Not approved for human therapeutic use anywhere

**Date generated:** 2026-01-22

**Global research centers:**
- **USA (Merck Research):** Original development, majority of clinical trials

- **Japan (Kurume):** Ghrelin discovery providing mechanistic foundation

**Evidence strength rating: MODERATE**

This rating reflects:
- Multiple Phase 2 RCTs with consistent positive results
- 2-year chronic dosing safety data
- Clear mechanism with well-characterized receptor pharmacology
- Development discontinued (but not due to safety concerns)
- Extensive human PK/PD characterization

**Distinction from peptides:**

MK-677 is included in this peptide library despite being a non-peptide small molecule because:
1. It acts on the same ghrelin receptor as peptide secretagogues
2. Users frequently search for it alongside peptides
3. Direct comparisons to GHRP-6, ipamorelin are clinically relevant
4. Its oral bioavailability represents the "solution" to peptide limitations

**Key differentiating features:**
- Only orally-active GH secretagogue with extensive human trial data
- Longest duration clinical trials (2 years) of any GHS
- Sustained 24-hour IGF-1 elevation vs pulsatile peptide effects
- Sleep improvement uniquely well-documented

**Update triggers that would require revision:**
- New clinical trial results
- Regulatory approval in any jurisdiction
- Safety signals from ongoing monitoring
- New mechanism discoveries

**Limitations of this dossier:**
- Does not cover research chemical products (quality uncertain)
- No dosing guidance per editorial policy
- Phase 3 data unavailable (never conducted)
- Long-term effects beyond 2 years unknown
