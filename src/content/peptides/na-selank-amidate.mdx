## Overview

### Quick Summary

- **Modified version of Selank** with two chemical modifications: N-acetylation and C-terminal amidation
- **No independent clinical research** — all claims extrapolated from parent Selank studies
- **Not approved** by any regulatory agency (Russia approved unmodified Selank)
- **Marketed claims:** Enhanced stability, longer half-life, improved intranasal absorption
- **Evidence base:** Extremely limited; modification rationale based on general peptide chemistry principles
- **Status:** Research chemical with no regulatory pathway

**Evidence Strength: Very Low** — The parent compound Selank has moderate evidence from Russian research, but this specific modified form has virtually no independent research. Claims are based on theoretical peptide chemistry rather than demonstrated efficacy.

**2. Claimed Stability Improvements:**

| Modification | Purpose | Evidence |
|--------------|---------|----------|
| **N-acetylation** | Protects from aminopeptidase degradation | General peptide chemistry principle |
| **C-amidation** | Protects from carboxypeptidase degradation | General peptide chemistry principle |
| **Combined** | Extended half-life, improved bioavailability | Theoretical, not demonstrated for this peptide |

### Why These Modifications?

The rationale for acetylation and amidation comes from general peptide pharmacology:

**N-Acetylation:**
```
Unprotected N-terminus → Vulnerable to aminopeptidases
    ↓
Acetyl group blocks → Cannot cleave N-terminal amino acid
    ↓
Extended half-life (in theory)
```

**C-Amidation:**
```
Free carboxylic acid terminus → Vulnerable to carboxypeptidases
    ↓
Amide group blocks → Cannot cleave C-terminal amino acid
    ↓
Extended half-life (in theory)
```

### Critical Limitation

**These modifications, while chemically logical, may or may not:**
- Actually improve efficacy
- Maintain the same biological activity as Selank
- Enhance CNS penetration
- Have favorable safety profiles

**No research has been conducted to verify these assumptions for this specific peptide.**
