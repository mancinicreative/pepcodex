GHRP-2 is a synthetic hexapeptide that acts as a potent agonist of the ghrelin receptor (GHS-R1a). Human pharmacodynamic studies establish it as the most potent hexapeptide GH secretagogue, approximately 3x more effective than GHRP-6 at stimulating growth hormone release.

### How It Works (Simplified)

GHRP-2 triggers growth hormone release by mimicking the hunger hormone ghrelin:

    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Receptor Activation</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Binds to ghrelin receptors (GHS-R1a) on pituitary somatotrophs with high affinity, triggering intracellular calcium release.</p>

    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Also acts at hypothalamic level to stimulate GHRH neurons, creating supra-additive effects when combined with exogenous GHRH.</p>

    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Unlike ipamorelin, GHRP-2 significantly increases cortisol and prolactin (more than GHRP-6) while causing less appetite stimulation.</p>

**Key Research:** Arvat et al. (Italy, 1997) established GHRP-2 as the most potent hexapeptide secretagogue with highest cortisol/prolactin effects. [PMID:9285939](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9285939/)

### Important Limitations

- More potent GH release comes with **less selectivity** (cortisol/prolactin elevation)
- Paradoxically causes **less appetite stimulation** than GHRP-6 despite same receptor
- Short half-life (~30 minutes) requires frequent dosing for sustained effects
- **No therapeutic trials completed** - only diagnostic use validated
- Long-term safety data limited to repeat diagnostic administrations
