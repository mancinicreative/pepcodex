## Overview

Selank and NA-Selank-Amidate share the same core structure, but NA-Selank-Amidate has N-acetylation and C-terminal amidation modifications. These changes aim to improve stability and bioavailability. Selank is approved in Russia as an anxiolytic; NA-Selank-Amidate has no approval anywhere.

### C-Terminal Amidation

| Aspect | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Modification** | Convert -COOH to -CONH2 |
| **Purpose** | Protect from carboxypeptidase degradation |
| **Expected Effect** | Improved metabolic stability |

## Rationale for Modifications

Peptide degradation occurs via:
1. **Aminopeptidases** - cleave from N-terminus
2. **Carboxypeptidases** - cleave from C-terminus
3. **Other proteases** - internal cleavage

Modifications address #1 and #2, potentially extending half-life.
