# Content Repurposing System

*Turn one piece of content into many*
*Created: 2026-01-27*

---

## The Repurposing Formula

```
1 Blog Post (1,500 words)
         ↓
├── 3 Instagram Carousels
├── 5-7 Instagram Stories
├── 1 Newsletter Section
├── 3-5 Tweets/X Posts
└── 1 LinkedIn Post (optional)
```

**Time Investment:**
- Blog post: 2 hours
- Repurposing: 1.5 hours
- **Total: 3.5 hours for 10+ pieces of content**

---

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Extract Key Points (10 min)

After writing a blog post, identify:

1. **3 main findings/claims** (for carousels)
2. **5-7 shareable stats** (for stories)
3. **1 hook sentence** (for newsletter)
4. **3 quotable lines** (for tweets)

**Template:**
```
Blog Title: _________________

Main Points:
1. ___________________
2. ___________________
3. ___________________

Key Stats:
- ___________________
- ___________________
- ___________________

Hook: ___________________

Quotable Lines:
1. ___________________
2. ___________________
3. ___________________
```

### Step 2: Create Carousels (45 min)

Turn each main point into a carousel:

**Carousel Structure (8 slides):**
| Slide | Content |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Hook question or bold claim |
| 2-6 | Evidence points (1 per slide) |
| 7 | "Save this for later" |
| 8 | CTA: Follow + Link in bio |

**Design Rules:**
- Brand colors: #8B5CF6 → #7C3AED gradient
- Dark background: #0F0F0F
- Bold sans-serif for headlines
- One point per slide
- Consistent footer: @pepcodex

**Example:**

Blog: "BPC-157 Research Review"

Carousel 1: "BPC-157: What 47 Studies Show"
- Slide 1: "Is BPC-157 overhyped?"
- Slide 2: "47 studies exist, but..."
- Slide 3: "Only 3 are in humans"
- Slide 4: "Most are rat studies"
- Slide 5: "Here's what we actually know..."
- Slide 6: "Gut healing: promising animal data"
- Slide 7: "Save this for later"
- Slide 8: "Follow @pepcodex for more"

Carousel 2: "3 Things Misunderstood About BPC-157"
Carousel 3: "BPC-157 Evidence Timeline"

### Step 3: Create Stories (20 min)

Turn stats into quick story slides:

**Story Types:**
| Type | Format | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| Quick Fact | Stat + Source | "Only 3 human studies exist on BPC-157. Source: PubMed" |
| Poll | Question + 2 options | "Have you tried BPC-157? Yes / No" |
| Teaser | New content alert | "New dossier: BPC-157. Link in bio." |
| Behind the Scenes | Process photo | "Researching the literature..." |
| Q&A | Answer a DM question | "You asked: Is BPC-157 safe?" |

**Design Rules:**
- Same brand colors as carousels
- Large text (readable on mobile)
- 1 point per story
- Add interactive elements (polls, questions)

### Step 4: Write Newsletter Section (15 min)

Summarize blog for newsletter:

**Format:**
```
📊 EVIDENCE SPOTLIGHT

[Blog title]

[2-3 sentence summary]

Key findings:
• [Finding 1]
• [Finding 2]

Evidence grade: [Known/Suggestive/Early]

→ Read the full post: [link]
```

**Example:**
```
📊 EVIDENCE SPOTLIGHT

BPC-157 Research Review: What 47 Studies Show

We analyzed all available research on BPC-157. The results?
Promising for gut healing, but the evidence is almost entirely
from animal studies. Here's what you need to know.

Key findings:
• 47 studies exist, only 3 in humans
• Strong animal data for gut protection
• No long-term human safety data

Evidence grade: Suggestive

→ Read the full post: pepcodex.com/blog/bpc-157-research-review
```

### Step 5: Create Tweets (10 min)

Turn quotable lines into tweets:

**Tweet Formats:**
| Format | Structure |
|--------|-----------|
| Stat tweet | "[Stat]. Source: [study]. More: [link]" |
| Hot take | "[Opinion]. Here's why: [reason]. Thread 🧵" |
| Myth buster | "Myth: [claim]. Reality: [truth]. Data: [source]" |
| Thread intro | "[Topic] explained in 5 tweets. 🧵 Thread:" |

**Example tweets from BPC-157 blog:**

1. "47 studies on BPC-157. Only 3 are in humans. That's a problem. Source: PubMed search."

2. "BPC-157 isn't 'proven' — it's promising. There's a difference. Animal data ≠ human proof."

3. "If someone tells you BPC-157 'cures' anything, they're overstating the evidence. Here's what research actually shows: [link]"
