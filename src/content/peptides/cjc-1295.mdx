<h2 id="mechanism-of-action">Mechanism of Action</h2>

### How CJC-1295 Works

CJC-1295 is a modified version of GHRH (the hormone that tells your pituitary gland to release growth hormone). What makes it special is that it lasts much longer in your body than natural GHRH. Here's how:

1. **Modified to resist breakdown** - Your body has an enzyme called DPP-IV that quickly destroys natural GHRH. CJC-1295 has four amino acid changes that make it resistant to this enzyme, so it stays active longer.

2. **Two versions exist with very different behaviors:**
   - **CJC-1295 with DAC:** Contains a special molecule that attaches to albumin (a protein in your blood). This extends the half-life to 6-8 days, meaning one injection keeps GH elevated for nearly a week.
   - **CJC-1295 without DAC (Mod GRF 1-29):** Has a much shorter half-life of about 30 minutes, creating more natural GH pulses.

3. **Different GH release patterns:**
   - The DAC version creates sustained GH elevation (like leaving a faucet running)
   - The no-DAC version creates pulses (more like how your body naturally works)

**Why it matters:** The with-DAC version keeps IGF-1 elevated for 9-11 days after a single injection. The without-DAC version is preferred by some because pulsatile GH release may be more physiologic and have fewer side effects.

**Development note:** Clinical development was discontinued in 2006 after a participant death in a Phase 2 trial, though this was deemed unrelated to the drug (caused by pre-existing coronary artery disease).

### Molecular Structure: Tetrasubstituted GHRH Analog

CJC-1295 is a 29-amino acid synthetic GHRH analog with **four amino acid substitutions** at positions 2, 8, 15, and 27:
- **D-Ala at position 2**: Prevents DPP-IV cleavage
- **Gln at position 8**: Enhances receptor binding
- **Ala at position 15**: Improves stability
- **Leu at position 27**: Increases potency

These modifications make CJC-1295 **resistant to DPP-IV degradation** (the enzyme that rapidly inactivates endogenous GHRH).

### The Drug Affinity Complex (DAC)

**CJC-1295 with DAC** contains a **maleimidoproprionic acid (MPA)** linker that enables **covalent binding to serum albumin** after injection.

```
CJC-1295 DAC injected subcutaneously
    ↓
MPA group binds to cysteine on albumin
    ↓
Covalent conjugation to circulating albumin
    ↓
Half-life extended to albumin's half-life (~6-8 days)
    ↓
Sustained GH/IGF-1 elevation for days
```

### GHRH Receptor Signaling

Both variants (with/without DAC) activate the GHRH-R on somatotrophs:
```
CJC-1295 binds GHRH-R
    ↓
Gαs activation → Adenylyl cyclase
    ↓
↑ cAMP → PKA activation
    ↓
GH synthesis and secretion
```

### CJC-1295 DAC vs No DAC (Mod GRF 1-29)

| Parameter | With DAC | Without DAC |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| **Half-life** | 6-8 days | ~30 minutes |
| **GH pattern** | Sustained elevation | Pulsatile (more physiologic) |
| **Dosing** | Weekly | Multiple daily |
| **IGF-1 effect** | Elevated 9-11 days | Returns to baseline quickly |
| **GH increase** | 2-10x for 6+ days | Peak then rapid decline |

### Clinical Implications

With a single injection of CJC-1295 DAC:
- Plasma GH increases 2-10 fold for 6+ days
- IGF-1 increases 0.5-3 fold for 9-11 days
- With multiple doses, IGF-1 remains elevated for up to 28 days

**Development note**: CJC-1295 was being developed by ConjuChem for HIV-associated visceral adiposity (lipodystrophy). Phase 2 trials (NCT00267527) were halted in July 2006 after a participant death in Argentina. The attending physician concluded the death was likely due to asymptomatic coronary artery disease with plaque rupture, unrelated to CJC-1295 treatment.

<h2 id="key-human-trials">Key Human Trials</h2>

### Phase 1/2 Studies

**Teichman SL et al. (2006)** [PMID:16352683](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16352683/)
- **Design:** Two randomized, placebo-controlled, double-blind, ascending dose trials (28 and 49 days)
- **Subjects:** Healthy adults ages 21-61 years
- **Intervention:** CJC-1295 SC in ascending single or multiple doses (30-120 mcg/kg)
- **Results:**
  - Single injection: GH increased 2-10 fold for 6+ days; IGF-1 increased 1.5-3 fold for 9-11 days
  - Estimated half-life: 5.8-8.1 days
  - Safe and well-tolerated, particularly at 30-60 mcg/kg doses
  - Evidence of cumulative effect with multiple doses

**Ionescu M et al. (2006)** [PMID:17018654](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17018654/)
- **Design:** GH pulsatility study in healthy men aged 20-40 years
- **Intervention:** Single injection of 60 or 90 mcg/kg CJC-1295
- **Results:**
  - GH pulsatility preserved despite continuous GHRH stimulation
  - Basal (trough) GH levels increased 7.5-fold (p&lt;0.0001)
  - Mean GH levels increased 46% (p&lt;0.01)
  - IGF-1 levels increased 45% (p&lt;0.001)

### Phase 2 Trial (Discontinued)

**NCT00267527** — HIV-Associated Visceral Obesity
- **Design:** Multicenter RCT, N=192 enrolled, 12 weeks treatment + 6-week follow-up
- **Intervention:** Low-dose (60-90-120 mcg/kg escalating) vs high-dose (60-120-240 mcg/kg escalating) vs placebo, weekly SC injections
- **Status:** Terminated July 2006 after participant death (deemed unrelated to study drug)

| Study | N | Key Finding |
|-------|---|-------------|
| Phase 1/2 (Teichman) | 21-61 yr adults | Sustained GH/IGF-1 elevation; half-life 5.8-8.1 days |
| PK/PD study (Ionescu) | 12 men | GH pulsatility preserved; 7.5x increase in trough GH |

Limited published efficacy data in clinical endpoints due to early termination of development program.

<h2 id="preclinical-evidence">Preclinical Evidence</h2>

### Key Preclinical Studies

**Jette L et al. (2005)** [PMID:15817669](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15817669/)
- **Model:** Rat anterior pituitary cells and in vivo Sprague Dawley rats
- **Findings:**
  - CJC-1295 showed 4-fold increase in GH AUC over 2 hours vs hGRF(1-29)
  - Albumin bioconjugates remained in circulation >24 hours
  - DAC mechanism confirmed: MPA group binds covalently to Cys34 on serum albumin
  - Enhanced stability against DPP-IV degradation

<h2 id="canadian-development">Canadian Development History</h2>

### ConjuChem Biotechnologies (Montreal, Quebec)

CJC-1295 was developed by **ConjuChem Biotechnologies**, a Montreal-based biotechnology company that pioneered the Drug Affinity Complex (DAC) platform technology.

**Key Canadian Contributions:**
- Original synthesis and patent of CJC-1295 DAC technology
- Drug Affinity Complex (DAC) platform development enabling albumin binding
- Conducted Phase 1/2 clinical trials
- Bioanalytical methods development (Boulanger et al. 2005)
- Mechanism of action characterization (Jette et al. 2005)

**Patents:**
- US Patent 7,144,854 - Peptide extended release
- US Patent 6,887,470 - Long-lasting GRF analogs
