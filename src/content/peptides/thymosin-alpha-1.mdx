## Overview

<h2 id="overview" class="sr-only">Overview</h2>

### Quick Summary

- **28-amino acid immunomodulatory peptide** derived from thymosin fraction 5 (thymus extract)
- **Approved in 35+ countries** including China, Italy, Philippines, and several Asian/South American nations
- **Brand name: Zadaxin** (SciClone Pharmaceuticals / Alpha-1 Biomedical)
- **Key indications:** Hepatitis B, Hepatitis C, cancer adjuvant therapy, immunodeficiency states
- **Orphan drug status** granted by FDA for hepatitis B and hepatocellular carcinoma
- **Over 100 clinical trials** conducted globally since the 1980s
- **First synthetic peptide drug** approved for immune modulation

**Evidence Strength: High** - Extensive Phase 2/3 clinical trials, regulatory approvals in multiple countries, decades of clinical use with established safety profile. One of the most well-studied immunomodulatory peptides.

### Discovery Timeline

- **1972:** Thymosin fraction 5 (crude thymus extract) isolated by Allan Goldstein at University of Texas
- **1977:** Thymosin alpha-1 purified and sequenced from fraction 5 by Goldstein and colleagues
- **1983:** Chemical synthesis achieved; clinical development begins
- **1984-1990s:** Extensive Phase 2/3 trials in hepatitis and cancer
- **1996:** First regulatory approval (China) for chronic hepatitis B
- **1999:** Approved in Italy for hepatitis B/C and as vaccine adjuvant
- **2001:** FDA Orphan Drug designation for hepatitis B
- **2003:** FDA Orphan Drug designation for hepatocellular carcinoma
- **2006:** Approved in over 35 countries worldwide
- **2020-2021:** Emergency use in COVID-19 (China, Italy)

<h2 id="mechanism-of-action">Mechanism of Action</h2>

Thymosin alpha-1 is a pleiotropic immunomodulator that enhances both innate and adaptive immunity through multiple pathways.

### How Thymosin Alpha-1 Works

Think of your immune system as an army with many divisions. Thymosin alpha-1 acts like a training officer and communication coordinator:

1. **Trains T-cells** - T-cells are the "special forces" of your immune system. Thymosin alpha-1 helps immature T-cells develop into fully functional soldiers that can recognize and attack threats like viruses and cancer cells.

2. **Activates dendritic cells** - Dendritic cells are the "scouts" that identify threats and alert the rest of the immune system. Thymosin alpha-1 makes these scouts more active and better at presenting enemy information to T-cells.

3. **Boosts natural killer cells** - NK cells are the "rapid response team" that kills infected or cancerous cells quickly. Thymosin alpha-1 increases their number and killing ability.

4. **Balances immune responses** - Rather than simply boosting everything (which could cause autoimmunity), thymosin alpha-1 helps restore balance - enhancing weak immune responses while calming overactive ones.

5. **Improves vaccine responses** - By priming the immune system, thymosin alpha-1 helps vaccines work better, especially in elderly or immunocompromised patients.

**Clinical relevance:** These mechanisms explain why thymosin alpha-1 is approved for viral infections (hepatitis B/C) and as a cancer adjuvant - it helps the immune system fight both.

### Scientific Pathways

**1. T-Cell Development and Activation**
```
Thymosin Alpha-1
    |
    v
Binds TLR2/TLR9 on dendritic cells
    |
    v
MyD88-dependent signaling
    |
    v
NF-kB and MAPK activation
    |
    v
+-- CD4+ T-cell differentiation
+-- CD8+ cytotoxic T-cell activation
+-- Th1/Th2 balance regulation
+-- Treg modulation
```

Thymosin alpha-1 binds to Toll-like receptors (TLR2, TLR9) on dendritic cells, triggering intracellular signaling cascades that promote T-cell maturation and activation. This explains its ability to restore immune function in immunocompromised patients.

**2. Dendritic Cell Maturation**
```
Thymosin Alpha-1 + Dendritic Cells
    |
    v
Increased MHC class II expression
    |
    v
Enhanced antigen presentation
    |
    v
+-- Improved T-cell priming
+-- Stronger vaccine responses
+-- Better tumor antigen recognition
```

**3. Cytokine Network Modulation**
```
Thymosin Alpha-1
    |
    v
Increases:
├── IL-2 (T-cell growth factor)
├── IL-12 (Th1 polarization)
├── IFN-alpha (antiviral)
├── IFN-gamma (cellular immunity)
    |
    v
Decreases (in inflammation):
├── IL-1 (pro-inflammatory)
├── TNF-alpha (inflammation/cachexia)
├── IL-6 (acute phase response)
```

This bidirectional cytokine modulation is key - thymosin alpha-1 is immunomodulatory rather than simply immunostimulatory, making it suitable for conditions involving both immune deficiency and dysregulation.

**4. NK Cell Enhancement**
```
Thymosin Alpha-1
    |
    v
Increased NK cell proliferation
    |
    v
Enhanced perforin/granzyme production
    |
    v
Improved cytotoxic activity against:
├── Virus-infected cells
├── Tumor cells
└── Intracellular pathogens
```

Thymosin alpha-1 is N-terminally acetylated, which is critical for biological activity and stability:

| Feature | Value | Significance |
|---------|-------|--------------|
| Length | 28 amino acids | Optimal for immune recognition |
| N-terminus | Acetylated serine | Required for activity |
| Charge | -8 at pH 7 | Anionic peptide |
| Structure | Disordered in solution | Flexible for receptor binding |
| Stability | Relatively stable | Suitable for pharmaceutical use |

**United States**

- Over 70 countries with completed clinical trials

### Key Evidence Highlights

1. **Hepatitis B:** Multiple Phase 3 trials showing enhanced viral clearance when combined with standard therapy
2. **Hepatitis C:** Improved sustained virological response (SVR) in combination with interferon/ribavirin
3. **Hepatocellular Carcinoma:** Improved survival and reduced recurrence as adjuvant therapy
4. **Vaccine Enhancement:** 2-4 fold increased antibody responses in elderly
5. **Sepsis:** Reduced mortality in severe sepsis trials
6. **COVID-19:** Reduced mortality in severe cases (observational data)
