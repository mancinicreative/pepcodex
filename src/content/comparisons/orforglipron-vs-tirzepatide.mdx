## Overview

Orforglipron and tirzepatide represent different approaches to incretin therapy. Orforglipron is an oral, non-peptide, small molecule GLP-1 agonist taken daily, while tirzepatide is an injectable dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist given weekly. Both are from Eli Lilly, representing complementary portfolio options.

## Mechanism Comparison

| Aspect | Orforglipron | Tirzepatide |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| **Targets** | GLP-1 only | GIP + GLP-1 |
| **Molecule Type** | Small molecule | Peptide |
| **Route** | Oral | Injectable |
| **Frequency** | Once daily | Once weekly |

### Key Distinctions

**Orforglipron advantages:**
- Oral administration (no injections)
- Small molecule (easier manufacturing, storage)
- No food timing restrictions (unlike semaglutide oral)
- Potential cost advantages

**Tirzepatide advantages:**
- Dual agonism (GIP adds efficacy)
- Weekly dosing (less frequent)
- Higher weight loss demonstrated
- FDA approved

## Portfolio Strategy

Eli Lilly's approach:
- **Tirzepatide:** Maximum efficacy, injectable
- **Orforglipron:** Oral option, broader access
- **Retatrutide:** Next-gen triple agonist (pipeline)

Different patient populations may prefer different options.

## Summary

- **Orforglipron** offers oral convenience with ~10% weight loss - suitable for patients preferring pills
- **Tirzepatide** provides superior efficacy (~20%+ weight loss) but requires weekly injections
- Dual agonism appears to confer efficacy advantage over GLP-1 alone
- Market may segment by patient preference and indication severity
- Both likely to have roles in obesity treatment
