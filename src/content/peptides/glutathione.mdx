Glutathione is the most abundant intracellular antioxidant in mammalian cells, with well-characterized biochemistry supported by decades of research.

### How It Works (Simplified)

Glutathione functions as a "master antioxidant" through several interconnected pathways:

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 not-prose my-8">
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Free Radical Neutralization</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Donates electrons to neutralize reactive oxygen species (ROS), preventing DNA, protein, and lipid damage before it occurs.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Detoxification</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Conjugates to drugs and environmental toxins via glutathione S-transferases, making them water-soluble for excretion.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Antioxidant Recycling</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Regenerates vitamin C and vitamin E back to their active forms after they neutralize free radicals, amplifying antioxidant capacity.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Immune Support</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Maintains T cell and NK cell function. Adequate intracellular GSH is required for lymphocyte proliferation and cytotoxic activity.</p>
  </div>
</div>

**Key Research:** Richie JP et al. (2015) demonstrated that 6-month oral GSH supplementation increased body stores by 30-35%. [PMID:24791752](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24791752/)

### Important Limitations

- Oral bioavailability historically debated; newer forms (liposomal, sublingual) show improved absorption
- GSH levels decline 10-15% per decade after age 20, accelerating in chronic disease
- Plasma half-life is short (~15-30 minutes); intracellular turnover varies by tissue
- Some clinical trials show inconsistent results depending on delivery method and population studied
