## What is Semaglutide?

Semaglutide is a medication that mimics a natural hormone called GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1). It's FDA-approved for type 2 diabetes and weight management.

## Brand Names

- **Ozempic** — for type 2 diabetes (injection)
- **Wegovy** — for weight management (injection)
- **Rybelsus** — for type 2 diabetes (oral tablet)

## How Does It Work?

Semaglutide works by:
1. **Increasing insulin release** when blood sugar is high
2. **Reducing appetite** through brain signaling
3. **Slowing stomach emptying** to increase fullness
4. **Reducing glucagon** to lower blood sugar

## What Is It Used For?

### Approved Uses
- Type 2 diabetes (blood sugar control)
- Chronic weight management (in obesity)
- Cardiovascular risk reduction (in obesity with heart disease)

## Key Facts

| Fact | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Administration** | Once weekly injection or daily pill |
| **Weight Loss** | Average 15% in clinical trials |
| **FDA Approved** | Yes (2017 for diabetes, 2021 for weight) |

## Common Side Effects

- Nausea (usually improves over time)
- Diarrhea or constipation
- Decreased appetite

## Who Should Not Use It?

- People with personal/family history of medullary thyroid cancer
- People with Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia type 2
- Those allergic to semaglutide
