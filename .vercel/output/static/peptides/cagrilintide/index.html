Also known as: AM833, NN9838, ZP8396

Overview

Quick Summary

  • Long-acting amylin analog developed by Novo Nordisk for obesity treatment; activates amylin receptors (AMY1, AMY2, AMY3) and calcitonin receptor
  • Investigational status: Phase 3 trials ongoing (REDEFINE program); not yet approved for any indication
  • CagriSema combination: Co-formulated with semaglutide as fixed-dose combination; Phase 2 data shows ~22-25% weight loss
  • Phase 2 monotherapy: Up to 10.8% weight loss at 26 weeks with cagrilintide 4.5mg alone [PMID: 34798060]
  • Combination advantage: CagriSema shows additive/synergistic weight loss beyond either agent alone
  • Novel mechanism: Complements GLP-1 pathway via separate amylin-mediated satiety and gastric effects
  • Key trials pending: REDEFINE 1 (vs semaglutide), REDEFINE 2 (in T2D), REDEFINE 3 (vs tirzepatide), REDEFINE CVOT

Evidence Strength: MODERATE - Supported by Phase 2 RCTs with promising efficacy signals. Phase 3 results pending (expected 2025). No regulatory approvals yet. Mechanism well-established from pramlintide and amylin biology literature.

Why Cagrilintide Was Developed

The pramlintide problem: The first amylin analog, pramlintide (Symlin), was FDA-approved in 2005 but had significant limitations:

  • Required three-times-daily injections due to short half-life
  • Modest weight loss (~2-3% additional beyond insulin)
  • Separate injection from insulin required
  • Never achieved widespread adoption

Cagrilintide addresses these limitations through:

  1. Fatty acid acylation: Like semaglutide, cagrilintide has a C18 fatty acid chain enabling albumin binding
  2. Extended half-life: Approximately 7-day half-life allows once-weekly dosing
  3. Combination potential: Engineered for co-formulation with semaglutide (CagriSema)
  4. Higher potency: More sustained amylin receptor activation than pramlintide

Strategic rationale: As GLP-1 agonists face ceiling effects on weight loss (~15-20%), adding complementary mechanisms may achieve greater efficacy. Amylin and GLP-1 work through distinct but complementary pathways.

Mechanism of Action

How Cagrilintide Works (Plain English)

Cagrilintide mimics amylin, a hormone your pancreas releases along with insulin after eating. Here is what amylin does and why it matters:

  1. Sends powerful “full” signals to your brain - Amylin acts on an area called the area postrema in your brainstem, telling your brain you have eaten enough. This is a DIFFERENT pathway than GLP-1, so combining them is like having two separate volume controls for hunger.

  2. Slows stomach emptying - Like GLP-1, amylin slows how fast food leaves your stomach. This keeps you feeling full longer and prevents blood sugar spikes. The combination may produce additive effects.

  3. Restores leptin sensitivity - This is crucial. Many people with obesity have “leptin resistance” - their body produces the “stop eating” hormone leptin, but the brain does not respond to it. Amylin can help restore this sensitivity, potentially explaining why the combination works better than either drug alone.

  4. Reduces glucagon after meals - Amylin suppresses glucagon (a hormone that raises blood sugar), helping control post-meal glucose spikes in diabetes.

Why combine with semaglutide? Think of GLP-1 and amylin as complementary systems. GLP-1 works mainly through the hypothalamus (a different brain region), while amylin works through the brainstem. Using both is like having two independent braking systems - more effective and more reliable than one alone.

Receptor Targets: Amylin Receptors (Scientific Detail)

Cagrilintide is an agonist of amylin receptors, which are heterodimeric complexes of the calcitonin receptor (CTR) with receptor activity-modifying proteins (RAMPs).

The Leptin Sensitization Mechanism

Why this matters: Many people with obesity have elevated leptin levels but do not respond to it (leptin resistance). This creates a vicious cycle where the body keeps storing fat despite signals to stop.

Amylin’s unique role [PMID: 18458326]:

  • In diet-induced obesity, amylin treatment restores leptin responsiveness
  • This may explain why amylin + GLP-1 combination produces synergistic weight loss
  • The amylin pathway reaches satiety centers that leptin resistance may have blocked

In simpler terms: If GLP-1 is telling your brain “you are full” through one channel, amylin is telling it through a completely different channel. And crucially, amylin may help “unlock” the leptin channel that was blocked by obesity.

Why CagriSema May Outperform Individual Agents

The combination rationale:

  1. Complementary satiety pathways: Different brain regions targeted
  2. Additive gastric emptying delay: Potentially stronger effect on nutrient absorption
  3. Leptin sensitization: Unique to amylin pathway
  4. Reduced tachyphylaxis risk: Two mechanisms less likely to both desensitize
  5. Maintained GLP-1 benefits: CV protection, beta-cell effects, glucose control

Physiologic Context: Native Amylin Biology

Endogenous Amylin Physiology

Amylin (islet amyloid polypeptide, IAPP) is a 37-amino acid peptide hormone co-secreted with insulin from pancreatic beta cells in response to nutrient ingestion.

Normal secretion pattern:

  • Fasting amylin: ~5-10 pmol/L
  • Postprandial peak: ~20-30 pmol/L
  • Secreted in fixed ratio with insulin (~1:100)
  • Half-life of native amylin: ~10-15 minutes

Physiologic functions:

FunctionMechanismEffect
SatietyArea postrema activationReduced meal size
Gastric emptyingVagal efferent inhibitionSlower nutrient delivery
Glucagon suppressionDirect alpha-cell effectsReduced hepatic glucose output
Nutrient fluxMultiple mechanismsPrevents postprandial glucose spikes

Amylin in Obesity and Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes:

  • Amylin secretion is impaired as beta-cell function declines
  • Loss of amylin contributes to postprandial hyperglycemia
  • Pramlintide replacement showed modest glycemic benefits

Obesity:

  • Amylin secretion may be normal or elevated initially
  • Evidence for amylin resistance (similar to insulin resistance)
  • Central amylin signaling may be impaired
  • Supraphysiologic amylin (from cagrilintide) overcomes resistance

Supraphysiologic Amylin Effects

Cagrilintide produces plasma concentrations 10-50x higher than endogenous postprandial levels, sustained for a week. This:

  1. Overcomes potential amylin resistance
  2. Produces more sustained CNS effects
  3. May restore leptin sensitivity (not seen with physiologic levels)
  4. Achieves clinically meaningful weight loss

Mechanism Studies

Leptin sensitization (key finding):

  • Diet-induced obese (DIO) rats are leptin resistant
  • Amylin treatment for 2 weeks restores leptin responsiveness
  • Combination of amylin + leptin produces synergistic weight loss
  • Effect persists even after amylin discontinuation (temporary)

Brain penetration:

  • Amylin acts on area postrema (outside blood-brain barrier)
  • Downstream effects propagate to hypothalamus via neural circuits
  • Different access pattern than GLP-1 agonists

In Vitro Studies

  • High-affinity binding to AMY1, AMY2, AMY3 receptors
  • Minimal CGRP receptor activity (avoids vasodilatory effects)
  • Fatty acid acylation extends half-life via albumin binding
  • No significant off-target receptor activity

Inferences Section

This section contains logical reasoning from known evidence. These are NOT proven facts.

Inference 1: CagriSema will demonstrate >20% weight loss in Phase 3

(a) Statement: The CagriSema combination will demonstrate mean weight loss exceeding 20% in Phase 3 trials, potentially matching or exceeding tirzepatide.

(b) Why someone might believe it:

  • Phase 1b showed 17.1% at only 20 weeks (projected higher with longer treatment)
  • Phase 2 in T2D showed ~15.6% (typically less than non-diabetic populations)
  • Complementary mechanisms (amylin + GLP-1) should produce additive effects
  • Both components at maximum dose (2.4 mg each)

(c) Supporting evidence:

  • Phase 1b: -17.1% at 20 weeks [PMID: 33894838]
  • Phase 2 T2D: -15.6% at 32 weeks [PMID: 37364590]
  • Monotherapy: -10.8% at 26 weeks [PMID: 34798060]
  • Mechanistic rationale: distinct satiety pathways

(d) Contradicting evidence:

  • Phase 2 trials are small and can overestimate effects
  • GI tolerability may limit dose escalation in larger populations
  • Tirzepatide achieved ~22% without needing combination
  • Regulatory/commercial success requires head-to-head superiority

(e) Confidence: MODERATE

(f) Study needed: REDEFINE 1 and REDEFINE 3 results (expected 2025)

Inference 2: Amylin adds cardiovascular benefit beyond GLP-1

(a) Statement: The amylin component of CagriSema will provide additional cardiovascular benefit beyond what semaglutide alone offers.

(b) Why someone might believe it:

  • More weight loss generally correlates with CV benefit
  • Amylin affects glucagon and postprandial glucose (CV risk factors)
  • Different mechanism may affect different CV pathways

(c) Supporting evidence:

  • Weight loss strongly associated with CV risk reduction
  • Greater weight loss with combination likely translates to greater CV benefit
  • SELECT showed 20% MACE reduction with semaglutide for obesity

(d) Contradicting evidence:

  • No direct CV outcome data for cagrilintide or amylin analogs
  • Pramlintide did not demonstrate CV benefit in its indication
  • CV benefit may be GLP-1-specific mechanism, not just weight loss
  • REDEFINE CVOT not designed to show superiority vs semaglutide

(e) Confidence: LOW

(f) Study needed: REDEFINE CVOT (NCT05803421) results expected 2029. Note: This trial compares CagriSema to placebo, not semaglutide, so cannot directly test amylin’s incremental CV benefit.

Inference 3: Cagrilintide preserves more lean mass than GLP-1 alone

(a) Statement: The amylin pathway may lead to preferential fat loss with better lean mass preservation compared to GLP-1 agonist monotherapy.

(b) Why someone might believe it:

  • Amylin works through different satiety pathways
  • Leptin sensitization affects energy expenditure regulation
  • Different metabolic signaling may affect body composition

(c) Supporting evidence:

  • Limited; theoretical based on mechanism
  • Some preclinical suggestions of differential metabolic effects
  • Leptin pathway affects both intake and expenditure

(d) Contradicting evidence:

  • No body composition data from Phase 2 CagriSema trials
  • Tirzepatide showed similar ~75/25 fat/lean loss ratio
  • Rapid weight loss typically affects lean mass regardless of mechanism
  • No clinical evidence supports this inference

(e) Confidence: LOW

(f) Study needed: DEXA or MRI body composition substudies within REDEFINE program
