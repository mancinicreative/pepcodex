Sulanemadlin reactivates the p53 tumor suppressor pathway by blocking its negative regulators MDM2 and MDMX.

### How It Works (Simplified)

Sulanemadlin acts as a "decoy" that frees p53 to resume its cancer-fighting function:

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 not-prose my-8">
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">p53 Liberation</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Binds to MDM2 and MDMX proteins that normally suppress p53, freeing p53 to activate tumor suppressor genes.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Dual Targeting</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Unlike small molecule MDM2 inhibitors, blocks BOTH MDM2 and MDMX simultaneously, preventing resistance escape.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Cancer Cell Death</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Activated p53 triggers apoptosis in cancer cells through PUMA, BAX, and NOXA gene expression.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Chemoprotection</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">In normal cells, p53 activation causes temporary G1 arrest, allowing them to "hide" during chemotherapy exposure.</p>
  </div>
</div>

**Key Research:** Saleh MN et al. (USA, 2021) demonstrated a 59% disease control rate with low hematologic toxicity in a first-in-human Phase 1 trial. [PMID:34301750](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34301750/)

### Important Limitations

- Requires TP53 wild-type status; **ineffective in TP53-mutant tumors**
- Currently investigational with no regulatory approvals
- Administered IV only; oral formulation not available
- Long-term safety data limited to Phase 1/2 trial durations
