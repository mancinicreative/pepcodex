## Overview

<h2 id="overview" class="sr-only">Overview</h2>

### Quick Summary

- **Selective growth hormone secretagogue** that binds to ghrelin/GHSR receptors
- **Not FDA approved** for any indication; development discontinued
- **Minimal cortisol/prolactin effects** compared to other GH secretagogues
- **Limited human clinical data** despite early-phase trials
- **Popular in wellness/anti-aging** communities despite lack of approval

**Evidence Strength: Low** — Some Phase 1/2 human data exists showing GH release, but clinical development was not completed.

### Development History

- **1984:** Foundational GHRP discovery by Bowers et al. at Tulane University (USA)
- **1995-1996:** GHS-R receptor identified by Merck Research (USA)
- **1998:** Ipamorelin characterized by Novo Nordisk (Denmark) as first selective GH secretagogue
- **1999:** Human PK/PD studies; Danish-American collaboration with SUNY Buffalo
- **1999:** Ghrelin discovered in Japan (Kojima et al.); explains ipamorelin's mechanism
- **2000-2001:** Bone effects studied in Scandinavian collaboration (Denmark/Sweden)
- **2014:** Phase 2 trial for postoperative ileus completed (USA/Canada)
- **Current:** Development discontinued; no active pharmaceutical development

<h2 id="mechanism-of-action">Mechanism of Action</h2>

### How Ipamorelin Works

Ipamorelin tells your pituitary gland (a small gland at the base of your brain) to release growth hormone. Here's what makes it different from other similar peptides:

1. **Targets the ghrelin receptor** - Your body has a receptor called GHS-R1a (also known as the ghrelin receptor) that, when activated, triggers growth hormone release. Ipamorelin binds to this receptor.

2. **Highly selective** - Unlike older peptides (GHRP-6, GHRP-2), ipamorelin only triggers growth hormone release without raising cortisol (the stress hormone) or prolactin. This is its main advantage.

3. **Mimics natural GH pulses** - Rather than providing a constant flood of growth hormone, ipamorelin triggers the same pulsatile release pattern your body uses naturally.

**Why selectivity matters:** When you take older GH secretagogues like GHRP-6, you also get increased cortisol (which can cause stress effects), increased prolactin (which can have hormonal effects), and increased appetite. Ipamorelin avoids these side effects, making it theoretically "cleaner" for stimulating growth hormone alone.

**Development status:** Despite these advantages, ipamorelin's clinical development was discontinued after it failed to show efficacy in a Phase 2 trial for post-operative ileus (bowel recovery after surgery). It is not approved for any indication.

### GHS-R1a Receptor Binding

Ipamorelin is the **first selective GH secretagogue** with selectivity for GH release similar to GHRH. It binds to the growth hormone secretagogue receptor type 1a (GHS-R1a), also known as the ghrelin receptor.

**Receptor Classification (IUPHAR):**
- **Official name:** Ghrelin receptor (GHSR)
- **Gene:** GHSR (human)
- **Class:** Class A G protein-coupled receptor (7 transmembrane domains)
- **Endogenous ligand:** Ghrelin (pKd 8.6-9.4)
- **Constitutive activity:** Yes - receptor is active even without ligand binding

**Primary Signaling Cascade:**
```
Ipamorelin binds GHS-R1a (GPCR on somatotrophs)
    ↓
Gq/11 protein activation
    ↓
Phospholipase C (PLC) activation
    ↓
IP3 + DAG generation
    ↓
Intracellular Ca²⁺ mobilization
    ↓
GH vesicle exocytosis
```

**Secondary Signaling Pathways:**
- Gi/Go and G12/G13 coupling
- G protein-independent beta-arrestin signaling
- MAPK/ERK phosphorylation
- RhoA activation

### Unique Selectivity Profile

**The Key Difference from GHRP-6/GHRP-2:**
Ipamorelin **does not release ACTH or cortisol** even at doses >200-fold higher than the ED50 for GH release.

| Effect | GHRP-6 | GHRP-2 | Ipamorelin |
|--------|--------|--------|------------|
| GH release | Strong | Strong | **Strong** |
| ACTH/Cortisol | ↑ Moderate | ↑ Higher | **None** |
| Prolactin | ↑ Moderate | ↑ Higher | **None** |
| Appetite | ↑ Strong | ↑ Moderate | **Minimal** |

### Structural Features

Ipamorelin is a pentapeptide: **Aib-His-D-2-Nal-D-Phe-Lys-NH2**

**Design elements contributing to selectivity:**
- **Alpha-aminoisobutyric acid (Aib)** at N-terminus
- **D-amino acids** at positions 3 and 4 (D-naphthylalanine, D-phenylalanine)
- These modifications:
  - Enhance peptidase resistance
  - Optimize receptor binding affinity
  - Extend half-life vs endogenous GHRH

### GHS-R1a Receptor Distribution

| Tissue | Expression | Effect of Activation |
|--------|------------|---------------------|
| Anterior pituitary | High | GH release from somatotrophs |
| Hypothalamus (arcuate) | High | Food intake, energy balance |
| Hippocampus | Moderate | Cognitive effects |
| Myocardium | Low | Potential cardioprotection |

### GH Release Pattern

Studies show ipamorelin produces:
- **Dose-dependent GH release** with physiologic pulses
- Peak GH at 30-60 minutes post-injection
- Effects attenuated with repeated dosing (some tachyphylaxis)
- Synergistic with endogenous GHRH

1. **GH release:** Confirmed dose-dependent stimulation in humans (Danish-American collaboration)
2. **Selectivity:** Minimal cortisol/prolactin elevation demonstrated (Novo Nordisk, Denmark)
3. **Bone effects:** Increased BMC in rat models (Denmark/Sweden collaboration)
4. **Tolerability:** Generally well-tolerated in short-term studies (USA/Canada trials)
5. **Development:** Discontinued after Phase 2 POI trial failed to meet primary endpoint

### PK/PD Study in Healthy Volunteers (1999)

**Gobburu et al.** [PMID:10496658](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10496658/)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **N** | 40 healthy male volunteers (8 per dose level) |
| **Design** | Dose-escalation, IV infusion over 15 minutes |
| **Doses** | 4.21, 14.02, 42.13, 84.27, 140.45 nmol/kg |
| **SC50** | 214 nmol/L (concentration for half-maximal GH) |
| **Max GH production** | 694 mIU/L/h |
| **Half-life** | 2 hours |
| **Clearance** | 0.078 L/h/kg |
| **Volume of distribution** | 0.22 L/kg |

**Key finding:** PK parameters showed dose-proportionality. Single episode of GH release with peak at 0.67 hours, followed by exponential decline.

### Postoperative Ileus Trial - NCT00672074 (2014)

**Beck et al.** [PMID:25331030](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25331030/)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Design** | Multicenter, double-blind, placebo-controlled, Phase 2 |
| **Population** | Adults undergoing small/large bowel resection |
| **Intervention** | IV ipamorelin 0.03 mg/kg vs placebo BID, POD 1-7 |
| **N** | 114 (ipamorelin n=56, placebo n=58) |
| **Primary endpoint** | Time to tolerance of standardized solid meal |
| **Result** | Median 25.3h (ipamorelin) vs 32.6h (placebo), p=0.15 |
| **Safety** | 87.5% AEs (ipamorelin) vs 94.8% (placebo) |

**Outcome:** Ipamorelin was well tolerated but **did not meet primary endpoint**. No significant difference in GI recovery between groups. Development discontinued due to lack of efficacy.

### Selectivity Study (1998)

**Raun et al.** [PMID:9849822](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9849822/)

Demonstrated that unlike GHRP-6 and GHRP-2, ipamorelin **did not release ACTH or cortisol** at levels significantly different from GHRH stimulation, even at doses >200-fold higher than the ED50 for GH release. This established ipamorelin as the first selective GH secretagogue.

### Bone Effects Research (Scandinavian Studies)

The Novo Nordisk-Sahlgrenska collaboration (Denmark-Sweden) produced significant bone research:

**Johansen PB et al. 1999** [PMID:10373343](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10373343/)
- Model: Hypophysectomized and intact rats
- Ipamorelin stimulated tibial epiphyseal width comparable to GH treatment
- Demonstrates bone growth effects independent of endogenous GH

**Andersen NB et al. 2001** [PMID:10828840](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10828840/)
- Model: Adult female Sprague-Dawley rats
- 12-week treatment with ipamorelin increased whole-body BMC by 11%
- Comparable to GHRP-6 for bone anabolism
