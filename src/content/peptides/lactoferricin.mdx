The proposed mechanisms of Lactoferricin are based primarily on in vitro and animal studies. Human mechanistic data is lacking.

### How It Works (Simplified)

Lactoferricin acts as a natural antimicrobial by disrupting microbial membranes through several key mechanisms:

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 not-prose my-8">
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Electrostatic Attraction</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Cationic peptide (+8 charge) binds to negatively charged bacterial membranes via electrostatic attraction, initiating the killing process.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Membrane Disruption</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Amphipathic structure allows insertion into lipid bilayers, forming pores that cause membrane permeabilization and cell content leakage.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Selective Toxicity</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Human cells have neutral, cholesterol-rich membranes that resist attack, while bacterial and cancer cells have vulnerable anionic surfaces.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Apoptosis Induction</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">In cancer cells, triggers mitochondrial apoptosis pathway via ROS generation and caspase activation, independent of membrane lysis.</p>
  </div>
</div>

**Key Research:** Bellamy W et al. (1993) demonstrated rapid binding to bacterial surfaces with over 10 million molecules per cell, disrupting membrane permeability. [PMID:8300449](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8300449/)

### Important Limitations

- Almost all research is preclinical (in vitro and animal studies)
- No human clinical trials have evaluated therapeutic efficacy
- Stability and delivery challenges limit in vivo applications
- Optimal formulation for systemic use remains unknown
- Pharmacokinetics in humans not characterized
