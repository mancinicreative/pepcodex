## Overview

### Quick Summary

- **Synthetic hexapeptide** (6 amino acids) derived from ciliary neurotrophic factor (CNTF)
- **Not approved** by any regulatory agency worldwide
- **No human clinical trials** conducted or registered on ClinicalTrials.gov
- **Research origin:** New York State Institute for Basic Research in Developmental Disabilities
- **Mechanism claim:** Mimics BDNF to promote neurogenesis and synaptic plasticity
- **Proposed use:** Cognitive enhancement, Alzheimer's disease research
- **Sold as:** Research chemical with no quality standards

**Evidence Strength: Very Low** — Limited preclinical data primarily from a single research group with no human studies.

- **2010-2015:** Preclinical studies in Alzheimer's disease models

## Mechanism of Action

### Proposed Mechanism

P21 is designed to bypass the blood-brain barrier and activate neurotrophic signaling:

**The Neurogenesis Hypothesis:**

```
P21 (small peptide, CNS-penetrant)
    ↓
Claimed BDNF-like signaling activation
    ↓
├── Neurogenesis in dentate gyrus
├── Dendritic arborization
├── Synaptic plasticity enhancement
└── Anti-apoptotic effects in neurons
```

### Scientific Rationale

**Why CNTF-Derived?**

CNTF (Ciliary Neurotrophic Factor) is a neurotrophic factor that supports neuronal survival but is too large to cross the blood-brain barrier easily. The research team attempted to create a small peptide that could:

1. Cross the BBB when administered systemically
2. Retain neurotrophic signaling properties
3. Promote hippocampal neurogenesis

P21 (also called P021 or Ac-DGGLAG-NH2) was designed as a CNTF-derived small peptide that can cross the blood-brain barrier. Studies in 3xTg-AD mice showed correction of neurogenesis deficits and reduction of tau pathology through GSK3-beta inhibition and BDNF enhancement.

## Summary

P21 is an experimental CNTF-derived hexapeptide with:

**What We Know:**
- Designed to mimic BDNF neurotrophic effects
- Shows neurogenesis in rodent models
- Improves memory in AD mouse models
- Most research from one laboratory

**What We Don't Know:**
- Whether it works in humans
- Whether it's safe for human use
- Actual mechanism of action
- Long-term effects
- Whether claimed BDNF-mimetic activity is real

**Bottom Line:** P21 represents an interesting research concept, but the lack of clinical development after 15+ years, limited independent replication, and absence of human data make it a highly experimental compound with unknown risks.
