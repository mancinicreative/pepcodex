# Regional & Specialized Sources

Comprehensive guide to regional databases and specialized journals for peptide research.

---

## Russian Sources

### eLibrary.ru

**URL:** https://elibrary.ru
**Trust:** 4/5 | **Access:** Freemium | **Coverage:** 38M+ papers, 5,600+ journals

### What It Is
Russia's largest scientific electronic library. Essential for Soviet/CIS-era research and current Russian publications on peptide bioregulators (Khavinson school).

### Why It Matters for Peptides
Most foundational research on bioregulator peptides (Epithalon, Thymalin, Vilon, etc.) was conducted in Russia and published in Russian. This research is often not indexed in PubMed.

### Key Peptide Researchers (Russian)
- **Khavinson V.Kh.** (Хавинсон В.Х.) - Bioregulator peptides pioneer
- **Anisimov V.N.** (Анисимов В.Н.) - Aging research, melatonin
- **Morozov V.G.** (Морозов В.Г.) - Original bioregulator work
- **Kuznik B.I.** (Кузник Б.И.) - Hemostasis peptides

### Search Strategies

**Russian Keywords:**
```
пептидные биорегуляторы (peptide bioregulators)
эпиталон (Epithalon)
эпиталамин (Epithalamin)
тималин (Thymalin)
вилон (Vilon)
кортаген (Cortagen)
геропротекторы (geroprotectors)
теломераза (telomerase)
старение (aging)
```

**Author Search Format:**
```
Хавинсон В.Х.
Анисимов В.Н.
```

### Tips
- Search in Russian for best results (Cyrillic)
- Use author names + topic keywords
- Filter by year for recent work
- Check if English version exists
- Machine translate abstracts (Google Translate works well)
- Some articles have free PDFs (especially older)

### Trust Rationale
Authoritative for Russian publications, operated by Russian Foundation for Basic Research. Many journals are not internationally peer-reviewed, so verify journal reputation. Essential for bioregulator peptide research unavailable elsewhere.

### CyberLeninka

**URL:** https://cyberleninka.ru
**Trust:** 4/5 | **Access:** Free (Open Access) | **Coverage:** 3M+ articles

### What It Is
Top Russian open-access repository (among world's top 5 OA repositories). Full-text PDFs freely available.

### When to Use
- Getting full text when eLibrary requires subscription
- Open access Russian research
- University journal publications
- Dissertation-related articles

### Example Searches
```
Эпиталон (Epitalon)
пептиды старение (peptides aging)
биорегуляторы Хавинсон (bioregulators Khavinson)
тималин иммунитет (thymalin immunity)
```

### Tips
- Download PDFs directly
- Use with eLibrary for comprehensive coverage
- Filter by discipline
- Quality varies - check journal reputation

### Trust Rationale
Content from peer-reviewed Russian sources, openly available. Platform is credible but journal quality varies.

### Advances in Gerontology (Uspekhi Gerontologii)

**Journal:** Uspekhi Gerontologii / Advances in Gerontology
**ISSN:** 1561-9125
**Trust:** 4/5 | **Access:** Mixed

### What It Is
Flagship Russian gerontology journal. Primary publication venue for Khavinson group's bioregulator peptide research.

### Key Papers
- "Peptide bioregulators – new class of geroprotectors" (2013, Parts 1 & 2)
- Human clinical studies on Epithalon, Thymalin
- Telomerase activation studies
- Mortality reduction trials

### How to Access
- **eLibrary.ru:** Russian full text
- **Springer:** English translation (some articles)
- **PubMed:** Some abstracts indexed

### Search Tips
```
# In eLibrary
"Успехи геронтологии" пептид

# In PubMed
"Adv Gerontol"[Journal] peptide
```

### Biogerontology

**URL:** https://link.springer.com/journal/10522
**ISSN:** 1389-5729 | **Trust:** 5/5 | **Access:** Subscription (Springer)

### What It Is
International peer-reviewed journal on aging biology. Published key English-language reviews on Russian bioregulator peptide research.

### Key Papers
- Anisimov & Khavinson reviews on peptide bioregulation
- Epithalon mechanism studies
- Comparative aging research

### PubMed Search
```
"Biogerontology"[Journal] AND peptide
"Biogerontology"[Journal] AND Khavinson
"Biogerontology"[Journal] AND geroprotector
```

### Trust Rationale
Well-respected international journal, rigorous peer review. English-language bridge for Russian peptide research.

## Chinese Sources

### CNKI (China National Knowledge Infrastructure)

**URL:** https://www.cnki.net / https://oversea.cnki.net (international)
**Trust:** 5/5 (for scope) | **Access:** Subscription | **Coverage:** 20M+ papers

### What It Is
The central academic repository in China. Contains Chinese journal articles, dissertations, conference papers, newspapers.

### Why It Matters
- Massive volume of peptide/TCM research
- Many supplement trials only in Chinese
- Growing pharmaceutical peptide research
- Unique clinical data from Chinese hospitals

### Search Strategies

**Chinese Keywords:**
```
肽 (peptide)
生长因子 (growth factor)
多肽 (polypeptide)
胶原蛋白肽 (collagen peptide)
免疫调节肽 (immunomodulatory peptide)
临床试验 (clinical trial)
随机对照 (randomized controlled)
系统评价 (systematic review)
```

**Topic Combinations:**
```
肽 临床 效果 (peptide clinical effect)
多肽 老化 (polypeptide aging)
BPC-157 中国 (BPC-157 China)
```

### Access Options
- **CNKI Oversea:** International portal
- **CNKI Scholar:** English metadata
- **Institutional access:** Most universities
- **Individual articles:** Can purchase

### Tips
- Use Google Translate for search terms
- Check for English abstracts
- Identify journal reputation (core journals)
- Cross-reference with ChiCTR for trials
- Machine translate full text as needed

### Trust Rationale
Comprehensive and authoritative for Chinese research. Individual journal quality varies - prefer "core journals" (核心期刊).

## Translation Strategies

### For Russian Sources
1. **Google Translate:** Paste Cyrillic text
2. **DeepL:** Often better quality
3. **Yandex Translate:** Best for Russian technical terms
4. **Browser extensions:** Auto-translate pages

### For Chinese Sources
1. **Google Translate:** Generally good
2. **DeepL:** Improving for Chinese
3. **CNKI Translate:** Built-in option
4. **Baidu Translate:** Chinese-optimized

### Tips
- Translate abstracts first to assess relevance
- Keep original terms for key concepts
- Verify technical terms manually
- Use bilingual dictionaries for peptide names
