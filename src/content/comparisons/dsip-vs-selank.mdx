## Overview

DSIP (delta sleep-inducing peptide) and selank are both peptides that affect central nervous system function, but target different aspects of brain activity. DSIP is proposed to modulate sleep architecture, while selank is proposed as an anxiolytic. Neither is approved by Western regulatory agencies.

This comparison matters because both peptides are discussed in contexts of stress management and sleep optimization, though they work through different mechanisms.
