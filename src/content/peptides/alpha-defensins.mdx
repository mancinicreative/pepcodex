Alpha-defensins are endogenous antimicrobial peptides with well-characterized mechanisms supported by extensive structural and functional studies from multiple independent research groups worldwide.

### How It Works (Simplified)

Alpha-defensins function as key effectors of innate immunity through several pathways:

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 not-prose my-8">
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Membrane Disruption</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Cationic peptides bind to anionic microbial membranes, insert into lipid bilayer, and form pores causing ion leakage and cell death.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Immune Signaling</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Recruit and activate immune cells including T cells and dendritic cells, bridging innate and adaptive immunity responses.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Nanonet Formation</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">HD-6 self-assembles into fiber structures that physically trap bacteria, representing a unique non-lytic defense mechanism.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Antiviral Activity</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Directly inactivate viral particles, block entry receptors, and interfere with fusion to inhibit viral replication.</p>
  </div>
</div>

**Key Research:** Selsted ME et al. (UCLA, 1985) first characterized HNP structure and antimicrobial mechanism. [PMID:4056036](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/4056036/)

### Important Limitations

- **Salt sensitivity** reduces antimicrobial activity in physiological conditions (150 mM NaCl)
- In vitro MIC values may **overestimate** in vivo efficacy
- No alpha-defensin therapeutics currently approved despite decades of research
- High synthesis cost due to complex disulfide bonding (3 intramolecular bonds)
- Concentration-dependent cytotoxicity may limit therapeutic window
