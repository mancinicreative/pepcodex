Alixorexton is an orexin receptor 2 (OX2R) selective agonist that directly addresses the pathophysiology of narcolepsy type 1 by restoring orexin signaling lost due to autoimmune destruction of orexin-producing neurons.

### How It Works (Simplified)

Alixorexton acts as a replacement signal for the missing orexin in narcolepsy patients:

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 not-prose my-8">
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">OX2R Activation</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Selectively binds and activates orexin receptor 2 in wake-promoting brain regions, mimicking the natural orexin signal that is absent in NT1 patients.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Neuronal Excitation</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Triggers Gq-protein signaling cascade leading to neuronal depolarization and increased firing in the tuberomammillary nucleus, locus coeruleus, and other arousal centers.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Neurotransmitter Release</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Promotes release of histamine, norepinephrine, dopamine, and acetylcholine from wake-promoting neurons, collectively maintaining the wakeful state.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Cataplexy Prevention</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Restores orexin-mediated inhibition of inappropriate muscle atonia during wakefulness, reducing sudden loss of muscle tone triggered by emotions.</p>
  </div>
</div>

**Key Research:** Willie JT et al. (2003) demonstrated that OX2R knockout mice exhibit a distinct narcolepsy phenotype with disrupted wakefulness, validating OX2R as a critical target for wakefulness. [PMID:12797957](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12797957/)

### Important Limitations

- Phase 3 clinical trial data not yet available
- Long-term efficacy and safety with chronic use unknown
- Optimal dosing for different patient populations not established
- No head-to-head trials against current standard of care (sodium oxybate, stimulants)
- Investigational drug not approved by FDA or any regulatory agency
- Unknown whether efficacy extends to narcolepsy type 2 (without orexin deficiency)
