TB-500

Also known as: Thymosin Beta-4 Fragment, TB4-FRAG, TMSB4X

A synthetic fragment of thymosin beta-4 marketed for tissue repair. While the parent compound has Phase 3 clinical research (RGN-259), TB-500 specifically has no published human data.

Overview

Overview

Quick Summary

  • Synthetic fragment of thymosin beta-4, a naturally occurring 43-amino acid protein
  • Not FDA approved for any indication; no regulatory approval worldwide
  • Parent compound (Tb4) has Phase 3 clinical research (RGN-259 for ophthalmology)
  • Popular in veterinary/equine applications before human interest grew - extensively researched in Australia
  • Global research spans USA, Europe (UK, Germany, Italy), Asia (China, South Korea), and Australia
  • Limited human safety and efficacy data available for TB-500 specifically

Evidence Strength: Low - Moderate evidence for parent compound thymosin beta-4 including Phase 3 ophthalmology trials, but TB-500 fragment specifically lacks human clinical trials.

Background

Thymosin beta-4 is an endogenous protein involved in cell migration, wound healing, and tissue repair. TB-500 refers to a synthetic fragment or analog marketed for similar effects, though the exact relationship to Tb4 and its biological activity may vary by manufacturer. The parent compound has been extensively studied globally with significant research contributions from the USA, UK, Germany, China, South Korea, and Australia.

Mechanism of Action

The proposed mechanisms are largely extrapolated from thymosin beta-4 research, not TB-500 specifically.

What This Means in Plain English

TB-500 is related to a natural protein in your body called thymosin beta-4, which helps with:

  1. Cell movement and flexibility - Every cell has an internal “skeleton” made of proteins called actin. TB-500 may help reorganize this skeleton, making cells more flexible and able to move. Think of it like loosening a stiff joint so it can move more freely.

  2. Wound healing - When you get a cut, skin cells need to multiply and spread across the wound to close it. In animal studies, thymosin beta-4 helped wounds close faster by encouraging skin cells to migrate and cover the injury.

  3. Growing new blood vessels - Healing tissues need blood flow to receive oxygen and nutrients. TB-500 may help stimulate the growth of new blood vessels to injured areas, similar to building new roads to a construction site.

  4. Heart protection - In animal studies after simulated heart attacks, thymosin beta-4 helped heart cells survive and reduced scarring. Scars in the heart don’t pump blood, so less scarring = better heart function.

  5. Brain and nerve repair - Research from Henry Ford Hospital (USA) shows Tb4 promotes oligodendrogenesis (myelin-producing cells) and neuroregeneration after stroke and traumatic brain injury.

  6. Reducing inflammation - Chronic inflammation can delay healing. TB-500 may help calm down excessive inflammatory responses via NF-kB suppression and microRNA-146a pathway.

Important caveat: Almost all research is on the parent compound (thymosin beta-4), not TB-500 specifically. TB-500 is a fragment that may or may not have the same effects.

Scientific Pathways

Core Mechanism: Actin Binding & Cytoskeletal Regulation

Thymosin beta-4 is one of the most abundant intracellular proteins in mammalian cells. Its primary function is regulating actin dynamics.

The LKKTETQ Motif: TB-500’s active region (amino acids 17-23) contains the LKKTETQ sequence with high affinity for actin binding. This was confirmed by Sosne et al. (PMID:20179146) as critical for wound healing activity.

Actin is a protein that forms the “skeleton” inside cells. When actin molecules link together (polymerize), they form stiff fibers. TB-500 binds to individual actin molecules and prevents them from linking up, keeping them in reserve. This gives cells more flexibility to change shape and move - essential for healing.

Cardiac Repair (Animal Studies)

Mechanisms in Myocardial Injury:

  • Binds/alters cytoskeletal actin filaments
  • Activates ILK-Akt pathway (cell survival, motility) - key finding from Bock-Marquette et al., Nature 2004
  • Promotes epicardial progenitor cell migration (UK research - Riley lab, Oxford)
  • Reduces scar formation and prevents cardiac rupture in MI models (PMID:24993041)

After a heart attack, heart cells die and are replaced with scar tissue. Scars can’t pump blood, so more scarring means a weaker heart. In animal studies, thymosin beta-4 helped heart cells survive the injury and reduced scarring, leading to better heart function.

Neurological Effects (Animal Studies)

Research from Henry Ford Hospital (Chopp, Xiong et al.) demonstrates:

  • Stroke recovery: Improved functional neurological outcome in embolic stroke model (PMID:20434526)
  • Traumatic brain injury: Reduced lesion volume, increased neurogenesis (PMID:20617881)
  • Oligodendrogenesis: Promotes myelin-producing cell proliferation (PMID:19619644)
  • Potential for demyelinating diseases: MS, TBI recovery applications under investigation

Anti-Inflammatory Effects

Modulates inflammation through:

  • MicroRNA-146a pathway: Suppresses IRAK1 and TRAF6
  • NF-kB suppression: Key mechanism in corneal repair (PMID:26241399)
  • Reduced tissue fibrosis in multiple organ models
  • Regulates inflammatory cytokines and chemokines

Your body uses inflammation to fight infection and start healing, but too much inflammation causes collateral damage. TB-500 may help fine-tune this response - allowing enough inflammation to heal while preventing the excessive inflammation that creates scar tissue and tissue damage.

Key Evidence Highlights

For Thymosin Beta-4 (parent compound):

  1. Phase 3 trials in ophthalmology (neurotrophic keratopathy) - 60% achieved complete healing
  2. Phase 2 trials in wound healing and dry eye
  3. Phase 1 cardiac trial (RGN-352)
  4. Extensive preclinical across wound healing, cardiac, neuro, ophthalmology, anti-fibrosis

For TB-500:

  1. No completed human clinical trials
  2. Veterinary use in horses precedes human interest (Australian research significant)
  3. Assumed similar properties to Tb4 - but not validated

Ophthalmology Trials Detail

SEER-1 (Phase 3, USA) - PMID:36613994

  • 0.1% RGN-259 ophthalmic solution for neurotrophic keratopathy
  • 60% achieved complete corneal healing vs 31.9% placebo at 28 days
  • Statistically significant improvement in epithelial defect closure

SEER-3 (Phase 3, EU)

  • Failed to meet primary endpoint in 2025
  • Stronger-than-expected placebo effect noted
  • US trials continue (SEER-2)

Dry Eye Trials - PMID:30220049

  • RGN-259 improved corneal staining, tear breakup time, and ocular discomfort vs placebo
  • Phase 2 results published in Translational Vision Science & Technology (2018)

Wound Healing Trials

Phase 2 Venous Stasis Ulcers - NCT00832091

  • Approximately 1 month faster healing compared to placebo
  • Published in NYAS proceedings

TB-500 Specific Trials

No human clinical trials have been published for TB-500 specifically.

The lack of TB-500-specific human data means efficacy claims are extrapolated from thymosin beta-4 research, which may or may not apply.

Important Context

RGN-259 (thymosin b4 ophthalmic solution) is being developed by RegeneRx/HLB Therapeutics. The SEER-3 Phase 3 trial in Europe failed to meet primary endpoint in 2025 due to stronger-than-expected placebo effect. US Phase 3 trials continue.

Important Notes

  • TB-500 is a synthetic analog/fragment; activity may differ from full Tb4
  • Phase 2/3 trials used parent compound, not TB-500
  • Not FDA approved; prohibited by WADA
  • Australian and Hong Kong equine research significant for detection methods
