## Overview

### Quick Summary

- **Endogenous antimicrobial peptides** — key components of human innate immunity
- **Two subfamilies:** Neutrophil (HNP-1 to 4) and enteric (HD-5, HD-6)
- **Broad-spectrum activity** — antibacterial, antiviral, antifungal
- **Immunomodulatory** — beyond direct killing, modulate immune responses
- **Well-characterized** — decades of research, known structures and mechanisms
- **Therapeutic interest** — potential for infectious disease, wound healing, cancer

**Evidence Strength: Moderate** — Extensive basic science and human biomarker research from multiple independent laboratories worldwide. Alpha-defensins are established components of innate immunity with well-characterized mechanisms. Therapeutic development is ongoing but no approved products yet.

## Mechanism of Action

### Antimicrobial Mechanisms

**1. Membrane Disruption (Primary Mechanism):**

```
Alpha-defensin (cationic, amphipathic)
    ↓
Electrostatic attraction to microbial membrane (anionic)
    ↓
Insertion into lipid bilayer
    ↓
├── Pore formation
├── Membrane permeabilization
├── Ion leakage
└── Cell death
```

**Why Selective for Microbes:**

| Membrane Type | Charge | Susceptibility |
|---------------|--------|----------------|
| Bacterial | Anionic (negative) | High |
| Fungal | Anionic | High |
| Mammalian | Neutral (zwitterionic) | Low |

**2. Intracellular Targets:**

Beyond membrane disruption, defensins can:
- Inhibit cell wall synthesis
- Bind DNA and inhibit replication
- Disrupt protein synthesis
- Inhibit enzymatic functions

**3. Antiviral Mechanisms:**

```
Alpha-defensins → Viral particles
    ↓
├── Direct virion inactivation
├── Block viral entry receptors
├── Interfere with fusion
├── Inhibit intracellular replication
└── Enhance adaptive immune response
```

### Immunomodulatory Functions

| Function | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| Chemotaxis | Attract immune cells to infection site |
| Dendritic cell activation | Enhance antigen presentation |
| Cytokine modulation | Affect IL-1, TNF-alpha, others |
| Complement activation | Enhance innate immunity |
| Adaptive immunity bridging | Link innate to adaptive response |

### Unique HD-6 Mechanism: Nanonets

HD-6 has a unique ability to form "nanonets":

```
HD-6 → Self-assembly
    ↓
Nanonet fiber formation
    ↓
Physical trapping of bacteria
    ↓
Bacterial aggregation and neutralization
```

This represents a non-lytic antimicrobial mechanism distinct from pore formation.

### Salt Sensitivity

A major limitation of alpha-defensins:

```
Physiological salt (150 mM NaCl)
    ↓
Reduces electrostatic interactions
    ↓
Decreased antimicrobial activity
    ↓
Limits therapeutic potential
```

**3. Immunomodulatory Applications:**

| Application | Rationale |
|-------------|-----------|
| Vaccine adjuvants | Enhance immune responses |
| Cancer immunotherapy | Modulate tumor microenvironment |
| Wound healing | Beyond antimicrobial effects |

### Development Challenges

| Challenge | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Salt sensitivity** | Reduced activity in physiological conditions |
| **Cost** | Expensive synthesis (multiple disulfides) |
| **Stability** | Proteolytic degradation |
| **Toxicity** | Concentration-dependent cytotoxicity |
| **Delivery** | Systemic vs. local administration |

### No Approved Products

Despite decades of research, no alpha-defensin therapeutics are currently approved. The challenges above explain the gap between promising basic science and clinical translation.

## Summary

Alpha-defensins represent well-characterized components of human innate immunity:

**What We Know:**
- Six human alpha-defensins (HNP-1 to 4, HD-5, HD-6)
- Broad-spectrum antimicrobial activity
- Key role in neutrophil and Paneth cell defense
- Well-defined structures and mechanisms
- Disease associations (IBD, infections, others)
- Genetic variation influences expression

**What We Don't Know:**
- How to overcome salt sensitivity for therapy
- Optimal therapeutic applications
- Long-term safety of exogenous administration
- Whether analogs can improve on natural peptides

**Bottom Line:** Alpha-defensins are scientifically well-established as crucial immune effectors. The evidence for their antimicrobial and immunomodulatory functions is robust and replicated globally. However, therapeutic development has been challenging due to salt sensitivity, cost, and stability issues. Current interest focuses on topical applications and analog development. As biomarkers, alpha-defensins may have clinical utility in inflammatory and infectious diseases.
