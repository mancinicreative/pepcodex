## Overview

<h2 id="overview" class="sr-only">Overview</h2>

### Quick Summary

- **Dual agonist** of GLP-1 and glucagon receptors (GLP-1R/GCGR)
- **NMPA approved** in China (June 2025) for chronic weight management (brand: Xinermei)
- **GLORY-1 trial:** 14.3% weight loss at 48 weeks with excellent tolerability (0.5% discontinuation)
- **DREAMS-3:** First head-to-head trial showing GLP-1/GCGR dual agonist superiority vs semaglutide
- **48% achieved** HbA1c &lt;7% AND ≥10% weight loss vs 21% with semaglutide in T2D patients
- **80% liver fat reduction** in patients with baseline fatty liver (MRI-PDFF ≥10%)

**Evidence Strength: High** — Multiple Phase 3 RCTs, NEJM publication, NMPA approved, head-to-head superiority vs semaglutide.

<h2 id="mechanism-of-action">Mechanism of Action</h2>

### How Mazdutide Works (Plain English)

Mazdutide combines two hormone signals that work together to reduce appetite AND directly improve liver health:

1. **GLP-1 receptor activation** — Like semaglutide, mazdutide activates GLP-1 receptors to reduce appetite, slow stomach emptying, and improve blood sugar control.

2. **Glucagon receptor activation** — This is the key difference. Glucagon tells your liver to burn fat and release stored sugar. By activating glucagon receptors, mazdutide directly increases fat burning in the liver.

3. **Direct liver benefits** — Because glucagon acts directly on the liver, mazdutide shows powerful effects on fatty liver disease. In trials, patients with fatty liver saw 80% reduction in liver fat — far exceeding what GLP-1-only drugs achieve.

4. **Energy expenditure increase** — Glucagon activation increases your body's energy expenditure (calorie burning), contributing to weight loss beyond just eating less.

**Why this matters:** While GLP-1-only drugs like semaglutide primarily work through appetite suppression, mazdutide adds direct metabolic effects through glucagon. This may explain its superior liver benefits and potential for different weight loss patterns.

### The Science: GLP-1 + Glucagon Synergy

```
Mazdutide administration
    ↓
├── GLP-1R ACTIVATION
│   ├── Brain: ↑ Satiety signaling
│   ├── Pancreas: ↑ Insulin secretion (glucose-dependent)
│   └── Stomach: Delayed gastric emptying
│
└── GCGR ACTIVATION
    ├── Liver: ↑ Fatty acid oxidation → ↓ Liver fat
    ├── Liver: ↑ Gluconeogenesis (balanced by insulin)
    ├── Adipose: ↑ Lipolysis → ↑ Free fatty acids for oxidation
    └── Whole body: ↑ Energy expenditure
```

<h2 id="key-human-trials">Key Human Trials</h2>

### GLORY-1 (Obesity/Overweight - China)

| Trial | NCT ID | Population | Duration | N |
|-------|--------|------------|----------|---|
| GLORY-1 | [NCT05607680](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT05607680) | BMI ≥28 or ≥24 with comorbidity | 48 weeks | 610 |

**Key Results (NEJM 2025):**
- **Weight loss:** -14.3% (6mg) vs -3.3% placebo
- **≥10% weight loss:** 64% of participants
- **Discontinuation rate:** 0.5% (exceptionally low)
- **Liver fat (MRI-PDFF ≥10%):** -80.2% vs -5.3% placebo
- **Safety:** GI AEs most common; mild-moderate; dose-escalation period

### DREAMS-3 (Head-to-Head vs Semaglutide)

| Trial | Population | Comparison | Duration |
|-------|------------|------------|----------|
| DREAMS-3 | T2D with obesity | Mazdutide vs Semaglutide | 32 weeks |

**Key Results:**
- **Combined endpoint (HbA1c &lt;7% AND ≥10% WL):** 48% vs 21% (P&lt;0.0001)
- **HbA1c reduction:** Superior to semaglutide
- **Weight loss:** Superior to semaglutide
- **Interpretation:** 2.3x higher success rate for combined glycemic + weight goals

### DREAMS-1 and DREAMS-2 (Type 2 Diabetes)

| Trial | Comparison | Result |
|-------|------------|--------|
| DREAMS-1 | Placebo | Superior HbA1c reduction |

- **Liver safety:** No hepatotoxicity signals; liver fat improved
