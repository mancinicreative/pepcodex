CJC-1295 is a modified version of GHRH (growth hormone-releasing hormone) engineered for extended duration. The modifications confer resistance to enzymatic degradation and enable prolonged receptor activation.

### How It Works (Simplified)

CJC-1295 stimulates growth hormone release through four key mechanisms:

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 not-prose my-8">
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">DPP-IV Resistance</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Four amino acid substitutions at positions 2, 8, 15, and 27 prevent degradation by dipeptidyl peptidase IV, extending half-life from minutes to hours.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Albumin Binding (DAC)</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">The DAC version contains maleimidoproprionic acid that covalently binds to serum albumin, extending half-life to 6-8 days.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">GHRH-R Activation</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Binds to GHRH receptors on pituitary somatotrophs, activating Gαs-adenylyl cyclase-cAMP-PKA signaling cascade.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">GH/IGF-1 Elevation</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Sustained receptor activation produces prolonged GH secretion (2-10 fold for 6+ days) and IGF-1 elevation (9-11 days after single dose).</p>
  </div>
</div>

**Key Research:** Teichman SL et al. (2006) demonstrated 2-10 fold GH elevation for 6+ days and IGF-1 elevation for 9-11 days in Phase 1/2 trials. [PMID:16352683](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16352683/)

### Important Limitations

- Clinical development discontinued in 2006 after participant death (deemed unrelated to drug - pre-existing coronary disease)
- No approved indications in any country
- WADA prohibited substance
- Sustained vs pulsatile GH elevation may have different safety profiles
- Long-term effects of chronic IGF-1 elevation unknown
- Limited human clinical data due to early termination of development program
