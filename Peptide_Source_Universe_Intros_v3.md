# Peptide Source Universe — 300 Credibility Intros (Journals, Trials, Researchers, Societies, Infrastructure)

This document consolidates the **3–4 sentence intros** for each of the **300 items** (journals, trials/programs, researchers, societies/meetings, and infrastructure/databases/tools) exactly as delivered across Batches 1–5.

## Batch 1 (1–60)

### Journals (1–40)

1) **Nature**  
A flagship interdisciplinary science journal publishing major advances across biology, chemistry, and medicine. Peptide work here is usually “big leap” material (new modalities, mechanisms, structural biology, or translational breakthroughs). It’s credible because it’s highly selective, heavily peer-reviewed, and sets research agendas across disciplines.

2) **Science**  
A top general science journal known for publishing foundational discoveries and high-impact translational work. Peptide-related papers often involve novel bioactive peptides, mechanisms, or therapeutic platforms with broad relevance. Credibility comes from extreme selectivity, strong editorial standards, and high replication scrutiny from the community.

3) **Cell**  
A premier life-science journal focused on mechanistic biology and systems-level insights. Peptide topics commonly show up as signaling molecules, receptor biology, or therapeutic biology with deep molecular detail. It’s trusted for rigorous peer review and a strong track record of landmark cellular and molecular studies.

4) **New England Journal of Medicine (NEJM)**  
A leading clinical journal that publishes practice-changing evidence in medicine. Peptide therapeutics appear mainly through large clinical trials, guideline-shifting outcomes, and major safety/efficacy reports. Credibility is built on strict trial standards, high editorial scrutiny, and outsized influence on clinical practice.

5) **The Lancet**  
A globally influential clinical journal covering trials, public health, and major disease areas. Peptide interventions show up as high-impact clinical evidence and large international studies. It’s credible due to tight peer review, strong clinical relevance filters, and global reach across medicine.

6) **JAMA**  
A top clinical journal publishing major trials, guidelines, and evidence syntheses. Peptide-related content typically involves endocrine/metabolic drugs, cardiometabolic outcomes, or clinically relevant mechanisms. Credibility comes from stringent editorial standards, emphasis on methodology, and clinical translation.

7) **Nature Medicine**  
A high-impact journal sitting at the intersection of biology and clinical translation. Peptide papers here often include therapeutic innovation, human data, and disease-mechanism work that is close to patient impact. It’s credible due to selectivity and a strong bar for translational rigor.

8) **Nature Biotechnology**  
A leading journal for biotech innovation: therapeutics, platforms, engineering, and drug development. Peptide research here is often platform-driven (delivery tech, conjugates, discovery pipelines, engineering). Credibility comes from specialized peer review and emphasis on technical novelty + real-world impact.

9) **Nature Chemical Biology**  
A top venue for chemical biology—how molecules control biology. Peptide work often includes peptide probes, peptide-target interactions, biosynthesis, or mechanism-first therapeutic insights. It’s credible because it demands both chemical rigor and biological validation.

10) **Nature Chemistry**  
A premier chemistry journal highlighting major advances in chemical science. Peptide content is usually cutting-edge synthesis, macrocycles, ligation, or novel chemical strategies that enable peptide function. Credibility comes from strong peer review and high novelty thresholds.

11) **Science Translational Medicine**  
A translational journal bridging lab discovery and clinical application. Peptide papers often tie mechanism → animal data → early human signals, especially in therapeutics and diagnostics. Credible because it prioritizes clinical relevance, rigorous methodology, and reproducible translational logic.

12) **Nature Reviews Drug Discovery**  
A review journal that synthesizes the most important themes in drug discovery and development. Peptides show up as modality trends (e.g., delivery, stability, selectivity, targets) rather than single experiments. Credibility comes from expert authorship, heavy editorial curation, and high standards for balanced synthesis.

13) **Chemical Reviews**  
A top-tier review journal in chemistry that produces definitive, highly cited overviews. Peptide topics include synthesis methods, macrocycles, conjugation chemistry, and mechanistic/structural themes. It’s credible because reviews are exhaustive, technical, and written by leading experts.

14) **Journal of the American Chemical Society (JACS)**  
A leading chemistry journal for major advances across chemical disciplines. Peptide work here often involves new synthesis, ligation chemistry, conformational control, or bio-orthogonal methods. Credibility comes from rigorous peer review and consistently high technical standards.

15) **Angewandte Chemie International Edition**  
A highly selective chemistry journal emphasizing novelty and broad chemical impact. Peptide publications frequently involve advanced synthesis, supramolecular peptide materials, or new chemical biology tools. It’s credible due to stringent review and strong reputation across global chemistry.

16) **Journal of Medicinal Chemistry**  
A core medicinal chemistry journal focused on turning molecules into drug candidates. Peptide content often includes SAR optimization, stability/PK strategies, target engagement, and early efficacy. Credibility comes from deep domain peer review and strong expectations around data completeness.

17) **ACS Chemical Biology**  
A chemical biology journal emphasizing molecular tools and mechanistic experiments. Peptides appear as probes, inhibitors, engineered ligands, or delivery agents that reveal biology. Credible because it demands chemistry + functional biology evidence, not just one or the other.

18) **Bioconjugate Chemistry**  
A specialty journal for linking biomolecules to drugs, imaging agents, and delivery systems. Peptides are central here—especially peptide-drug conjugates, labeling, targeting ligands, and linker chemistry. It’s credible because it’s highly technical and reviewed by experts in conjugation and biotherapeutic chemistry.

19) **Molecular Pharmaceutics**  
A pharmaceutics journal focused on formulation, delivery, and drug behavior in biological systems. Peptide work often covers stability, permeability, delivery vehicles, and PK/PD strategies. Credibility comes from strong methodological expectations and relevance to real drug development problems.

20) **Advanced Drug Delivery Reviews**  
A high-impact review journal specializing in drug delivery science. Peptides show up in delivery challenges (oral, depot, nanoparticles), conjugates, and next-gen modalities. It’s credible because reviews are comprehensive, authored by specialists, and widely used in R&D planning.

21) **Journal of Controlled Release**  
A leading journal dedicated to controlled release and delivery technologies. Peptide studies commonly involve carriers, sustained release systems, targeted delivery, and pharmacokinetic improvements. It’s credible due to deep specialization and demanding experimental validation for delivery claims.

22) **Biomaterials**  
A flagship biomaterials journal covering materials for medical/biological applications. Peptide work often includes self-assembling peptides, peptide hydrogels, bioactive coatings, and tissue engineering. Credibility comes from high selectivity and strong experimental standards.

23) **PNAS**  
A broad, highly respected journal publishing important work across sciences. Peptide research can range from structural biology to immunology to therapeutic concepts with strong mechanistic depth. Credibility is reinforced by rigorous review and broad scientific scrutiny.

24) **Diabetes Care**  
A top clinical journal focused on diabetes and its complications. Peptide therapeutics show up often via GLP-1 class clinical evidence, real-world outcomes, and management guidelines. It’s credible because it’s disease-focused, clinically grounded, and methodologically strict.

25) **Diabetologia**  
A leading diabetes research journal spanning clinical and basic metabolic science. Peptide content often includes incretin biology, islet physiology, and translational metabolic therapeutics. Credibility comes from strong specialization and respected peer review in endocrinology/metabolism.

26) **Circulation**  
A premier cardiology journal covering high-impact clinical and translational cardiovascular research. Peptide-relevant content includes cardiometabolic outcomes trials and mechanistic work tied to vascular biology. Credible due to strict peer review and high influence in cardiology practice and science.

27) **European Heart Journal**  
A top cardiology journal with major trials, guidelines, and mechanistic CV science. Peptide drugs appear through cardiometabolic crossover (diabetes/obesity → CV outcomes) and thrombosis/vascular biology. Credibility comes from high standards and strong clinical relevance filters.

28) **Journal of the American College of Cardiology (JACC)**  
A leading cardiology journal publishing major trials and expert consensus. Peptide-related topics often involve metabolic therapies with CV endpoints and antithrombotic agents. Credibility is driven by tight editorial standards and prominence in cardiovascular medicine.

29) **The Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism**  
A core endocrinology journal spanning clinical and translational endocrine science. Peptides appear constantly (hormonal peptides, receptor agonists/antagonists, pituitary/endocrine therapeutics). It’s credible due to strong society roots and consistent methodological rigor.

30) **Endocrine Reviews**  
A high-impact review journal synthesizing endocrine research and clinical translation. It’s especially relevant for peptide hormones and peptide therapeutics because it consolidates mechanisms, clinical evidence, and controversies. Credible because reviews are expert-led, deeply referenced, and editorially curated.

31) **Trends in Endocrinology & Metabolism**  
A “Trends” review journal highlighting key emerging ideas in endocrine/metabolic science. Peptide content often focuses on incretins, appetite pathways, and endocrine signaling breakthroughs. Credibility comes from expert reviews and editorial selection of high-signal topics.

32) **Clinical Pharmacology & Therapeutics**  
A top journal for pharmacology in humans—PK/PD, dose-response, safety, and translational pharmacology. Peptides often appear through exposure–response modeling, clinical pharmacology of biologics, and therapeutic optimization. Credible because it prioritizes methodological precision and clinical interpretability.

33) **British Journal of Pharmacology**  
A major pharmacology journal covering mechanisms, targets, and therapeutic modulation. Peptide studies often involve receptor signaling, agonist bias, and experimental pharmacology. Credible due to long-standing reputation, specialist peer review, and methodological expectations in pharmacology.

34) **Journal of Pharmacology and Experimental Therapeutics**  
A classic pharmacology journal focusing on rigorous experimental pharmacology and mechanism. Peptide research here often includes receptor biology, signaling pathways, and preclinical pharmacodynamics. Credible because it emphasizes experimental design, controls, and mechanistic clarity.

35) **Journal of Biological Chemistry (JBC)**  
A long-running biochemistry journal publishing foundational molecular biology and biochemistry. Peptide-relevant work includes protein/peptide interactions, enzymology, signaling peptides, and mechanistic cellular pathways. Credible because of deep technical review culture and broad community use.

36) **Protein Science**  
A respected journal focused on protein structure, folding, design, and function. Peptide-adjacent work appears through structural motifs, folding principles, and engineered peptide-like scaffolds. Credible due to specialization in structure/function and strong technical expectations.

37) **Amino Acids**  
A specialty journal centered on amino acids, peptides, and related metabolic/biological roles. Peptide content includes synthesis, biology, nutrition/metabolism angles, and peptide signaling. Credibility comes from niche focus and consistent peer review within the peptide/amino-acid community.

38) **Peptides**  
A dedicated journal for peptide biology, chemistry, and pharmacology. Studies commonly cover neuropeptides, endocrine peptides, peptide receptors, and peptide-based therapeutics. Credible because it’s field-specific, with reviewers who understand peptide-specific pitfalls and standards.

39) **Journal of Peptide Science**  
A specialist journal emphasizing peptide chemistry, synthesis methods, and peptide applications. It frequently features methodological advances, structure–activity work, and new peptide scaffolds. Credibility is driven by field focus and technical peer review from peptide chemists.

40) **International Journal of Peptide Research and Therapeutics**  
A peptide-focused journal spanning peptide discovery, design, synthesis, and therapeutic development. Peptide studies here can include engineering, delivery, activity profiling, and translational angles. Credibility comes from topical specialization; it’s most useful for peptide-specific breadth rather than only “mega-journal” prestige.

### Infrastructure / Databases / Tools (41–50)

41) **UniProt**  
A core protein knowledgebase providing curated protein sequences and functional annotations. For peptide work, it’s crucial for precursor proteins, cleavage products, and mapping peptides to biology. Credible because it’s widely used, curated, and foundational to modern bioinformatics workflows.

42) **PeptideAtlas**  
A proteomics resource that aggregates peptide identifications from mass spectrometry datasets. It helps validate whether specific peptides are observed in real biological samples and under what conditions. Credible because it’s data-backed, community-used, and built around reproducible proteomics evidence.

43) **Immune Epitope Database (IEDB)**  
A major database of immune epitopes (including many peptide epitopes) with experimental metadata. It’s used for vaccine research, T-cell/B-cell epitope mapping, and immunogenicity assessment. Credible because it’s widely adopted in immunology and grounded in experimentally sourced entries.

44) **Protein Data Bank (PDB)**  
The canonical archive for 3D structures of proteins, peptides, and complexes. It’s essential for peptide–target binding analysis, structural mechanism, and rational design. Credible because entries are standardized, validated, and form the backbone of structural biology.

45) **AlphaFold Protein Structure Database**  
A large database of predicted protein structures enabling rapid structural hypotheses. For peptides, it’s most useful for understanding target protein conformations and potential binding regions (with caution). Credible as a best-in-class prediction resource, but you still treat it as “model” until experimentally confirmed.

46) **ClinicalTrials.gov**  
A primary registry of clinical studies, including peptide therapeutics and peptide-adjacent programs. It’s used to verify trial existence, endpoints, populations, and status—often before publications appear. Credible because it’s a major official registry and a key source for program timelines.

47) **PubMed**  
A central index/search engine for biomedical literature. It’s used to trace peptide evidence from early mechanistic papers to human trials and reviews. Credible because it aggregates peer-reviewed biomedical publishing and supports reliable literature discovery.

48) **PubChem**  
A chemical information database for compounds, including peptide-like molecules and related bioactivity links. For peptides, it can help with identifiers, cross-links, and chemistry metadata when available. Credible because it’s a major public resource maintained with standardized records.

49) **ChEMBL**  
A bioactivity database that links molecules to targets and assay results. It’s valuable for peptides and peptide mimetics when activity data is curated from publications. Credible because it’s heavily used in drug discovery and built around structured extraction of experimental data.

50) **DrugBank**  
A drug database combining drug data with targets, mechanisms, and interaction information. For peptide therapeutics, it’s useful as a “quick identity and context layer” (then you verify via primary literature). Credible because it’s widely referenced and curated, though depth varies by compound.

60) **European Peptide Symposium**

Credible due to continuity, established scientific committees, and strong participation from top peptide labs.


## Batch 2 (61–120)

61) **BindingDB**  
A public database focused on measured binding affinities between biomolecules and ligands, including many peptide–target interactions. It’s useful for sanity-checking whether a peptide (or peptide-like ligand) has reported binding data and what targets are most supported. Credible because it’s built around experimentally reported measurements and is widely used in drug discovery workflows.

62) **Guide to PHARMACOLOGY (IUPHAR/BPS)**  
A curated pharmacology resource that links targets (receptors, channels, enzymes) to ligands and key literature. For peptide work, it’s especially strong on GPCR ligands, peptide hormones, and signaling pathways. Credible because it’s expert-curated by pharmacologists and tied to a standards-driven community (IUPHAR/BPS).

63) **PRIDE Archive (Proteomics Identifications Database)**  
A major repository for mass spectrometry proteomics datasets. It helps validate peptide identifications by letting you inspect raw or processed evidence and reanalyze findings. Credible because it’s a long-running, widely adopted archive that supports reproducibility and data transparency.

64) **ProteomeXchange Consortium**  
A global consortium that standardizes how proteomics datasets are submitted, accessed, and referenced across repositories. It matters for peptide discovery because it makes peptide/proteome findings more findable and comparable across studies. Credible because it’s a community infrastructure initiative designed around reproducibility and shared standards.

65) **MassIVE (UCSD MS data repo)**  
A large mass spectrometry data repository used heavily by proteomics researchers. For peptide intelligence, it’s valuable when you want to trace evidence behind peptide identifications or discover peptides present in specific sample types. Credible because it hosts primary experimental data and supports reanalysis at scale.

66) **SwissModel**  
A well-known platform for homology-based protein structure modeling. It’s helpful in peptide work when you need approximate 3D structures of targets (or domains) to reason about binding interfaces. Credible because it’s been used for years in structural biology and follows established modeling principles (with clear limitations vs experimental structures).

67) **EMBL-EBI InterPro**  
A database that integrates protein families, domains, and functional sites from multiple sources. For peptide research, it’s often used to understand the domains a peptide might bind to or the architecture of peptide precursors. Credible because it’s maintained by a major bioinformatics institute and merges many validated annotation pipelines.

68) **NCBI BLAST**  
A foundational tool for sequence similarity searching across biological databases. In peptide discovery, it helps you find homologs of peptide precursors, conserved motifs, and cross-species evidence. Credible because it’s a standard bioinformatics method used across nearly all of molecular biology.

69) **RCSB PDB tools portal**  
A major access point and tool suite for exploring protein/peptide structures in the Protein Data Bank. It’s useful for analyzing binding sites, peptide–target complexes, and structural constraints for design. Credible because it sits on top of the most established structural archive in biology.

70) **PDBe (Protein Data Bank Europe)**  
A European portal to PDB data with strong tooling for structure interpretation and integration. For peptides, PDBe helps you quickly assess complex geometry, ligand annotations, and experimental context. Credible because it’s part of the official global PDB ecosystem and emphasizes validated structural metadata.

71) **PDBj (Protein Data Bank Japan)**  
Japan’s PDB partner site, providing access and tools for structural biology data. For peptide work, it’s another authoritative route to validated structures, including peptide ligands and peptide–protein complexes. Credible because it’s an official PDB consortium member with shared standards.

72) **BMRB (Biological Magnetic Resonance Bank)**  
A repository for biomolecular NMR data, which is especially relevant when structure or dynamics are studied beyond static crystal snapshots. For peptides, NMR can be central to understanding folding, conformational ensembles, and binding in solution. Credible because it archives primary experimental data supporting published structural claims.

73) **GlyTouCan**  
A global glycan structure repository. It becomes peptide-relevant when you’re dealing with glycopeptides, peptide hormones with glycosylation, or biologics where sugar modifications affect function and stability. Credible because it’s part of a standardized ecosystem for representing and referencing glycan structures.

74) **KEGG**  
A widely used database for biological pathways, diseases, and molecular interactions. For peptide dossiers, it helps connect targets and signaling pathways (e.g., endocrine or immune pathways) to broader systems biology. Credible because it’s deeply integrated into academic and industry analysis workflows.

75) **Reactome**  
An expert-curated pathway database that maps molecular events and signaling cascades. It’s valuable for peptide mechanisms because peptide therapeutics often act through well-defined receptor pathways, and Reactome helps keep that map coherent. Credible due to its curated, literature-backed pathway annotations.

76) **European Peptide Synthesis Conference**  
A specialized meeting series focused on peptide synthesis methods, scale-up challenges, and emerging chemistry. It’s where practical innovations (ligation, protecting group strategies, purification, process improvements) often surface early. Credible because it’s niche, technical, and attended by experts who build the methods others rely on.

77) **Gordon Research Conference (GRC) – Proteins / Peptides-related meetings**  
GRCs are known for high-trust, high-signal meetings with unpublished or early-stage results discussed under a strong scientific culture. Peptide-adjacent GRCs are great for seeing what’s coming next in mechanism, modality design, and enabling tech. Credible because participation is selective and the discussions are anchored by leading researchers.

78) **Gordon Research Seminar (GRS) – early-career peptide-adjacent tracks**  
GRSs are companion events to GRCs centered on trainees and early-career scientists. They’re useful for spotting emerging labs, fresh methods, and new peptide concepts before they become mainstream. Credible because they’re embedded in the GRC ecosystem and still maintain strong scientific oversight.

79) **HUPO (Human Proteome Organization)**  
An international organization driving proteomics standards, collaboration, and technology dissemination. It matters for peptides because proteomics is one of the main engines for discovering endogenous peptides and validating peptide biomarkers. Credible because it shapes field-wide best practices and convenes top proteomics groups.

80) **ASMS (Mass Spectrometry Society)**  
A major professional society and conference hub for mass spectrometry—core to modern peptide discovery and identification. Many peptide analytics breakthroughs (LC-MS methods, fragmentation strategies, quantitative workflows) spread rapidly through this community. Credible because it’s a central node for technical rigor in measurement science.

81) **ESCMID (clinical microbiology; AMP-adjacent sessions)**  
A leading European society for clinical microbiology and infectious disease. Antimicrobial peptides (AMPs) and host-defense peptides often intersect here via resistance, translational antimicrobial strategies, and clinical feasibility discussions. Credible because it’s clinically anchored and evidence-first in infectious disease.

82) **IUPAC (nomenclature/standards touching peptides)**  
The global authority on chemical nomenclature and standards. It’s relevant to peptide dossiers when you need consistent naming, representation, and standards for describing peptide chemistry and modifications. Credible because it sets international rules that keep scientific communication interoperable.

83) **European Federation of Endocrine Societies collaborations (peptide hormones)**  
A broad collaborative ecosystem connecting endocrine societies across Europe. Since endocrinology is peptide-heavy (hormones, receptor ligands, metabolic signaling), this network is influential for translating peptide biology into clinical thinking. Credible because it’s tied to established societies and clinical research communities.

84) **European Society for Medical Oncology (peptide-drug conjugates/PRRT tracks)**  
A top oncology society that disseminates clinical evidence and practice standards. Peptide-related oncology shows up via radiolabeled peptides (e.g., PRRT), targeting ligands, and peptide-based diagnostics/therapeutics. Credible because it’s guideline-adjacent, trial-driven, and clinically conservative.

85) **International Society for Neurochemistry (neuropeptides)**  
A global society focused on brain chemistry and molecular neuroscience. Neuropeptides are central to many neural signaling and behavior pathways, so this community is a strong “signal amplifier” for peptide neuroscience. Credible because it convenes serious mechanistic neuroscience with high methodological expectations.

86) **International Union of Basic and Clinical Pharmacology (IUPHAR)**  
An international pharmacology organization that promotes standards for targets, ligands, and pharmacological nomenclature. It’s particularly relevant for peptide receptors (especially GPCRs) and consistent interpretation of agonism/antagonism, bias, and target classification. Credible because it acts as a standards body for the discipline.

87) **Society for Endocrinology (peptide hormone science)**  
A key professional society supporting endocrine research and clinical translation. Peptide hormones and peptide therapeutics are central to endocrine systems, so this society’s ecosystem often reflects what’s clinically meaningful and mechanistically credible. Credible because it sits close to both clinical practice and basic endocrine science.

88) **European Association for the Study of Diabetes (incretins)**  
A major diabetes organization where incretin and metabolic-therapy evidence is heavily discussed and disseminated. Peptide therapeutics in diabetes/obesity often pass through this community as results mature and guidelines evolve. Credible because it’s evidence-driven, clinically connected, and focused on real-world disease impact.

89) **International Society for Computational Biology (peptide modeling)**  
A large computational biology society spanning sequence analysis, structural modeling, and systems biology. Peptide discovery increasingly uses ML and computational pipelines for target prediction, epitope mapping, and design—this community is where those methods mature. Credible because it aggregates peer-reviewed computational rigor and reproducible method culture.

90) **European Bioinformatics Institute community events (peptide/proteomics tooling)**  
A recurring ecosystem of workshops and community events around core bioinformatics resources. For peptides, it often intersects with proteomics data standards, structure resources, annotation pipelines, and reproducibility. Credible because it’s tied to major open science infrastructure and emphasizes method transparency.

91) **ACS Medicinal Chemistry Letters**  
A rapid-publication medicinal chemistry journal emphasizing timely drug discovery findings. Peptide-relevant work often includes optimized peptide ligands, peptide mimetics, and early-stage therapeutic concepts with clear SAR. Credible because it’s ACS-backed and reviewed by medicinal chemistry specialists.

92) **Organic Letters**  
A high-impact journal for concise reports of new organic chemistry methods and syntheses. Peptide-related work appears when new reactions, ligations, or macrocyclizations enable peptide design or modification. Credible because it’s selective and focuses on reproducible, chemistry-forward advances.

93) **Accounts of Chemical Research**  
A prestigious review-style journal where leaders summarize a cohesive research program. Peptide accounts often cover platforms (stapling, macrocycles, conjugation strategies, discovery technologies) with conceptual clarity. Credible because authors are typically recognized experts and the articles are heavily curated.

94) **Analytical Chemistry**  
A leading journal for measurement science and analytical methods. Peptide work here often includes LC-MS innovations, quantitative proteomics workflows, and detection strategies critical for validating peptide presence and behavior. Credible because it’s method-rigorous and demands strong validation and reproducibility.

95) **Biomacromolecules**  
A journal focused on polymers and biomacromolecular materials, including peptide-based materials. Peptide studies commonly include self-assembly, biomaterials performance, and bioactive scaffolds. Credible because it sits at a strong intersection of chemistry + materials + biology with solid experimental expectations.

96) **Journal of Proteome Research**  
A key proteomics journal emphasizing discovery, quantification, and interpretation of proteins and peptides. It’s especially useful for endogenous peptide discovery and biomarker validation where methods must be robust. Credible because it’s discipline-specific and consistently methodological.

97) **Molecular & Cellular Proteomics**  
A high-signal proteomics journal with a strong emphasis on biological interpretation and data quality. Peptide work often comes through advanced MS workflows, pathway integration, and rigorous quantitative designs. Credible due to strong standards around data reporting and reproducibility.

98) **Journal of Proteomics**  
A broad proteomics journal covering methods, applications, and biological discoveries. It’s relevant for peptide dossiers when you want sample-specific peptide evidence (tissues, fluids, disease states). Credible because it’s focused on the proteomics evidence pipeline and peer-reviewed by specialists.

99) **Proteomics**  
A long-standing journal in the proteomics field. Peptide findings often appear as workflow improvements, quantitative strategies, and discovery studies tied to peptide/protein identification. Credible because it’s focused on the proteomics evidence pipeline and peer-reviewed by specialists.

100) **Journal of Mass Spectrometry**

101) **Rapid Communications in Mass Spectrometry**

Peptide papers often include optimization, stability strategies, and target-driven activity profiling. Credible due to its drug-discovery focus and expectations for chemical + biological data together.

105) **European Journal of Medicinal Chemistry**

106) **ChemMedChem**

Peptide work often appears as modality engineering, target engagement strategies, and early pharmacology that bridges chemistry to medicine. Credible because it maintains a clear drug-development lens with specialist review.
