## Overview

<h2 id="overview" class="sr-only">Overview</h2>

### Quick Summary

- **Synthetic heptapeptide** (7 amino acids) derived from tuftsin, an endogenous immunomodulatory tetrapeptide
- **Approved in Russia since 2009** for generalized anxiety disorder and neurasthenia (nasal spray formulation)
- **Not approved** by FDA, EMA, or other Western regulatory agencies
- **Dual mechanism:** Anxiolytic effects via GABA/serotonin modulation plus immunomodulatory activity from tuftsin backbone
- **Unique profile:** Non-sedating anxiolytic without cognitive impairment, muscle relaxation, or dependence potential
- **Developed by:** Institute of Molecular Genetics, Russian Academy of Sciences

**Evidence Strength: Moderate** - Extensive Russian clinical trials (including RCTs) and over 15 years of post-marketing experience in Russia. However, most research originates from Russian institutions with limited independent Western replication.

### Research Timeline

- **1970s:** Tuftsin discovered as immunostimulatory peptide from IgG heavy chain
- **1990s:** Selank developed at Institute of Molecular Genetics, Russian Academy of Sciences
- **1998:** First major preclinical publication demonstrating anxiolytic and nootropic effects
- **2001-2008:** Multiple clinical trials conducted in Russia
- **2009:** Approved in Russia as 0.15% nasal spray for anxiety disorders
- **2009-present:** Ongoing post-marketing surveillance and continued research
- **Current:** Available as prescription medication in Russia; research chemical elsewhere

<h2 id="mechanism-of-action">Mechanism of Action</h2>

Selank has a complex, multi-target mechanism of action that distinguishes it from traditional anxiolytics. Its effects arise from modulation of multiple neurotransmitter systems and immune function.

### How Selank Works

Think of Selank as a "balance restorer" that works through several complementary pathways:

1. **Enhances GABA signaling** - GABA is your brain's main "calm down" chemical. Selank increases GABA activity, but unlike benzodiazepines, it does this by modulating gene expression of GABA receptor subunits rather than directly binding to receptors. This produces calming effects without sedation or dependence.

2. **Balances serotonin** - Serotonin regulates mood, anxiety, and well-being. Selank modulates serotonin receptor expression (particularly 5-HT1A) and metabolism, contributing to mood stabilization and anxiolytic effects.

3. **Protects enkephalins** - Enkephalins are your body's natural "feel-good" opioids. Selank inhibits the enzymes that break down enkephalins, allowing these natural painkillers and mood regulators to work longer.

4. **Boosts BDNF** - Brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) is like fertilizer for your neurons. Selank increases BDNF expression in the hippocampus and frontal cortex, supporting learning, memory, and stress resilience.

5. **Modulates immunity** - From its tuftsin backbone, Selank retains immunomodulatory activity, balancing immune responses and demonstrating antiviral properties.

**Why this matters:** Unlike benzodiazepines (which directly enhance GABA receptor activity and cause sedation, tolerance, and dependence), Selank works at the gene expression level, producing more subtle, balanced effects without these drawbacks.

### Scientific Pathways

**1. GABAergic System Modulation**
```
Selank → Gene expression changes
    ↓
GABA-A receptor subunit modulation (alpha, beta, gamma)
    ↓
├── Enhanced GABAergic neurotransmission
├── Reduced neuronal excitability
└── Anxiolytic effect (without benzodiazepine-like sedation)

Key difference from benzodiazepines:
- Benzodiazepines: Direct receptor binding → rapid tolerance/dependence
- Selank: Gene expression modulation → sustained effect without tolerance
```

Selank modulates expression of genes encoding GABA-A receptor subunits and enzymes involved in GABA synthesis (GAD) and degradation (GABA-T). This produces anxiolytic effects through enhanced inhibitory neurotransmission without the rapid tolerance development seen with benzodiazepines.

**2. Serotonergic System**
```
Selank → Hippocampal gene expression
    ↓
├── 5-HT1A receptor modulation (anxiolytic, antidepressant)
├── 5-HT2A receptor modulation (mood regulation)
└── Serotonin metabolism normalization
    ↓
Balanced serotonergic tone → mood stability
```

The 5-HT1A receptor is a key target for anxiolytic drugs. Selank modulates its expression, contributing to anxiety reduction. This mechanism is similar to buspirone but operates at the transcriptional level.

**3. Enkephalinergic System**
```
Enkephalin-degrading enzymes (aminopeptidases)
    ↓ (Selank inhibition)
Increased endogenous enkephalin levels
    ↓
├── Mu and delta opioid receptor activation
├── Anxiolytic and mood-elevating effects
└── Natural "well-being" enhancement
```

Selank and its parent peptide tuftsin inhibit enkephalin-degrading enzymes, leading to elevated enkephalin levels. This provides a natural opioid system boost without the risks of exogenous opioids.

**4. Neurotrophic Effects**
```
Selank → Frontal cortex & hippocampus
    ↓
BDNF mRNA upregulation
    ↓
├── Neuroplasticity enhancement
├── Memory consolidation improvement
├── Stress resilience
└── Potential antidepressant effects
```

BDNF is critical for learning, memory, and neuronal survival. Low BDNF is associated with depression and anxiety disorders. Selank's ability to increase BDNF provides a mechanism for both cognitive enhancement and mood benefits.

**5. Dopaminergic System**
```
Selank → Frontal cortex & striatum
    ↓
├── Dopamine receptor gene modulation (D1, D2)
├── Dopamine metabolism normalization
└── Catecholamine balance restoration
    ↓
Improved attention and cognitive function under stress
```

Selank normalizes disturbed dopamine metabolism in anxiety states without affecting baseline dopamine function, contributing to maintained cognitive performance.

### Evidence Highlights

1. **Russian Clinical Trials:** Multiple double-blind, placebo-controlled trials demonstrating efficacy for generalized anxiety disorder
2. **Regulatory Approval:** Approved prescription medication in Russia since 2009 with post-marketing surveillance data
3. **Comparative Studies:** Shown comparable to diazepam and buspirone for anxiolysis without their side effects
4. **Mechanistic Research:** Well-characterized molecular mechanisms through gene expression studies
5. **Safety Profile:** Favorable safety in clinical trials and post-marketing experience

### Key Strengths of Evidence

- **Regulatory approval** in a major country (Russia) with ongoing surveillance
- **Controlled trials** with adequate sample sizes
- **Consistent mechanistic data** across multiple research groups
- **Long post-marketing experience** (15+ years)

### Key Limitations of Evidence

- **Geographic concentration:** Most research from Russian institutions
- **Limited Western replication:** Few independent studies outside Russia
- **Language barrier:** Many studies published only in Russian
- **No FDA/EMA trials:** Western regulatory pathway not pursued
- **Publication bias:** Negative Russian studies may be underreported

### Post-Marketing Experience

Since Russian approval in 2009, Selank has accumulated significant real-world usage data:

- **Indication:** Generalized anxiety disorder, neurasthenia, cognitive impairment
- **Formulation:** 0.15% nasal spray (Selanx)
- **Typical course:** 14-day treatment periods
- **Adverse event profile:** Favorable; no serious safety signals reported
- **No withdrawal syndrome:** Unlike benzodiazepines

### Advantages of Selank vs. Tuftsin

The C-terminal Pro-Gly-Pro extension provides:

1. **Increased stability:** Resistance to proteolytic degradation
2. **CNS penetration:** Enhanced ability to cross blood-brain barrier
3. **Anxiolytic activity:** Added GABA modulation not present in tuftsin alone
4. **Nootropic effects:** Cognitive enhancement beyond immunomodulation

- Hypersensitivity to peptide components
- Pregnancy and lactation (insufficient data)
- Severe hepatic or renal impairment (caution advised)

### Known Parameters

| **Active metabolites** | Yes | Glyprolines (Pro-Gly-Pro) retain activity |
| **Bioavailability (IN)** | ~90% | Excellent intranasal absorption |
| **CNS penetration** | Yes | Crosses blood-brain barrier |
| **Metabolism** | Proteolytic cleavage | Sequential degradation to active fragments |
| **Onset of action** | Hours to days | Depends on gene expression changes |
| **Duration of effect** | Outlasts plasma presence | Gene expression changes persist |

### Metabolic Pathway

```
Selank (Thr-Lys-Pro-Arg-Pro-Gly-Pro)
    ↓ (aminopeptidases)
Tuftsin fragment (Thr-Lys-Pro-Arg) + Glyprolines (Pro-Gly-Pro)
    ↓
├── Tuftsin → Immunomodulatory activity
├── Glyprolines → CNS activity, neuroprotection
└── Further degradation to amino acids
```

### Clinical Implications

The rapid metabolism to active fragments means:

1. **Gene expression mechanism:** Effects depend on transcriptional changes, not sustained plasma levels
2. **No accumulation:** Low risk of build-up with repeated dosing
3. **Nasal delivery optimal:** Rapid absorption, bypasses first-pass metabolism
4. **Multiple active species:** Both parent and metabolites contribute to effects
