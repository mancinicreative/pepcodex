## Overview

### Quick Summary

- **Mitochondria-derived peptide (MDP)** — discovered 2016 by Cohen laboratory
- **26 amino acids** in length, encoded in mitochondrial 16S rRNA region
- **Sister peptide to humanin** — from same genomic region but distinct sequence
- **Not approved** by any regulatory agency
- **Key effects (preclinical):** Cytoprotection, metabolic regulation, chaperone-like activity
- **Status:** Early research phase with limited data

**Evidence Strength: Low** — SHLP-2 was discovered in 2016 and has limited published research compared to humanin or MOTS-c. Promising preclinical signals but minimal human data beyond biomarker correlations.

### The SHLP Family

SHLP-2 is part of a family of six small humanin-like peptides:

| Peptide | Size | Key Feature |
|---------|------|-------------|
| SHLP-1 | 20 aa | Least characterized |
| **SHLP-2** | 26 aa | Most potent/studied |
| SHLP-3 | 21 aa | Mitochondrial effects |
| SHLP-4 | 18 aa | Metabolic effects |
| SHLP-5 | 14 aa | Least characterized |
| SHLP-6 | 22 aa | Pro-apoptotic (unlike others) |

### Research Timeline

- **2001:** Humanin discovered as first MDP
- **2015:** MOTS-c discovered as metabolic MDP
- **2016:** SHLP family (1-6) discovered by Cobb et al. at USC
- **2016-present:** Characterization studies ongoing
- **Current:** Early research phase; no clinical development

## Summary

SHLP-2 represents an early-stage research peptide in the emerging MDP field:

**What We Know:**
- 26-amino acid peptide from mitochondrial 16S rRNA region
- Discovered 2016 as part of SHLP family
- Shows cytoprotective and metabolic effects in preclinical models
- Part of growing understanding of mitochondrial-derived signaling

**What We Don't Know:**
- Molecular mechanism (no defined receptor)
- In vivo pharmacokinetics
- Safety profile
- Therapeutic potential in humans
- Whether effects translate to clinical benefit

**Bottom Line:** SHLP-2 is scientifically interesting as part of the expanding MDP field that humanin opened. The discovery of six SHLPs from the same genomic region as humanin suggests significant biological signaling complexity from mitochondria. However, SHLP-2 specifically has minimal research compared to humanin or MOTS-c. Any claims of therapeutic benefit are highly speculative at this stage. The evidence strength is "low" reflecting genuine scientific interest but insufficient data for clinical application.
