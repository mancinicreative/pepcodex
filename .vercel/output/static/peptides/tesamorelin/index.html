Tesamorelin

Also known as: Egrifta, Egrifta SV, Egrifta WR, TH9507

An FDA-approved GHRH analog for reducing visceral fat in HIV-associated lipodystrophy. One of few GH-releasing peptides with regulatory approval and Phase 3 data.

Overview

Overview

Quick Summary

  • Synthetic GHRH analog with demonstrated clinical efficacy
  • FDA approved (2010) for HIV-associated lipodystrophy (visceral adiposity)
  • Phase 3 evidence supporting efficacy and safety
  • Daily subcutaneous injection required
  • Reduces visceral fat without significant effect on subcutaneous fat

Evidence Strength: High — FDA approval based on Phase 3 trials demonstrating efficacy in reducing visceral adipose tissue.

Mechanism of Action

How Tesamorelin Works

Tesamorelin is a modified version of GHRH (the hormone your brain uses to trigger growth hormone release). It’s one of the only GH-releasing peptides with actual FDA approval. Here’s what makes it unique:

  1. Targets belly fat specifically - Tesamorelin was approved because it reduces visceral fat (the dangerous fat around your organs) without significantly affecting subcutaneous fat (the fat under your skin). Visceral fat is more sensitive to growth hormone’s fat-burning effects.

  2. Why it was developed for HIV patients - Many HIV patients on antiretroviral therapy develop lipodystrophy (abnormal fat distribution), with fat accumulating in their abdomen. This is linked to reduced GH secretion. Tesamorelin restores more normal GH patterns.

  3. Maintains natural GH rhythm - Unlike injecting growth hormone directly, tesamorelin stimulates your pituitary to release its own GH in natural pulses. This preserves your body’s feedback systems and may be safer than constant GH levels.

  4. Effects don’t last after stopping - Here’s the catch: when you stop taking tesamorelin, the visceral fat comes back. The drug treats symptoms, not the underlying cause, so continuous therapy is needed.

Regulatory status: FDA-approved since 2010 for HIV-associated lipodystrophy. Currently in Phase 3 trials for NAFLD/NASH (fatty liver disease), where it showed a 37% reduction in liver fat.

Molecular Structure: Modified GHRH(1-44)

Tesamorelin is the full 44-amino acid human GHRH sequence with a trans-3-hexenoic acid (hexenoyl) modification at the N-terminus tyrosine. This modification:

  • Provides resistance to DPP-IV degradation
  • Enhances stability vs endogenous GHRH
  • Maintains full receptor binding activity

Why HIV Patients Have Lipodystrophy

Context for mechanism:

  • HIV patients on ART often develop reduced GH secretion and lower GH pulse amplitude
  • This correlates with increased visceral adipose tissue (VAT)
  • Up to 30% of ART patients experience abdominal fat accumulation
  • Tesamorelin restores a more youthful GH rhythm

Visceral Fat Specificity

Why tesamorelin targets visceral fat:

  • VAT has higher lipolytic sensitivity to GH/IGF-1
  • GH preferentially mobilizes fat from visceral compartment
  • Subcutaneous fat less affected (different receptor density)
  • No significant effect on limb fat or total body weight

Duration of Effect

Effects of tesamorelin are not sustained after discontinuation:

  • VAT returns to near-baseline within weeks of stopping
  • Chronic therapy required to maintain reductions
  • Highlights that mechanism addresses symptoms, not underlying cause

Key Evidence Highlights

  1. Phase 3 Trials: Consistent VAT reduction demonstrated (n=806 pooled)
  2. NAFLD/NASH: Reduced liver fat 37%, prevented fibrosis progression
  3. Cognitive Function (TEAME): Trend toward improvement, not statistically significant
  4. Metabolic Effects: Improvements in triglycerides, trunk fat, IGF-1
  5. Long-term Data: Effects maintained with continued treatment
  6. Safety: Generally well-tolerated, known GH-related effects

Warnings and Precautions

  • Fluid retention: Can exacerbate edema conditions
  • Glucose effects: May impair glucose tolerance
  • Carpal tunnel: GH-related risk
  • Malignancy: Contraindicated with active malignancy
  • Hypersensitivity: Rare allergic reactions

Contraindications

  • Active malignancy
  • Disruption of hypothalamic-pituitary axis
  • Known hypersensitivity to tesamorelin or mannitol
  • Pregnancy

NAFLD/NASH Development

Phase 2 trial in HIV-associated NAFLD showed (PMID: 31611038):

  • 37% relative reduction in liver fat (HFF)
  • Prevented fibrosis progression vs placebo
  • 35% achieved HFF <5% (vs 4% placebo)
  • Improved hepatic gene expression (oxidative phosphorylation)
  • Decreased inflammatory markers

Regulatory progress: FDA approved Phase 3 NASH development in December 2020 for patients with biopsy-confirmed NASH (CRN stage 2-3).

Cognitive Function Research (TEAME Trial)

Phase 2 trial in HIV with abdominal obesity (PMID: 39813152):

  • Mechanism hypothesis: Visceral adiposity linked to neuroinflammation and reduced IGF-1
  • Primary outcome: Trend toward cognitive improvement (p=0.060), not significant between groups
  • Secondary outcome: Significant waist circumference reduction (-2.7 cm, p=0.015)
  • Limitations: Underpowered, no placebo arm
  • Conclusion: Short-term VAT reduction may not be sufficient for cognitive benefits
