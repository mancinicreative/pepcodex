## Overview

Cagrilintide is Novo Nordisk's investigational long-acting amylin analog, while semaglutide is an approved GLP-1 receptor agonist. These peptides work through different mechanisms and are being developed as a combination therapy (CagriSema) that may provide superior weight loss to either alone.

## Mechanism Comparison

| Aspect | Cagrilintide | Semaglutide |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| **Target** | Amylin receptors (AMY1, AMY2, AMY3) | GLP-1 receptor |
| **Natural Hormone** | Amylin analog | GLP-1 analog |
| **Primary Site** | Area postrema (brainstem) | Hypothalamus, GI, pancreas |
| **Half-life** | ~160 hours | ~165 hours |

### Why Different Mechanisms Matter

**Cagrilintide (Amylin):**
- Slows gastric emptying
- Reduces glucagon secretion
- Promotes satiety via brainstem signaling
- Distinct neuronal pathways from GLP-1

**Semaglutide (GLP-1):**
- Enhances insulin secretion
- Suppresses glucagon
- Appetite reduction via hypothalamus
- Delays gastric emptying

These complementary mechanisms support the combination approach.

## The CagriSema Strategy

Novo Nordisk's approach:
1. **Combine different satiety pathways** - Amylin + GLP-1
2. **Maximize weight loss** - Target 20%+
3. **Maintain tolerability** - Fixed-dose combination
4. **Compete with tirzepatide** - Match or exceed dual agonist efficacy

## Summary

- **Cagrilintide** is an amylin analog with modest standalone efficacy but strategic value as a combination partner
- **Semaglutide** is the proven GLP-1 agonist with extensive data and CV benefit
- **Different mechanisms** allow additive effects when combined (CagriSema)
- **CagriSema** may offer weight loss comparable to or exceeding tirzepatide
- Cagrilintide is unlikely to be marketed as standalone therapy
