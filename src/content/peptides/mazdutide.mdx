Mazdutide is a dual GLP-1 and glucagon receptor agonist with robust Phase 3 clinical trial data. Its mechanisms are well-characterized in human studies.

### How It Works (Simplified)

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 not-prose my-8">

    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Appetite Suppression</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">GLP-1 receptor activation in the brain increases satiety signals, reducing hunger and food intake similar to semaglutide.</p>

  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Liver Fat Burning</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Glucagon receptor activation directly stimulates hepatic fatty acid oxidation, burning fat stored in the liver.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Insulin Enhancement</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Glucose-dependent insulin secretion improves blood sugar control without causing hypoglycemia when blood sugar is normal.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Energy Expenditure</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Glucagon increases metabolic rate and calorie burning, contributing to weight loss beyond appetite reduction alone.</p>
  </div>

**Key Research:** Ji L et al. (NEJM 2025) demonstrated 14.0% weight loss at 48 weeks with the 6mg dose, alongside beneficial effects on all prespecified cardiometabolic measures, in the GLORY-1 trial. [PMID:40421736](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40421736/)

### Important Limitations

- Currently only approved in China (NMPA); not yet FDA approved
- Long-term safety data beyond 48 weeks is limited
- Head-to-head data vs tirzepatide not yet available
- Optimal patient selection criteria still being refined
- GI tolerability requires careful dose titration
