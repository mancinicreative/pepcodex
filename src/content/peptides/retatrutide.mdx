## Overview

<h2 id="overview" class="sr-only">Overview</h2>

### Quick Summary

- **Triple agonist** targeting GIP, GLP-1, and glucagon receptors simultaneously
- **Not yet FDA approved** - currently in Phase 3 clinical trials (TRIUMPH program)
- **First Phase 3 results (TRIUMPH-4):** 28.7% weight loss at 68 weeks in patients with knee osteoarthritis
- **Phase 2 results:** Up to 24.2% weight loss at 48 weeks in patients with obesity
- **Differentiated mechanism** from tirzepatide (dual) and semaglutide (single)
- **Being developed by Eli Lilly** for obesity, type 2 diabetes, MASLD, and osteoarthritis

**Evidence Strength: Moderate** — Strong Phase 2 data with consistent results. First Phase 3 results positive (TRIUMPH-4, Dec 2025). Additional Phase 3 trials ongoing. No regulatory approval yet.

<h2 id="mechanism-of-action">Mechanism of Action</h2>

### How Retatrutide Works

Retatrutide activates **three different hormone receptors** at once, making it the most comprehensive weight loss peptide in development. Here's what each receptor does:

1. **GLP-1 receptor** - Tells your brain you're full and slows down digestion. This is the same receptor that Ozempic targets.

2. **GIP receptor** - Works with GLP-1 to boost insulin release after meals and may help your fat cells function more healthily. This is what Mounjaro adds on top of GLP-1.

3. **Glucagon receptor** - This is retatrutide's unique addition. Glucagon tells your liver to burn fat for energy and increases your metabolic rate. It's like having your internal furnace turned up.

**Why the glucagon receptor matters:** The liver has almost no GLP-1 or GIP receptors, but it's packed with glucagon receptors. This is why retatrutide may be especially effective for fatty liver disease, reducing liver fat by over 80% in trials.

**The balancing act:** Adding glucagon might seem counterintuitive since it raises blood sugar. However, the GLP-1 and GIP components counteract this by boosting insulin, resulting in improved blood sugar control alongside greater weight loss than either mechanism alone.

### Triple Receptor Science

Retatrutide is a **triple receptor agonist** (GIP/GLP-1/Glucagon), the most comprehensive incretin-based therapy in development. It adds **glucagon receptor agonism** to the dual mechanism of tirzepatide.

### Receptor Potency Profile

Retatrutide has a specific potency hierarchy:

| Receptor | EC50 | Relative to Native Hormone |
|----------|------|----------------------------|
| **GIPR** | 0.064 nM | **Highest potency** |
| **GLP-1R** | 0.78 nM | Moderate potency |
| **GCGR** | 5.79 nM | Lower but physiologically relevant |

### The Glucagon Paradox: Why Add a "Diabetogenic" Hormone?

**Traditional view:** Glucagon raises blood sugar → bad for diabetes
**Modern understanding:** Glucagon also provides metabolic benefits when balanced with GLP-1/GIP

**What Glucagon Receptor Agonism Adds:**
```
Glucagon Receptor Activation
    ↓
├── LIVER (rich in GCGR - unlike GLP-1/GIP!)
│   ├── ↑ Hepatic lipid oxidation
│   ├── ↑ Gluconeogenesis (balanced by GLP-1/GIP insulin)
│   ├── ↓ Lipogenesis
│   ├── ↓ Oxidative stress in mitochondria
│   └── Anti-fibrotic benefits (NASH improvement)
│
├── ADIPOSE TISSUE
│   ├── ↑ Lipolysis (fat breakdown)
│   └── ↑ Non-esterified fatty acid release
│
├── BROWN ADIPOSE TISSUE
│   └── ↑ Thermogenesis → Energy expenditure
│
└── GI TRACT
    └── ↓ GI motility (additive to GLP-1)
```

### Why the Liver Is Key

**Critical insight:** The liver has **NO GLP-1 or GIP receptors** but is **rich in glucagon receptors**.

This makes retatrutide uniquely positioned for:
- **NASH/MASLD treatment** (direct liver effects)
- **Hepatic fat reduction** (82% reduction in Phase 2a)
- **Improved mitochondrial function**
- **Anti-fibrotic benefits**

### Triple Agonist Signaling Cascade

```
Retatrutide administration
    ↓
├── GLP-1R PATHWAY
│   ├── Brain: Satiety ↑, hunger ↓
│   ├── Pancreas: Insulin ↑, glucagon ↓
│   └── GI: Gastric emptying delayed
│
├── GIPR PATHWAY
│   ├── Pancreas: Additional insulin secretion
│   ├── Adipose: Healthy adipocyte function
│   └── Brain: Enhanced satiety integration
│
└── GCGR PATHWAY
    ├── Liver: Fat oxidation ↑, steatosis ↓
    ├── Brown fat: Thermogenesis ↑
    └── Energy expenditure: 10-15% increase
```

### Why ~24% Weight Loss?

The triple mechanism provides three complementary pathways:

| Mechanism | Contribution | Receptor |
|-----------|--------------|----------|
| **Reduced intake** | ~60% | GLP-1R, GIPR |
| **Increased expenditure** | ~25% | GCGR |
| **Improved fat oxidation** | ~15% | GCGR, GIPR |

### Safety Balancing Act

The GLP-1/GIP components **counterbalance** glucagon's hyperglycemic potential:
- GLP-1/GIP → ↑ insulin, ↓ glucagon secretion
- GCGR → hepatic glucose production
- **Net effect:** Improved glycemia with greater weight loss

### Unique Liver Findings

Phase 2a MASLD trial results:
- **81-82% liver fat reduction** at 48 weeks
- Direct GCGR-mediated hepatoprotection
- Potential to be first triple agonist approved for liver disease

<h2 id="key-human-trials">Key Human Trials</h2>

### Phase 2 in Obesity (NEJM 2023)

**ClinicalTrials.gov:** NCT04881760
**Study design:** 48-week, dose-ranging RCT in adults with obesity
**N = 338** participants without diabetes
**Publication:** Jastreboff AM et al. N Engl J Med. 2023; PMID: 37366315

| Dose | Weight Loss (48 wk) | Key Notes |
|------|---------------------|-----------|
| 1 mg | -8.7% | Starting tier |
| 4 mg | -17.1% | Mid-range |
| 8 mg | -22.8% | Near-maximum |
| 12 mg | -24.2% | Highest studied |
| Placebo | -2.1% | Control |

**Key findings:**
- 93% of 12mg participants achieved ≥10% weight loss
- 83% achieved ≥15% weight loss
- Weight plateau not yet reached at 48 weeks
- GI adverse events manageable with dose titration

### Phase 2 in Type 2 Diabetes (Lancet 2023)

**ClinicalTrials.gov:** NCT04867785
**N = 281** participants with T2D (36 weeks)
**Publication:** Rosenstock J et al. Lancet. 2023; PMID: 37385280

| Metric | 12 mg Dose | Placebo |
|--------|------------|---------|
| HbA1c reduction | -2.02% | -0.01% |
| Weight loss | -16.9% | +1.6% |
| HbA1c ≤6.5% achieved | 82% | — |

### Phase 2a in MASLD (Nature Medicine 2024)

<h2 id="safety-profile">Safety Profile</h2>

### Phase 2 Adverse Events

| Event | Incidence (approx.) | Notes |
|-------|---------------------|-------|
| Nausea | 20-25% | Higher than tirzepatide early |
| Diarrhea | 15-20% | Dose-related |
| Vomiting | 10-15% | During titration |
| Constipation | 8-12% | Variable |
| Decreased appetite | 15-20% | Expected mechanism |

### Notable Observations

- **Heart rate:** Small increases observed (2-4 bpm)
- **Liver enzymes:** Transient elevations in some subjects
- **Thyroid:** Standard GLP-1 class monitoring applies

### Theoretical Concerns

- **Glucagon effects:** Could affect glycemic control if imbalanced
- **Long-term safety:** Awaiting Phase 3 data
- **Hepatic effects:** Being monitored in trials
