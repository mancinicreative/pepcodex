## Why Titration Matters

Starting at full dose can cause:
- Increased side effects
- Poor tolerability
- Treatment discontinuation

Gradual titration allows:
- Body to adapt
- Side effect management
- Better long-term adherence

## The Science Behind Titration

### Receptor Adaptation
- Receptors adjust to new signaling levels
- Gradual exposure prevents overwhelming response
- Downstream pathways adapt

### Physiological Adjustment
- GI system adapts to slowed gastric emptying
- Appetite centers recalibrate
- Metabolic pathways adjust

### Side Effect Minimization
- Nausea most common early
- Peaks during dose increases
- Usually improves with time

## Titration Strategies

### Fixed Schedule
- Predetermined dose increases
- Timed intervals (usually weekly)
- Standard approach for most patients

### Response-Based
- Adjust based on tolerance
- May slow titration if side effects severe
- May accelerate if well-tolerated

### Goal-Based
- Titrate until therapeutic goal reached
- May not need maximum dose
- Individualized approach

## Managing Side Effects During Titration

| Side Effect | Strategy |
|-------------|----------|
| Nausea | Eat smaller meals, avoid fatty foods |
| GI upset | Stay hydrated, eat bland foods |
| Constipation | Increase fiber, fluids |
| Fatigue | Usually temporary, rest as needed |

If side effects severe, may need to:
- Hold at current dose longer
- Step back to previous dose
- Discuss alternatives with provider

## Titration Best Practices

### Do
- Follow prescribed schedule
- Keep track of doses and effects
- Communicate with healthcare provider
- Be patient—adaptation takes time

### Don't
- Skip titration steps
- Increase dose faster than recommended
- Stop abruptly (especially if side effects)
- Adjust without medical guidance

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why can't I just start at the full dose?

Your body needs time to adapt. GLP-1 agonists affect multiple systems (GI, appetite, glucose), and sudden full-dose activation often causes significant nausea and GI side effects. Gradual titration dramatically improves tolerability.

### What if I miss a dose during titration?

Follow the specific medication's guidelines. Generally, if close to your regular time, take it. If significant time has passed, skip and resume schedule. Don't double up. Consult your prescriber for specific guidance.

### Can I titrate faster if I'm tolerating it well?

Not recommended without medical supervision. The titration schedule is designed based on clinical trials. Even if you feel fine, your body may still be adapting. Faster titration may cause delayed side effects or miss optimal dosing.
