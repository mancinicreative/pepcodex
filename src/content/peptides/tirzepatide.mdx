## Overview

<h2 id="overview" class="sr-only">Overview</h2>

### Quick Summary

- **Dual agonist** of GIP and GLP-1 receptors, a novel mechanism distinct from GLP-1-only drugs
- **FDA approved** for type 2 diabetes (Mounjaro, 2022) and chronic weight management (Zepbound, 2023)
- **Substantial clinical evidence** from multiple Phase 3 trials (SURPASS and SURMOUNT programs)
- **Weight loss** of 15-22% observed in clinical trials, among the highest for any approved medication
- **Safety profile** characterized in thousands of patients; GI side effects most common

**Evidence Strength: High** — Multiple large RCTs with consistent results, FDA approval, post-marketing data accumulating.

<h2 id="mechanism-of-action">Mechanism of Action</h2>

Tirzepatide was designed to improve upon existing GLP-1 receptor agonists by adding GIP receptor agonism. The hypothesis was that dual agonism would provide additive or synergistic effects on glucose metabolism and body weight regulation.

### Supported Mechanisms (Direct Evidence)

Tirzepatide acts on two incretin hormone receptors:

**GLP-1 Receptor Agonism:**
- Stimulates glucose-dependent insulin secretion from pancreatic beta cells
- Suppresses glucagon secretion (glucose-dependent)
- Delays gastric emptying
- Promotes satiety through CNS pathways

**GIP Receptor Agonism:**
- Stimulates glucose-dependent insulin secretion (additive to GLP-1)
- May influence lipid metabolism and fat tissue directly
- Potential effects on bone metabolism (under investigation)

Both mechanisms are supported by receptor binding studies, clinical pharmacology data, and mechanistic trials [PMID: 34170647].

### Hypothesized Mechanisms (Under Investigation)

- Direct effects on adipose tissue metabolism via GIP receptors
- Potential cardiovascular benefits independent of weight loss
- Effects on hepatic lipid metabolism relevant to MASH/NASH

<h2 id="key-human-trials">Key Human Trials</h2>

### Type 2 Diabetes (SURPASS Program)

The SURPASS clinical trial program evaluated tirzepatide in patients with type 2 diabetes:

| Trial | Comparison | HbA1c Reduction | Weight Change | N |
|-------|------------|-----------------|---------------|---|
| SURPASS-1 | Placebo | -1.87 to -2.07% | -7 to -9 kg | 478 |
| SURPASS-2 | Semaglutide 1mg | -2.01 to -2.30% | -7.6 to -11.2 kg | 1,879 |
| SURPASS-3 | Insulin degludec | -1.93 to -2.37% | -7.5 to -12 kg | 1,444 |
| SURPASS-4 | Insulin glargine | -1.87 to -2.11% | -7.1 to -11.7 kg | 2,002 |

**Key findings:**
- Superior HbA1c reduction compared to semaglutide 1mg [PMID: 34170647]
- Greater weight loss across all comparisons
- Consistent results across trials

### Weight Management (SURMOUNT Program)

The SURMOUNT program evaluated tirzepatide specifically for weight management:

| Trial | Population | Weight Loss (15mg) | N |
|-------|------------|-------------------|---|
| SURMOUNT-1 | Obesity without diabetes | -22.5% | 2,539 |
| SURMOUNT-2 | Obesity with T2D | -15.7% | 938 |
| SURMOUNT-3 | After lifestyle intervention | -24.1%* | 579 |
| SURMOUNT-4 | Withdrawal study | Maintained vs regain | 670 |

*SURMOUNT-3 included intensive lifestyle intervention lead-in.

**SURMOUNT-1 Key Results [PMID: 35658024]:**
- Placebo-adjusted weight loss: ~19%
- 36% of participants achieved ≥25% weight loss at highest dose
- Improvements in cardiometabolic risk factors

<h2 id="preclinical-evidence">Preclinical Evidence</h2>

### Animal Studies

| Study Type | Model | Key Findings |
|------------|-------|--------------|
| Pharmacology | Rodent | Confirmed dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor binding |
| Efficacy | Obese mice | Dose-dependent weight loss, improved glucose tolerance |
| Mechanism | Rodent | Central and peripheral effects on appetite regulation |
| Toxicology | Rodent/Primate | Thyroid C-cell tumors in rodents (class effect) |

### In Vitro Studies

- Receptor binding assays confirm high affinity for both GIP and GLP-1 receptors
- GIP receptor activity is ~5x relative to native GIP
- GLP-1 receptor activity is comparable to native GLP-1
- Half-life extended through fatty acid acylation (albumin binding)

### Limitations of Preclinical Data

- Thyroid C-cell findings in rodents have uncertain relevance to humans
- GIP receptor biology differs between rodents and humans
- Long-term effects not fully characterized in animal models

<h2 id="safety-profile">Safety Profile</h2>

### Common Adverse Events

Based on clinical trial data, the most frequent adverse events are gastrointestinal:

| Event | Incidence (approx.) | Notes |
|-------|---------------------|-------|
| Nausea | 12-18% | Usually transient, decreases over time |
| Diarrhea | 12-17% | Dose-dependent |
| Vomiting | 5-9% | More common during dose escalation |
| Constipation | 6-7% | Less common than other GI effects |
| Decreased appetite | 5-10% | Often considered part of mechanism |

### Serious Adverse Events & Warnings

- **Thyroid C-cell tumors:** Boxed warning based on rodent studies; clinical relevance uncertain
- **Pancreatitis:** Rare reports; monitor for symptoms
- **Gallbladder disease:** Increased incidence with rapid weight loss
- **Hypoglycemia:** Low risk as monotherapy; increased with insulin/sulfonylureas

### Contraindications

- Personal/family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma
- Multiple endocrine neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN2)
- Known hypersensitivity to tirzepatide

<h2 id="pharmacokinetics">Pharmacokinetics</h2>

### Key Parameters

| Parameter | Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Half-life** | ~5 days | Supports once-weekly dosing |
| **Bioavailability** | ~80% (subcutaneous) | High for peptide drug |
| **Time to peak** | 8-72 hours | Broad range |
| **Metabolism** | Proteolytic cleavage | Not dependent on CYP enzymes |
| **Elimination** | Renal and fecal | As peptide fragments |

### Dosing

- **Starting dose:** 2.5 mg once weekly
- **Titration:** Increase by 2.5 mg every 4 weeks as tolerated
- **Maximum dose:** 15 mg once weekly
- **Administration:** Subcutaneous injection (abdomen, thigh, or upper arm)

### Special Populations

- **Renal impairment:** No dose adjustment required
- **Hepatic impairment:** No dose adjustment required
- **Elderly:** No dose adjustment required; similar efficacy and safety

### Tirzepatide vs Semaglutide

Head-to-head comparison from SURPASS-2 [PMID: 34170647]:

| Outcome | Tirzepatide 15mg | Semaglutide 1mg |
|---------|------------------|-----------------|
| HbA1c reduction | -2.30% | -1.86% |
| Weight loss | -11.2 kg | -5.7 kg |
| GI adverse events | Similar | Similar |

**Key difference:** Tirzepatide showed greater efficacy on both endpoints, though direct comparison with semaglutide 2.4mg (weight management dose) not available from this trial.

### Tirzepatide vs Retatrutide

- Retatrutide is a triple agonist (GIP/GLP-1/glucagon) in Phase 3 trials
- Early data suggests potentially greater weight loss (up to 24% in Phase 2)
- No approved product yet; head-to-head data limited
- Different mechanism: adds glucagon receptor agonism
