## Feature 1: Featured Snippets

### Queries Triggering Featured Snippets

| Query Type | Example | Snippet Format | Opportunity |
|------------|---------|----------------|-------------|
| "What is" | "what is bpc-157" | Paragraph | High |
| "How does" | "how does semaglutide work" | Paragraph | High |
| "Difference between" | "difference between semaglutide and tirzepatide" | Table | High |
| "List of" | "list of gh secretagogues" | Bulleted list | Medium |
| "Benefits of" | "benefits of bpc-157" | Bulleted list | High |
| "Side effects of" | "side effects of semaglutide" | Bulleted list | Medium |

### Optimization Strategy

**For Paragraph Snippets:**
- Include a 40-60 word definition immediately after H2
- Start with "[Term] is..." format
- Include the query phrase in the answer

**For List Snippets:**
- Use H2 with query phrase
- Immediately follow with `<ul>` or `<ol>`
- 3-8 items optimal

**For Table Snippets:**
- Use H2 with "vs" or "comparison"
- Immediately follow with `<table>`
- Include key attributes in rows

# COMPETITOR WEAKNESS EXPLOITATION

## Weakness 1: No Evidence Grading

**Gap:** Neither competitor systematically grades evidence quality.

**Exploitation:**
- Prominently display evidence grades on all pages
- Include in meta descriptions: "Evidence Grade: [High/Moderate/Low]"
- Create comparison tables showing evidence levels
- Target queries with "evidence", "research", "studies" modifiers

## Weakness 2: Unlabeled Animal Studies

**Gap:** Competitors present animal study findings without clear labeling.

**Exploitation:**
- Always label study type: "In animal models..." / "In human trials..."
- Show study breakdown: "X human studies, Y animal, Z in-vitro"
- Position as "transparent research synthesis"

## Weakness 3: No Methodology Transparency

**Gap:** Neither competitor explains how they evaluate or grade content.

**Exploitation:**
- Create detailed methodology page
- Link from every dossier page
- Use in E-E-A-T positioning
- Target journalists/researchers who want citable sources

## Weakness 4: No Author Credentials

**Gap:** Neither competitor shows who creates/reviews content.

**Exploitation:**
- Add editorial team page
- Show credentials prominently
- Medical/scientific review badges (if applicable)

## Weakness 5: Publication Bias Acknowledgment

**Gap:** Neither competitor discusses limitations of peptide research.

**Exploitation:**
- Include "Research Limitations" section
- Cite: "All published BPC-157 studies report positive effects, suggesting possible publication bias"
- Position as intellectually honest, unlike cheerleader sites
