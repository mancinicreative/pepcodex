## Overview

<h2 id="overview" class="sr-only">Overview</h2>

### Quick Summary

- **The only human cathelicidin** - One of two major human antimicrobial peptide families (the other being defensins)
- **37 amino acids** beginning with two leucines (hence "LL-37")
- **Discovered at Karolinska Institute, Sweden** in 1995 by Agerberth, Gudmundsson, and colleagues
- **Broad-spectrum antimicrobial** - Active against bacteria, fungi, viruses, and some parasites
- **Immunomodulatory functions** - Beyond direct killing, modulates immune responses
- **Endogenous human molecule** - Produced by neutrophils, epithelial cells, and macrophages
- **Clinical trials ongoing** - Investigational use for wound healing, cancer immunotherapy

**Evidence Strength: Moderate** - Extensive mechanistic and preclinical research with growing human translational data. Multiple clinical trials completed or in progress. Understanding of biological roles is well-established.

### Discovery Timeline

- **1995:** Initial discovery of FALL-39 gene at Karolinska Institute, Sweden (Agerberth et al.)
- **1996:** Characterization of mature LL-37 peptide processing from hCAP18 precursor (Gudmundsson et al.)
- **1997-1999:** Structural characterization; alpha-helical nature established
- **2001:** First in vivo proof of essential antimicrobial role (knockout mouse studies)
- **2003:** Angiogenic properties discovered; wound healing roles established
- **2006:** Vitamin D regulation of LL-37 expression discovered (tuberculosis immunity)
- **2007:** Role in autoimmune diseases (psoriasis, rosacea) identified
- **2010s-present:** Clinical trials for wound healing, cancer immunotherapy

<h2 id="mechanism-of-action">Mechanism of Action</h2>

LL-37 is a multifunctional peptide with well-characterized mechanisms based on extensive research from laboratories worldwide, with foundational work from Swedish researchers.

### How LL-37 Works

Think of LL-37 as your body's "Swiss Army knife" of defense:

1. **Punches holes in bacterial membranes** - LL-37 is positively charged and attracted to negatively charged bacterial surfaces. Like a key fitting into a lock, it inserts into bacterial membranes and creates pores, causing the bacteria to leak and die.

2. **Neutralizes bacterial toxins** - When bacteria die or multiply, they release toxins called LPS (lipopolysaccharide) or endotoxin. LL-37 binds to these toxins like a sponge, preventing them from triggering dangerous inflammation (sepsis).

3. **Calls immune cells to battle** - LL-37 acts as a chemical signal that attracts neutrophils, monocytes, and T cells to sites of infection or injury. It is essentially a "come here" signal for your immune system.

4. **Helps wounds heal** - Beyond fighting infection, LL-37 promotes the growth of new blood vessels (angiogenesis) and helps skin cells migrate to close wounds.

5. **Regulates inflammation** - LL-37 can both activate and calm immune responses depending on the context, helping balance the inflammatory response.

**Important context:** These mechanisms are supported by human studies and clinical observations, not just animal models.

### Scientific Pathways

**1. Membrane Disruption (Direct Antimicrobial)**
```
LL-37 (cationic, +6 charge)
    |
    v
Attracted to anionic bacterial membrane
    |
    v
Alpha-helix formation in membrane
    |
    v
Membrane permeabilization/pore formation
    |
    v
Cell lysis and death
```

The positive charge of LL-37 (+6 at physiological pH) is electrostatically attracted to the negatively charged lipopolysaccharides (LPS) of Gram-negative bacteria or lipoteichoic acids of Gram-positive bacteria. Human cell membranes have neutral phospholipids, explaining selective toxicity to bacteria.

**2. Immunomodulatory Pathways**

*FPRL1/FPR2 Receptor Signaling:*
```
LL-37 + FPRL1 receptor
    |
    v
G-protein activation
    |
    v
Multiple downstream effects:
├── Chemotaxis (immune cell recruitment)
├── Angiogenesis (new blood vessel formation)
├── Cytokine modulation
└── Cell proliferation
```

*TLR Modulation:*
- Binds bacterial DNA/LPS and delivers to intracellular TLRs
- Can enhance or suppress TLR signaling depending on context
- Links innate and adaptive immunity

**3. Wound Healing Cascade**
```
Tissue injury
    |
    v
LL-37 released from neutrophils/keratinocytes
    |
    v
EGFR transactivation
    |
    v
├── Keratinocyte migration
├── Fibroblast proliferation
├── Endothelial cell proliferation (angiogenesis)
└── Re-epithelialization
```

LL-37 promotes wound healing through multiple parallel mechanisms, including direct cell migration signals via EGFR (epidermal growth factor receptor) transactivation and angiogenesis through FPRL1/VEGF pathways.

**4. Vitamin D Regulation**
```
Vitamin D (sunlight/diet)
    |
    v
1,25(OH)2D3 (active vitamin D)
    |
    v
Vitamin D receptor activation
    |
    v
CAMP gene transcription
    |
    v
hCAP18 production
    |
    v
Protease cleavage
    |
    v
Active LL-37
```

This pathway, discovered in 2006, explains why vitamin D deficiency increases susceptibility to infections like tuberculosis. Sunlight exposure and vitamin D supplementation can increase LL-37 production.

### Structural Features

| Property | Value | Significance |
|----------|-------|--------------|
| Length | 37 amino acids | Optimal for membrane interaction |
| Sequence | LLGDFFRKSKEKIGKEFKRIVQRIKDFLRNLVPRTES | Amphipathic |
| Charge | +6 at pH 7 | Bacterial selectivity |
| Structure | Alpha-helical (in membrane) | Required for activity |
| Cysteine residues | 0 | Unique among AMPs |

The lack of cysteine residues makes LL-37 unique among antimicrobial peptides, which typically rely on disulfide bonds for structure. LL-37 instead forms an alpha-helix only when encountering bacterial membranes.

### Key Evidence Highlights

1. **Wound Healing:** LL-37 deficient in chronic non-healing ulcers (human studies); promotes re-epithelialization
2. **Urinary Tract Defense:** Essential for UTI protection; deficiency linked to recurrent infections
3. **Skin Infections:** Knockout mice susceptible to Group A Streptococcus; humans with low LL-37 have more skin infections
4. **Vitamin D Connection:** Explains how vitamin D protects against infections
5. **Autoimmune Link:** Aberrant LL-37 processing drives psoriasis and rosacea inflammation

**Strength of Evidence:** LL-37 research benefits from being an endogenous human molecule. Unlike synthetic peptides studied only in animals, LL-37's roles are validated by human genetic studies, disease associations, and clinical observations.
