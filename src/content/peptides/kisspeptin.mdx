Kisspeptin is the **master regulator** of reproduction, acting as the upstream controller of the entire hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis. Human clinical trials have established its physiological role and therapeutic potential.

### How It Works (Simplified)

Kisspeptin serves as the gatekeeper of fertility by directly activating GnRH neurons:

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 not-prose my-8">
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">GnRH Activation</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Binds KISS1R on GnRH neurons, triggering Gq/11-coupled signaling cascade that causes calcium mobilization and GnRH release.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">LH/FSH Release</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">GnRH stimulates pituitary gonadotrophs to release luteinizing hormone (LH) and follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) in physiological pulses.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Steroid Feedback</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Kisspeptin neurons express estrogen and progesterone receptors, integrating sex steroid feedback to regulate ovulation timing.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Self-Limiting Action</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Unlike hCG, kisspeptin produces a short-lived LH surge (hours vs days), eliminating ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome risk in IVF.</p>
  </div>
</div>

**Key Research:** Abbara A et al. (Imperial College London, 2017) demonstrated kisspeptin-54 as safe IVF trigger with zero OHSS cases. [PMID:28854728](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28854728/)

### Important Limitations

- Clinical trials primarily conducted by single research group (Imperial College London)
- Long-term safety data not yet available
- Optimal dosing regimens still being refined
- Continuous administration causes tachyphylaxis (desensitization)
- Not yet approved by FDA or EMA; investigational use only
