The proposed mechanisms of EVX-01 are based on Phase 1/2a clinical data and established neoantigen vaccine immunology. The personalized approach uses AI-driven prediction to identify patient-specific tumor targets.

### How It Works (Simplified)

EVX-01 is a personalized cancer vaccine that trains the immune system to attack tumor-specific targets:

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 not-prose my-8">
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">AI Target Selection</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">PIONEER platform analyzes tumor DNA to predict which mutations will generate immunogenic neoantigens unique to your cancer.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Dendritic Cell Uptake</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Long peptides are taken up by dendritic cells at injection site, processed, and presented on MHC-I and MHC-II molecules.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">T-Cell Priming</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Both CD8+ cytotoxic and CD4+ helper T cells are activated, creating a coordinated immune response against tumor neoantigens.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Checkpoint Synergy</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Combined with anti-PD-1 therapy, vaccine-primed T cells can effectively attack tumor cells without immunosuppressive brakes.</p>
  </div>
</div>

**Key Research:** The Phase 1/2 trial (NCT03715985) demonstrated 100% T-cell immunogenicity and a 67% objective response rate (8/12; 6 partial, 2 complete) in melanoma when combined with anti-PD-1 therapy.

### Important Limitations

- Single-arm Phase 1/2a trial with only 12 evaluable patients
- No randomized comparison to pembrolizumab alone
- Historical control comparison has inherent limitations
- Personalized manufacturing adds 6-8 weeks from biopsy to first dose
- Scalability and cost-effectiveness not yet demonstrated
