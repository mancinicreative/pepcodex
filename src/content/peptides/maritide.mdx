## Overview

<h2 id="overview" class="sr-only">Overview</h2>

### Quick Summary

- **Bispecific molecule:** GLP-1 receptor agonist + GIP receptor antagonist (antibody conjugate)
- **Unique mechanism:** GIP ANTAGONISM (opposite of tirzepatide's GIP agonism)
- **Once-monthly dosing** — longest dosing interval in obesity drug development
- **Phase 2:** ~20% weight loss at 52 weeks; weight still declining (no plateau)
- **MARITIME program:** Phase 3 trials initiated 2025
- **21-day half-life** enables monthly administration

**Evidence Strength: High** — Robust Phase 2 data, Phase 3 initiated, novel mechanism, strong sponsor (Amgen).

<h2 id="mechanism-of-action">Mechanism of Action</h2>

### How MariTide Works (Plain English)

MariTide takes a fundamentally different approach from tirzepatide:

1. **GLP-1 agonism (same as others)** — The GLP-1 peptide portion reduces appetite, improves blood sugar, and slows stomach emptying. This is familiar territory.

2. **GIP antagonism (unique)** — Here's where MariTide differs dramatically. While tirzepatide ACTIVATES GIP receptors, MariTide BLOCKS them. The antibody portion binds to GIP receptors and prevents GIP from working.

3. **Why block GIP?** — This is the million-dollar question. GIP's role in obesity is complex. In some contexts, blocking GIP may:
   - Reduce fat storage signals
   - Prevent GIP-driven hunger rebound
   - Alter nutrient partitioning

4. **Monthly dosing** — The antibody portion has a 21-day half-life, allowing once-monthly injections. This is the longest dosing interval in the obesity drug class.

**The big question:** Is GIP antagonism better than GIP agonism? No head-to-head data yet, but both tirzepatide (agonist) and MariTide (antagonist) show strong efficacy. The field is watching closely.

### The Science: GLP-1 Agonist + GIP Antagonist

```
MariTide administration
    ↓
├── GLP-1 PEPTIDE PORTION
│   ├── GLP-1R activation → Standard incretin effects
│   └── Appetite suppression, glucose control
│
└── ANTI-GIP ANTIBODY PORTION
    ├── GIPR blockade → Prevents GIP signaling
    ├── Adipose tissue: Altered lipid handling
    ├── Pancreas: Modified incretin effect
    └── 21-day half-life → Monthly dosing
```

### The GIP Controversy

| Approach | Drug | Weight Loss | Theory |
|----------|------|-------------|--------|
| GIP **Agonism** | Tirzepatide | ~22% | GIP enhances GLP-1 effects; improves adipose function |
| GIP **Antagonism** | MariTide | ~20% | GIP blockade prevents fat storage; reduces rebound hunger |

**Both work.** The field doesn't fully understand why both approaches produce similar weight loss. This suggests GIP's role is more nuanced than simple agonism/antagonism.

<h2 id="key-human-trials">Key Human Trials</h2>

### Phase 2 (Obesity without Diabetes)

| Trial | Population | Duration | N |
|-------|------------|----------|---|
| Phase 2 | BMI ≥30 or ≥27 with comorbidity | 52 weeks | ~400 |

**Key Results (ADA 2025):**
- **Weight loss:** ~20% at 52 weeks vs 2.6% placebo
- **No plateau:** Weight curves still declining at study end
- **Monthly dosing:** Titration: 21mg → 35mg → 70mg over 8 weeks
- **Safety:** GI events (class effect); generally well tolerated

### Phase 2 (Obesity with Type 2 Diabetes)

| Trial | Population | Duration | N |
|-------|------------|----------|---|
| Phase 2 T2D | T2D + obesity | 52 weeks | ~400 |

**Key Results:**
- **Weight loss:** ~17% at 52 weeks vs 1.4% placebo
- **HbA1c reduction:** Significant improvement
