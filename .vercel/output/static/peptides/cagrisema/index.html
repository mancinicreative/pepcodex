CagriSema

Also known as: Cagrilintide-Semaglutide, NNC0174-0833

A fixed-dose combination of cagrilintide (amylin analog) and semaglutide (GLP-1 agonist) for chronic weight management. Phase 3 REDEFINE trials demonstrate 20-23% weight loss at 68 weeks. NDA submitted to FDA December 2025. First GLP-1/amylin combination therapy.

Overview

Overview

Quick Summary

  • Fixed-dose combination of cagrilintide 2.4mg (amylin analog) + semaglutide 2.4mg (GLP-1 agonist)
  • NDA submitted to FDA December 2025; review expected 2026
  • REDEFINE-1 trial: 22.7% weight loss at 68 weeks (treatment policy estimand)
  • 60% of participants achieved ≥20% weight loss; 40.4% achieved ≥25% weight loss
  • Dual mechanism targets both GLP-1 and amylin pathways for synergistic appetite suppression
  • First-in-class GLP-1/amylin combination therapy for obesity

Evidence Strength: High — Large Phase 3 RCTs (4,600+ patients), NEJM publication, NDA filed, consistent results across populations.

Mechanism of Action

How CagriSema Works (Plain English)

CagriSema combines two different appetite-suppressing hormones into one weekly injection. Here’s why this matters:

  1. Two “I’m full” signals instead of one — Your brain has separate systems for feeling satisfied after eating. GLP-1 (mimicked by semaglutide) works through one pathway, while amylin (mimicked by cagrilintide) works through another. CagriSema activates BOTH, creating a stronger combined “stop eating” signal.

  2. Different brain regions targeted — The two components work in different areas of your brain involved in hunger and reward. Semaglutide affects the hypothalamus (hunger center), while cagrilintide additionally impacts the hindbrain and septum (satiety and reward areas). Together, they create more complete appetite control.

  3. True synergy, not just addition — In clinical trials, the combination produced MORE weight loss than you’d expect from adding the two drugs together. This suggests the two pathways enhance each other’s effects.

  4. Better blood sugar control — For people with prediabetes or diabetes, the combination improves glycemic control more effectively than either component alone. 88% of participants with prediabetes returned to normal blood sugar levels.

Why this matters: CagriSema produced 22.7% weight loss — approaching surgical levels of weight reduction with a weekly injection. This represents a meaningful advance beyond single-mechanism therapies like Wegovy (semaglutide alone).

The Science: Why Two Pathways Matter

CagriSema harnesses two distinct but complementary hormonal systems:

The Amylin System (Cagrilintide)

What is amylin? A 37-amino acid peptide co-secreted with insulin from pancreatic β-cells.

Natural functions:

  • Promotes satiety signaling in the hindbrain
  • Slows gastric emptying
  • Suppresses postprandial glucagon secretion
  • Reduces food intake through area postrema and nucleus tractus solitarius

Why it was overlooked: Unlike GLP-1, amylin deficiency in obesity was not well-characterized. Pramlintide (amylin analog) was approved for diabetes but never achieved widespread use due to multiple daily injections.

The GLP-1 System (Semaglutide)

What is GLP-1? A 30-amino acid incretin hormone released from intestinal L-cells.

Natural functions:

  • Glucose-dependent insulin secretion
  • Appetite suppression via hypothalamic POMC/CART neurons
  • Delayed gastric emptying
  • Reduced glucagon secretion

Key Evidence Highlights

  1. REDEFINE-1: 22.7% weight loss at 68 weeks in obesity without diabetes; 60% achieved ≥20% weight loss
  2. REDEFINE-2: 13.7% weight loss in obesity with T2D; 73.5% achieved HbA1c ≤6.5%
  3. Synergy demonstrated: Combination exceeded semaglutide alone (-14.9%) and cagrilintide alone (-11.5%)
  4. Cardiometabolic benefits: Improved BP, waist circumference, lipids, glycemic parameters
  5. Prediabetes reversal: 88% of participants with prediabetes returned to normoglycemia

Key Human Trials

REDEFINE-1 (Obesity without Diabetes)

TrialNCT IDPopulationDurationN
REDEFINE-1NCT05567796BMI ≥30 or ≥27 with comorbidity68 weeks3,417

Design: Phase 3a, double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled and active-controlled trial. Participants randomized 21:3:3:7 to CagriSema, semaglutide, cagrilintide, or placebo.

Key Results [PMID: 40544432]:

  • Primary endpoint: -20.4% weight loss with CagriSema vs -3.0% placebo (ETD -17.3%; P<0.001)
  • Treatment policy estimand: -22.7% weight loss (accounting for adherence)
  • ≥20% weight loss: 60% with CagriSema
  • ≥25% weight loss: 40.4% with CagriSema
  • ≥30% weight loss: 23% with CagriSema

Safety Profile

Severity and Duration

  • GI adverse events were mostly mild to moderate in severity
  • Events were transient, typically occurring during dose titration
  • Discontinuation due to AEs: ~5-7% (similar to GLP-1 monotherapy)

Serious Adverse Events

  • Pancreatitis: Rare; monitor for symptoms
  • Gallbladder disease: Increased risk with rapid weight loss (class effect)
  • Hypoglycemia: Low risk as monotherapy; increased with insulin/sulfonylureas

Precautions (Expected Based on Class)

  • Personal/family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC)
  • Multiple endocrine neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN2)
  • History of pancreatitis

Clinical Relevance

The matched pharmacokinetic profiles of both components enable once-weekly co-administration as a fixed-dose combination. Both peptides use fatty acid acylation for albumin binding, extending half-life and enabling weekly dosing.
