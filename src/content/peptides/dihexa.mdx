## Overview

### Quick Summary

- **Experimental small molecule peptide** (modified dipeptide) designed as angiotensin IV analog
- **Not approved** by any regulatory agency worldwide
- **No human clinical trials** conducted or registered
- **All research** originates from one research group at Washington State University
- **Mechanism claim:** Activates HGF/c-Met pathway to promote synaptogenesis
- **Potency claim:** "10 million times more potent than BDNF" (based on rodent studies, no human validation)
- **Sold as:** Research chemical with no quality standards

**Evidence Strength: Very Low** — Limited preclinical data from a single research group with no independent replication and no human studies.

## Mechanism of Action

### Proposed Mechanism

Dihexa is claimed to work through a novel mechanism involving hepatocyte growth factor (HGF):

**The HGF/c-Met Hypothesis:**

```
Dihexa → Proposed AT4R/IRAP interaction
    ↓
HGF/c-Met signaling potentiation
    ↓
├── Dendritic spine formation
├── Synaptogenesis
├── Long-term potentiation (LTP)
└── Memory consolidation
```

### Key Claims (From Preclinical Data)

1. **Potentiates HGF activity:** Claimed to enhance HGF binding to c-Met receptor
2. **Promotes synapse formation:** Increased dendritic spine density in rodent hippocampus
3. **Enhances LTP:** Improved long-term potentiation in brain slices
4. **Reverses cognitive deficits:** Improved performance in scopolamine-challenged rats

### Critical Mechanism Considerations

| Claim | Status | Caveat |
|-------|--------|--------|
| HGF potentiation | Shown in vitro | Mechanism disputed by some researchers |
| Synaptogenesis | Rodent data only | Human relevance unknown |
| Cognitive enhancement | Rodent behavior tests | No human data |
| "10 million x BDNF" | Marketing claim | Based on limited in vitro assays |

**Important:** The HGF/c-Met pathway is involved in cancer cell growth. Long-term effects of enhancing this pathway are unknown and could theoretically carry risks.

## Critical Analysis

### Red Flags

1. **"10 million times more potent than BDNF"**
   - Marketing-style claim
   - Based on narrow in vitro comparison
   - Potency ≠ efficacy in humans
   - BDNF isn't a drug, so comparison is misleading

2. **Single Research Group**
   - All publications from same lab
   - No independent confirmation
   - Potential conflict of interest (patent holders)

3. **No Clinical Development**
   - 10+ years since key publication
   - No registered clinical trials
   - No pharmaceutical company interest

4. **Research Chemical Market**
   - Sold despite lack of human safety data
   - Quality and purity unknown
   - Users are uncontrolled experimenters

### What Would Good Evidence Look Like?

- [ ] Independent replication of preclinical findings
- [ ] Comprehensive toxicology studies
- [ ] Phase 1 human safety trial
- [ ] Multiple animal models with consistent results
- [ ] Peer review of mechanism claims

Dihexa is an experimental compound with:

- No human studies exist

**Bottom Line:** Dihexa has generated interest due to extreme potency claims, but the evidence base is critically limited. Without independent replication or human trials, the compound remains highly experimental with unknown risks.
