## Overview

<h2 id="overview" class="sr-only">Overview</h2>

### Quick Summary

- **Personalized neoantigen peptide vaccine** developed by Evaxion Biotech (Copenhagen, Denmark)
- **AI-driven neoantigen prediction** using proprietary PIONEER platform for patient-specific vaccine design
- **Phase 1 completed in melanoma** with promising efficacy signals in combination with checkpoint inhibitors
- **67% overall response rate** when combined with anti-PD-1 therapy (pembrolizumab)
- **100% immunogenicity** - Neoantigen-specific T-cell responses detected in all vaccinated patients
- **Peptide-based platform** - Distinct from mRNA-based approaches like Moderna's mRNA-4157/V940
- **Durable responses observed** - Some patients maintaining responses beyond 2 years

**Evidence Strength: Moderate** - Phase 1 clinical data demonstrates proof-of-concept with strong immunogenicity and encouraging clinical responses. Larger randomized trials needed to confirm efficacy. The AI-driven approach represents a novel paradigm in personalized cancer immunotherapy.

### Why EVX-01 Was Developed

Cancer immunotherapy has transformed oncology treatment, but checkpoint inhibitors alone benefit only a subset of patients. The hypothesis underlying neoantigen vaccines is that:

1. **Tumor-specific neoantigens** arise from somatic mutations unique to each patient's cancer
2. **The immune system can recognize these** but often fails to mount sufficient responses
3. **Vaccination can prime and expand** neoantigen-specific T cells to attack the tumor
4. **Combination with checkpoint inhibitors** removes immunosuppressive brakes, allowing vaccine-primed T cells to function

**The challenge:** Each patient's tumor has a unique mutational profile, requiring truly personalized vaccine design. Evaxion's PIONEER platform uses artificial intelligence to:

- Analyze whole-exome sequencing and RNA-seq data from tumor biopsies
- Predict which mutations will generate immunogenic neoantigens
- Select optimal peptide sequences for vaccination
- Design patient-specific vaccine formulations

**Original therapeutic target:** Advanced melanoma, chosen because melanoma has high mutational burden and established responsiveness to immunotherapy.

<h2 id="mechanism-of-action">Mechanism of Action</h2>

### How EVX-01 Works (Plain English)

EVX-01 is a "personalized cancer vaccine" - but not like a flu shot. Here is how it works:

1. **Your tumor is unique** - Every patient's cancer has different genetic mistakes (mutations). Some of these mutations create abnormal proteins called "neoantigens" that exist ONLY in cancer cells, not normal cells.

2. **AI finds the targets** - Evaxion's PIONEER artificial intelligence analyzes your tumor's DNA to identify which mutations are most likely to trigger an immune response. Think of it like a detective finding fingerprints unique to the criminal.

3. **A vaccine made just for you** - Based on the AI predictions, scientists synthesize short protein fragments (peptides) matching your specific neoantigens. Typically 10-15 different peptides are combined into one vaccine.

4. **Training your immune system** - When injected with an adjuvant (immune booster), the vaccine teaches your immune system to recognize those specific neoantigens. Your T cells learn to identify and attack cells displaying those markers.

5. **Combining with checkpoint inhibitors** - Drugs like pembrolizumab (Keytruda) remove the "brakes" that cancer uses to hide from the immune system. The vaccine provides the "target," and the checkpoint inhibitor ensures T cells can act on it.

**Why this matters:** Standard immunotherapy helps ~40% of melanoma patients. By adding a personalized vaccine, the goal is to help more patients respond and respond more completely.

### The Science: PIONEER AI-Immunology Platform

EVX-01 represents the clinical application of Evaxion's PIONEER computational platform, which integrates multiple machine learning models to predict immunogenic neoantigens.

### Neoantigen Identification Pipeline

```
Patient Tumor Biopsy
    |
    v
Whole-Exome Sequencing (WES) + RNA-Seq
    |
    v
Somatic Mutation Calling
    |
    v
PIONEER AI Platform Analysis:
├── MHC-I binding prediction (HLA-A, -B, -C)
├── MHC-II binding prediction (HLA-DR, -DP, -DQ)
├── Proteasomal cleavage prediction
├── TAP transport prediction
├── Expression level filtering
└── Immunogenicity scoring
    |
    v
Top 10-15 Neoantigen Peptides Selected
    |
    v
Synthetic Long Peptide (SLP) Manufacturing
    |
    v
Patient-Specific Vaccine Formulation
```

### Synthetic Long Peptide (SLP) Design

EVX-01 uses synthetic long peptides (typically 20-30 amino acids) rather than minimal epitopes (8-10 amino acids) because:

| Feature | Short Peptide (8-10 aa) | Long Peptide (20-30 aa) |
|---------|-------------------------|-------------------------|
| MHC presentation | Direct loading, transient | Requires processing, more durable |
| Immune response | Primarily CD8+ T cells | Both CD4+ and CD8+ T cells |
| Duration | Short-lived responses | Prolonged presentation |
| Tolerance risk | Higher | Lower (requires APC processing) |

**Key insight:** Long peptides must be taken up by professional antigen-presenting cells (dendritic cells) and processed, ensuring proper cross-presentation and activation of both helper (CD4+) and cytotoxic (CD8+) T cells.

### Adjuvant System

EVX-01 is administered with an adjuvant to enhance immunogenicity:

- **Adjuvant choice:** Montanide ISA 51 VG (water-in-oil emulsion) and/or poly-ICLC (TLR3 agonist)
- **Mechanism:** Creates depot effect, activates innate immunity, promotes DC maturation
- **Rationale:** Peptide vaccines alone often induce weak responses; adjuvants are critical for efficacy

### Synergy with Checkpoint Inhibition

EVX-01 is designed for combination with anti-PD-1 therapy:

| Component | Function | Alone | Combined |
|-----------|----------|-------|----------|
| EVX-01 vaccine | Primes neoantigen-specific T cells | Limited expansion (TME suppression) | T cells activated and expanded |
| Anti-PD-1 (pembrolizumab) | Removes T cell brakes | Only helps existing responses | Allows vaccine-primed T cells to function |
| **Combined effect** | Synergistic | — | Enhanced tumor killing |

**Mechanistic rationale:**
1. Vaccine generates tumor-specific T cells (provides the "army")
2. PD-1 blockade prevents T cell exhaustion (removes the "handcuffs")
3. Together: more T cells + functional T cells = better tumor control

<h2 id="key-human-trials">Key Human Trials</h2>

### Phase 1/2a Trial in Melanoma

The primary clinical evidence for EVX-01 comes from an open-label Phase 1/2a study in patients with advanced melanoma.

**Trial Registration:** NCT03715985

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Design** | Open-label, Phase 1/2a |
| **Population** | Advanced/metastatic melanoma, checkpoint-inhibitor naive |
| **N (evaluable)** | 12 patients in primary analysis cohort |
| **Treatment** | EVX-01 + pembrolizumab (Keytruda) |
| **Vaccine schedule** | SC injections on weeks 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 11, 14, 20, 26 |
| **Primary endpoints** | Safety, immunogenicity |
| **Secondary endpoints** | Clinical response (ORR, PFS, DOR) |
| **Sponsor** | Evaxion Biotech |

### Key Efficacy Results

| Outcome | Result | Context |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Overall Response Rate (ORR)** | **67% (8/12)** | Historical pembrolizumab monotherapy ORR ~40% |
| **Complete Response (CR)** | 25% (3/12) | Complete tumor disappearance |
| **Partial Response (PR)** | 42% (5/12) | Significant tumor shrinkage |
| **Disease Control Rate (DCR)** | 83% (10/12) | CR + PR + SD |
| **Stable Disease (SD)** | 17% (2/12) | Tumor neither growing nor shrinking |
| **Progressive Disease (PD)** | 17% (2/12) | Despite treatment |

**Context:** The 67% ORR compares favorably to historical pembrolizumab monotherapy response rates of approximately 33-45% in similar populations. However, this is a small, non-randomized cohort, and cross-trial comparisons must be interpreted cautiously.

### Immunogenicity Results

| Measure | Result | Significance |
|---------|--------|--------------|
| **T-cell response rate** | **100% (12/12)** | All patients developed neoantigen-specific T cells |
| **Median neoantigens targeted** | 9 of 15 peptides | Majority of vaccine peptides induced responses |
| **CD8+ T cell responses** | Present in all responders | Cytotoxic T cells detected |
| **CD4+ T cell responses** | Present in majority | Helper T cells support durability |
| **Persistence** | Detected at 1 year+ | Responses maintained long-term |

**Key finding:** The 100% immunogenicity rate validates the PIONEER AI platform's ability to predict immunogenic neoantigens. This is a critical proof-of-concept for the personalized vaccine approach.

### Durability of Responses

| Duration | Patients with Ongoing Response |
|----------|--------------------------------|
| 6 months | 8/8 responders (100%) |
| 12 months | 7/8 responders (88%) |

Several patients demonstrated durable complete responses maintained beyond 2 years, suggesting potential for long-term disease control in a subset of patients.

### In Vitro Studies

- PIONEER predictions validated using human PBMC stimulation assays
- Predicted MHC binding confirmed by competition assays
- T cell reactivity to predicted neoantigens demonstrated in patient samples
- Cross-reactivity to wild-type sequences minimal (tumor specificity)

### Limitations of Preclinical Data

- Mouse models have different MHC (H-2) than humans (HLA)
- Tumor microenvironment differs between mice and humans
- Vaccination timing in models (prophylactic vs therapeutic) affects outcomes
- Translation of PIONEER predictions requires human validation

<h2 id="comparisons">Comparisons</h2>

### EVX-01 vs mRNA-4157 (V940)

EVX-01 (peptide-based) and mRNA-4157/V940 (Moderna/Merck, mRNA-based) represent two distinct approaches to personalized neoantigen vaccination.

| Factor | EVX-01 (Evaxion) | mRNA-4157/V940 (Moderna/Merck) |
|--------|------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Platform** | Synthetic long peptides (SLP) | mRNA encoding neoantigens |
| **Prediction AI** | PIONEER platform | Moderna proprietary algorithms |
| **Neoantigens per vaccine** | 10-15 peptides | Up to 34 neoantigens encoded |
| **Administration** | SC with adjuvant | IM injection (LNP-formulated) |
| **Manufacturing time** | 6-8 weeks | 4-6 weeks |
| **Clinical stage** | Phase 1/2a | Phase 3 (KEYNOTE-942) |
| **Partnership** | Evaxion standalone | Moderna + Merck |
| **Indication** | Melanoma | Melanoma (adjuvant) |
| **Pivotal data** | Phase 1: 67% ORR | Phase 2b: 44% RFS improvement |

### Mechanism Differences

| Aspect | Peptide (EVX-01) | mRNA (mRNA-4157) |
|--------|------------------|------------------|
| **Antigen presentation** | Requires DC uptake, processing | Intracellular expression after transfection |
| **Immune response** | CD4+ and CD8+ T cells | Primarily CD8+ T cells (some CD4+) |
| **Duration of antigen** | Depot effect with adjuvant | Transient expression (~days) |
| **Innate activation** | Adjuvant-dependent | Intrinsic (mRNA + LNP) |
| **Cold chain** | Standard (2-8C) | Ultra-cold initially (-20C to -70C) |

### Clinical Development Status Comparison

| Milestone | EVX-01 | mRNA-4157 |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Phase 1 complete | Yes | Yes |
| Phase 2 initiated | Yes (expansion) | Yes (KEYNOTE-942) |
| Phase 3 initiated | No | Yes (2024) |
| Breakthrough designation | No | Yes (FDA, 2023) |
| Regulatory submissions | No | Planned 2025-2026 |

**Key difference:** mRNA-4157 is significantly ahead in clinical development, with Phase 3 trials ongoing and Breakthrough Therapy designation. EVX-01 has demonstrated promising Phase 1 results but requires larger randomized trials to establish efficacy.

### Complementary Approaches

Both platforms validate the personalized neoantigen vaccine concept:

- **Shared hypothesis:** Patient-specific neoantigens can drive anti-tumor immunity
- **Shared challenge:** Manufacturing individualized vaccines at scale
- **Shared promise:** Potential to improve checkpoint inhibitor responses
- **Different solutions:** Peptide vs mRNA delivery of tumor-specific antigens
