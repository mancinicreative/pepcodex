## What is Dihexa?

Dihexa (N-hexanoic-Tyr-Ile-(6) aminohexanoic amide) is a synthetic peptide derived from angiotensin IV. It was developed at Washington State University as a potential cognitive enhancer. It is **not approved** for human use and has never been tested in human clinical trials.

## Key Facts

| Fact | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **FDA Approved** | No |
| **Human Trials** | None |
| **Administration** | Oral (claimed), intranasal |
| **Evidence Level** | Preclinical only |

## How Is It Supposed to Work?

Dihexa is designed to enhance the activity of hepatocyte growth factor (HGF) at its receptor (c-Met). This pathway is involved in:
- Neuronal growth and survival
- Synapse formation
- Brain plasticity

The compound is claimed to be orally active and able to cross the blood-brain barrier, though this has not been verified in humans.

## Research Status

### What Studies Show

- Animal studies (primarily rats) showed improved memory and learning
- Researchers reported it was more potent than BDNF in promoting synapse formation
- Studies used healthy and dementia-model animals
- All data is preclinical

### Evidence Quality

The evidence is **extremely preliminary**:
- Only animal studies exist
- No human safety or efficacy data
- Research comes primarily from one lab
- No clinical development announced

## Significant Concerns

### HGF and Cancer

The HGF/c-Met pathway is frequently overactive in cancer cells. Compounds that enhance this pathway raise theoretical concerns about tumor growth. This is a major reason for caution.

### Unknown Safety

- Never tested in humans
- Long-term effects completely unknown
- Dose-response in humans not established
- Theoretical cancer risk from mechanism

## Important Warnings

- **No human data exists**—all claims extrapolate from animal studies
- Products sold online are entirely unregulated
- The HGF pathway has known cancer associations
- This is a highly experimental compound
