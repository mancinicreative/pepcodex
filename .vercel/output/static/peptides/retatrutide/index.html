Overview

Overview

Quick Summary

  • Triple agonist targeting GIP, GLP-1, and glucagon receptors simultaneously
  • Not yet FDA approved - currently in Phase 3 clinical trials (TRIUMPH program)
  • First Phase 3 results (TRIUMPH-4): 28.7% weight loss at 68 weeks in patients with knee osteoarthritis
  • Phase 2 results: Up to 24.2% weight loss at 48 weeks in patients with obesity
  • Differentiated mechanism from tirzepatide (dual) and semaglutide (single)
  • Being developed by Eli Lilly for obesity, type 2 diabetes, MASLD, and osteoarthritis

Evidence Strength: Moderate — Strong Phase 2 data with consistent results. First Phase 3 results positive (TRIUMPH-4, Dec 2025). Additional Phase 3 trials ongoing. No regulatory approval yet.

Mechanism of Action

How Retatrutide Works

Retatrutide activates three different hormone receptors at once, making it the most comprehensive weight loss peptide in development. Here’s what each receptor does:

  1. GLP-1 receptor - Tells your brain you’re full and slows down digestion. This is the same receptor that Ozempic targets.

  2. GIP receptor - Works with GLP-1 to boost insulin release after meals and may help your fat cells function more healthily. This is what Mounjaro adds on top of GLP-1.

  3. Glucagon receptor - This is retatrutide’s unique addition. Glucagon tells your liver to burn fat for energy and increases your metabolic rate. It’s like having your internal furnace turned up.

Why the glucagon receptor matters: The liver has almost no GLP-1 or GIP receptors, but it’s packed with glucagon receptors. This is why retatrutide may be especially effective for fatty liver disease, reducing liver fat by over 80% in trials.

The balancing act: Adding glucagon might seem counterintuitive since it raises blood sugar. However, the GLP-1 and GIP components counteract this by boosting insulin, resulting in improved blood sugar control alongside greater weight loss than either mechanism alone.

Triple Receptor Science

Retatrutide is a triple receptor agonist (GIP/GLP-1/Glucagon), the most comprehensive incretin-based therapy in development. It adds glucagon receptor agonism to the dual mechanism of tirzepatide.

Why the Liver Is Key

Critical insight: The liver has NO GLP-1 or GIP receptors but is rich in glucagon receptors.

This makes retatrutide uniquely positioned for:

  • NASH/MASLD treatment (direct liver effects)
  • Hepatic fat reduction (82% reduction in Phase 2a)
  • Improved mitochondrial function
  • Anti-fibrotic benefits

Safety Balancing Act

The GLP-1/GIP components counterbalance glucagon’s hyperglycemic potential:

  • GLP-1/GIP → ↑ insulin, ↓ glucagon secretion
  • GCGR → hepatic glucose production
  • Net effect: Improved glycemia with greater weight loss

Unique Liver Findings

Phase 2a MASLD trial results:

  • 81-82% liver fat reduction at 48 weeks
  • Direct GCGR-mediated hepatoprotection
  • Potential to be first triple agonist approved for liver disease

Key Evidence Highlights

  1. Phase 3 TRIUMPH-4: 28.7% weight loss at 68 weeks (12mg), with significant osteoarthritis pain relief
  2. Phase 2 Obesity: Up to 24.2% weight loss at 48 weeks (NEJM 2023)
  3. Phase 2 T2D: 16.9% weight loss, HbA1c -2.02% at 36 weeks (Lancet 2023)
  4. Phase 2a MASLD: 82-86% liver fat reduction at 48 weeks (Nature Medicine 2024)
  5. Tolerability: GI effects similar to class; manageable with dose titration
  6. Differentiation: Greater weight loss than seen with tirzepatide in cross-trial comparisons
