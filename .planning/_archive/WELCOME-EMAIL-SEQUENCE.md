## Email 2: How We're Different

**Send:** 2 days after signup
**Subject Line Options:**
1. How we grade peptide evidence (and why it matters)
2. Why most peptide content is unreliable
3. The framework behind every PepCodex page

**Preview Text:** Not all studies are equal. Here's how we filter the noise.

---

**Body:**

Most peptide content online has a problem:

It doesn't tell you whether the evidence is strong or weak.

A rat study gets treated the same as a Phase 3 trial. A case report sounds as convincing as a meta-analysis. That's how misinformation spreads.

**How we do it differently:**

Every claim on PepCodex gets an evidence grade:

- **HIGH** — Multiple RCTs, meta-analyses, strong human data
- **MODERATE** — Single RCTs, strong observational studies
- **LOW** — Limited human data, mostly animal studies
- **VERY LOW** — Preclinical only, theoretical, or conflicting

We also mark whether evidence comes from human or animal studies. Because a rat study, no matter how promising, isn't proof of anything in humans.

**Why this matters:**

When you read a PepCodex dossier, you know exactly how confident to be in each claim. No guessing. No hype.

→ [See the full methodology](https://pepcodex.com/methodology/)

— PepCodex
