## Overview

### Quick Summary

- **Mitochondria-derived peptide (MDP)** — encoded within mitochondrial 16S rRNA gene
- **24 amino acids** in length (endogenous form)
- **Discovered 2001** by Japanese researchers screening for Alzheimer's protective factors
- **Not approved** by any regulatory agency for therapeutic use
- **Extensive academic research** — 1,500+ publications indexed on PubMed
- **Key effects:** Cytoprotection, anti-apoptosis, neuroprotection, metabolic regulation

**Evidence Strength: Moderate** — Substantial preclinical evidence from multiple independent research groups worldwide, some human biomarker studies, but no completed therapeutic clinical trials.

## Mechanism of Action

### Discovery Context

Humanin was discovered through an innovative "death trap" assay:

1. Researchers screened cDNA libraries for proteins that could rescue cells from Alzheimer's-related death
2. Clones that kept cells alive were isolated
3. One protective factor mapped to mitochondrial DNA
4. This revealed a new class of peptides: mitochondria-derived peptides (MDPs)

### Primary Mechanisms

**1. Receptor Signaling (Extracellular)**

```
Humanin (secreted/exogenous)
    ↓
FPRL1/2 receptor (formyl peptide receptors)
    ↓
├── ERK1/2 activation
├── STAT3 signaling
├── Anti-apoptotic cascades
└── Cytoprotective gene expression
```

**2. Binding Partners**

| Partner | Interaction | Consequence |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **BAX** | Direct binding | Blocks pro-apoptotic BAX activity |
| **Bid** | Direct binding | Prevents mitochondrial permeabilization |
| **IGFBP-3** | Binding partner | Modulates IGF signaling |
| **TRIM11** | Binding partner | Affects intracellular signaling |

**3. Mitochondrial Effects**

```
Humanin → Mitochondria
    ↓
├── Improved mitochondrial function
├── Reduced oxidative stress
├── Decreased cytochrome c release
└── Prevention of apoptosis cascade
```

### Key Discovery Context

Humanin was discovered through functional expression screening of a cDNA library from the occipital lobe of an Alzheimer's patient. The peptide protected neurons from death induced by amyloid-beta and mutant FAD genes (APP, PS1, PS2). The S14G modification (HNG) increases potency approximately 1000-fold.

## Summary

Humanin represents a significant scientific discovery:

**What We Know:**
- Mitochondria-derived peptide discovered in 2001
- Cytoprotective and anti-apoptotic
- BAX binding and receptor signaling characterized
- Human levels correlate with health outcomes
- Extensive independent preclinical validation

**What We Don't Know:**
- Whether therapeutic administration will work in humans
- Long-term safety profile
- Relationship with cancer
- Optimal therapeutic applications

**Bottom Line:** Humanin has the strongest evidence base of any longevity-focused peptide in this database. The discovery opened an entirely new field (MDPs) and has robust independent replication. However, despite 20+ years of research, no therapeutic has advanced to market. The complexity of its biology and the challenges of peptide development explain this gap. For now, humanin remains an important research molecule with therapeutic potential that remains unrealized.
