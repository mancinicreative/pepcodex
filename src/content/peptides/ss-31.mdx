## Overview

<h2 id="overview" class="sr-only">Overview</h2>

### Quick Summary

- **First-in-class mitochondria-targeting tetrapeptide** that binds cardiolipin
- **Not FDA approved** - multiple Phase 3 trials completed with mixed results
- **Developed by Stealth BioTherapeutics** (Newton, MA)
- **Strong preclinical evidence** across 15+ disease models
- **Clinical development** in mitochondrial myopathy, Barth syndrome, heart failure, AMD

**Evidence Strength: Moderate** - Robust preclinical data and clear mechanism, but Phase 3 trials in primary mitochondrial myopathy did not meet primary endpoints. Barth syndrome trial showed positive results in a small population.

<h2 id="mechanism-of-action">Mechanism of Action</h2>

### How SS-31 Works

SS-31 is fundamentally different from typical antioxidants. Rather than scavenging free radicals after they form, it goes directly to the source - the mitochondria - and prevents excess reactive oxygen species (ROS) from being produced in the first place.

1. **Targets the inner mitochondrial membrane** - SS-31 is a small, cell-permeable peptide that concentrates 1,000-5,000x inside mitochondria within minutes. It specifically localizes to the inner mitochondrial membrane where the electron transport chain operates.

2. **Binds to cardiolipin** - Cardiolipin is a unique phospholipid found almost exclusively in the inner mitochondrial membrane. It's essential for the proper function of respiratory complexes (I, III, IV) and ATP synthase. SS-31 binds to cardiolipin and optimizes its interactions with these complexes.

3. **Optimizes electron transport** - When SS-31 binds cardiolipin, it improves the efficiency of electron transfer through the respiratory chain. This means more ATP is produced per oxygen consumed, and fewer electrons "leak" to form damaging superoxide radicals.

4. **Prevents cardiolipin peroxidation** - During stress (ischemia, aging, disease), cardiolipin becomes oxidized and dysfunctional. SS-31 protects cardiolipin from oxidative damage, maintaining mitochondrial integrity.

**Why this matters:** Mitochondrial dysfunction is implicated in heart failure, neurodegenerative diseases, aging, and inherited mitochondrial disorders. By targeting the root cause of mitochondrial inefficiency, SS-31 aims to address multiple diseases with a single mechanism.

### Molecular Structure and Properties

SS-31 belongs to the Szeto-Schiller (SS) peptide family, designed for mitochondrial targeting:

**Sequence:** D-Arg-Dmt-Lys-Phe-NH2

| Feature | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **D-Arginine** | Provides positive charge for membrane interaction |
| **Dmt (dimethyltyrosine)** | Modified aromatic amino acid; key for cardiolipin binding |
| **Lysine** | Additional positive charge |
| **Phenylalanine** | Aromatic residue for membrane partitioning |
| **C-terminal amide** | Increases stability |

**Key Properties:**
- Alternating aromatic-cationic motif enables membrane penetration
- Net charge of +3 at physiological pH
- Molecular weight: ~640 Da
- Rapidly crosses plasma membrane without requiring active transport

### Cardiolipin Interaction Mechanism

```
SS-31 enters cell (minutes)
    |
Concentrates 1000-5000x in mitochondria
    |
Binds cardiolipin on inner mitochondrial membrane
    |
+-- Optimizes cytochrome c binding to cardiolipin
|   +-- Enhances electron transfer efficiency
|   +-- Reduces electron leak at Complex I and III
|
+-- Protects cardiolipin from peroxidation
|   +-- Maintains cristae structure
|   +-- Preserves respiratory supercomplex assembly
|
+-- Stabilizes mitochondrial membrane potential
    +-- Prevents mPTP opening
    +-- Reduces cytochrome c release
    +-- Inhibits apoptotic cascade
```

### Why Cardiolipin is Critical

Cardiolipin is not just a structural lipid - it's functionally essential:

| Function | Consequence of Dysfunction |
|----------|---------------------------|
| Anchors cytochrome c | Loss leads to apoptosis |
| Organizes respiratory supercomplexes | Decreased ATP production |
| Maintains cristae morphology | Structural collapse |
| Regulates mPTP | Uncontrolled cell death |
| ATP synthase dimerization | Impaired energy production |

**Disease relevance:** Cardiolipin abnormalities occur in:
- Barth syndrome (genetic cardiolipin remodeling defect)
- Heart failure (cardiolipin depletion)
- Ischemia-reperfusion injury (cardiolipin oxidation)
- Aging (progressive cardiolipin loss)
- Neurodegenerative diseases (cardiolipin peroxidation)

### Distinguishing SS-31 from Conventional Antioxidants

| Property | SS-31 | Vitamin E/C | MitoQ |
|----------|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Targeting** | Inner mitochondrial membrane | Non-specific | Mitochondrial matrix |
| **Mechanism** | Optimizes ETC function | Scavenges ROS | Scavenges ROS |
| **Cardiolipin interaction** | Direct binding | None | Indirect |
| **Concentration in mito** | 1000-5000x | Minimal | 100-500x |
| **Effect on bioenergetics** | Improves ATP production | None | Variable |

### Evidence Overview

### Key Evidence Highlights

1. **Barth Syndrome (Positive):** 67-meter improvement in 6-minute walk test; significant improvements in muscle strength and fatigue (n=12)

2. **Primary Mitochondrial Myopathy (Negative for primary endpoint):** TAZPOWER and MMPOWER-3 Phase 3 trials did not meet primary endpoint (6MWT at 24 weeks); post-hoc subgroup analyses showed improvements

3. **Heart Failure (Mixed):** PROGRESS-HF showed trends but not significant improvements in LVESV; preclinical data remains compelling

4. **STEMI (Negative for primary endpoint):** EMBRACE STEMI did not reduce CK-MB AUC; exploratory anterior MI subgroup showed signals

5. **Age-Related Macular Degeneration (Promising):** ReCLAIM Phase 2 showed improved low-luminance visual acuity; ReCLAIM-2 ongoing

6. **Preclinical:** Consistent efficacy in 15+ animal models including heart failure, ischemia-reperfusion, ALS, Parkinson's, kidney disease, muscle atrophy

<h2 id="key-human-trials">Key Human Trials</h2>

### Phase 3 Trials: Primary Mitochondrial Myopathy

| Trial | NCT ID | N | Duration | Primary Endpoint | Result |
|-------|--------|---|----------|------------------|--------|
| TAZPOWER | NCT02805790 | 218 | 24 weeks | 6MWT change | Not met |
| MMPOWER-3 | NCT03323749 | 120 | 24 weeks | 6MWT change | Not met |

**TAZPOWER (2021):** 218 patients with genetically confirmed primary mitochondrial myopathy; randomized to elamipretide 40 mg SC daily or placebo. Primary endpoint (change in 6-minute walk test at 24 weeks) was not statistically significant. Long-term extension (144 weeks) showed durable improvements in completers.

**MMPOWER-3 (2023):** Confirmatory Phase 3 trial with subcutaneous formulation. Primary endpoint not met. Post-hoc analyses identified potential responder populations.

**FDA Response:** Complete Response Letter issued in 2021; requested additional data for PMM indication.

### Phase 2/3 Trial: Barth Syndrome

| Parameter | Elamipretide | Placebo | p-value |
|-----------|--------------|---------|---------|
| N | 12 | Crossover | - |
| Duration | 12 weeks | 12 weeks | - |
| 6MWT improvement | +67 meters | - | &lt;0.05 |
| Muscle strength | Improved | - | &lt;0.05 |
| Fatigue score | Improved | - | &lt;0.05 |

**Reference:** Reid Thompson W et al. Genet Med. 2021 (PMID: 32994548)

**Why Barth is different:** Barth syndrome is caused by mutations in TAZ gene, which encodes tafazzin - the enzyme responsible for cardiolipin remodeling. This creates a direct mechanistic rationale for SS-31, which targets cardiolipin function.

### Phase 2 Trials: Cardiovascular

| Trial | NCT ID | N | Indication | Primary Endpoint | Result |
|-------|--------|---|------------|------------------|--------|
| EMBRACE STEMI | NCT02388464 | 118 | STEMI + PCI | CK-MB AUC | Not met |
| PROGRESS-HF | NCT02814097 | 71 | HFrEF | LVESV change | Not met |
| EVREST | NCT02693119 | 14 | Renal artery stenosis | Cortical perfusion | Positive |

**EMBRACE STEMI (2016):** Elamipretide given IV at time of reperfusion in STEMI patients undergoing PCI. CK-MB AUC (marker of infarct size) was not significantly reduced. Exploratory analyses showed trends in anterior MI subgroup.

**PROGRESS-HF (2020):** 71 patients with HFrEF (LVEF ≤40%) randomized to elamipretide or placebo for 28 days. Primary endpoint (LVESV change) not met. Secondary endpoints showed trends toward improved LVEF.

### Phase 2 Trials: Ophthalmology

| Trial | NCT ID | N | Indication | Key Finding |
|-------|--------|---|------------|-------------|
| ReCLAIM | NCT03098797 | 40 | Dry AMD | Improved LLVA |
| ReCLAIM-2 | NCT03891875 | 180 | Geographic atrophy | Ongoing |

**ReCLAIM (2019):** Subcutaneous elamipretide in patients with dry age-related macular degeneration. Showed statistically significant improvement in low-luminance visual acuity (LLVA). Supported larger Phase 2b trial.

**ReCLAIM-2 (Ongoing):** Phase 2b trial evaluating effect on geographic atrophy progression. Results expected 2026.

### Key Preclinical Insights

**Aging and Muscle:**
- Single 1-hour SS-31 treatment in aged mice reversed mitochondrial dysfunction
- Improved fatigue resistance and ATP production
- Effects rapid and sustained

**Heart Failure:**
- 3-month treatment in canine HF model normalized mitochondrial respiration
- Improved LVEF from 25% to 35%
- Reduced LV end-systolic and end-diastolic volumes
- Decreased reactive oxygen species generation

**Ischemia-Reperfusion:**
- Effective when administered at time of reperfusion (clinically relevant)
- Reduced infarct size across multiple species
- Protected mitochondrial function without affecting hemodynamics

**Neurodegeneration:**
- Efficacy in MPTP Parkinson's model (protected dopamine neurons)
- Extended survival in ALS model
- Restored mitochondrial transport in Alzheimer's model

### Translational Gap

Despite strong preclinical efficacy, clinical translation has been challenging:

| Factor | Preclinical | Clinical |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Population** | Homogeneous models | Heterogeneous patients |
| **Disease stage** | Early/acute | Advanced/chronic |
| **Endpoints** | Objective (infarct size) | Functional (6MWT) |
| **Duration** | Days to weeks | Months |
| **Mitochondrial defect** | Defined | Variable |

<h2 id="safety-profile">Safety Profile</h2>

### Clinical Trial Safety Summary

SS-31/Elamipretide has demonstrated a favorable safety profile across clinical trials:

| Event | Incidence | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Injection site reactions | 15-25% | Most common; mild erythema, pain |
| Headache | 5-10% | Mild to moderate |
| Fatigue | 3-8% | Transient |
| Nausea | 3-5% | Generally mild |
| Dizziness | 2-5% | Transient |

### Serious Adverse Events

- No drug-related serious adverse events attributed to elamipretide in Phase 2/3 trials
- No significant laboratory abnormalities
- No cardiac safety signals (QTc, arrhythmias)
- No immunogenicity concerns

### Long-Term Safety

Long-term extension studies (up to 144 weeks) showed:
- Continued favorable safety profile
- No cumulative toxicity observed
- Injection site reactions decreased over time

### Preclinical Safety

- No genotoxicity in standard battery
- No carcinogenicity signals in chronic studies
- No reproductive/developmental toxicity observed
- Wide therapeutic index in animal studies

### Populations Not Yet Studied

- Pediatric patients (limited data)
- Pregnancy and lactation
- Severe renal or hepatic impairment
- Drug-drug interactions not fully characterized

### Mitochondrial Pharmacokinetics

**Unique consideration:** Plasma levels do not reflect tissue/mitochondrial concentrations.

- SS-31 concentrates 1,000-5,000x in mitochondria
- Rapid uptake (minutes) into mitochondria
- Tissue half-life likely longer than plasma half-life
- Cardiolipin binding creates functional "reservoir"

- Once-daily SC dosing achieves steady state within days
- No dose adjustment needed for mild-moderate renal impairment
- Food does not affect absorption
- No significant drug-drug interactions identified

**Aging and Longevity:**

- No clinical trials yet initiated

- Strong preclinical data in ischemia-reperfusion models
- Academic interest in transplant nephrology

- Paclitaxel-induced neuropathy prevention in preclinical models
- Potential for cardio-oncology applications

### Mechanistic Research

**Ongoing investigations:**
- Precise stoichiometry of SS-31:cardiolipin binding
- Effects on supercomplex assembly dynamics
- Tissue-specific pharmacokinetics
- Biomarkers of mitochondrial response

### Future Directions

1. **Better patient selection:** Identifying mitochondrial dysfunction biomarkers
2. **Optimal endpoints:** Beyond 6MWT to capture mitochondrial-specific effects
3. **Combination therapy:** With other mitochondrial-targeting agents
4. **Earlier disease stages:** When mitochondria are still rescuable
