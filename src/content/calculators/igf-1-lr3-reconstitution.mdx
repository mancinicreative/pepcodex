## What This Calculator Does

This tool calculates the **concentration** of a reconstituted IGF-1 LR3 solution based on two inputs: the mass of peptide in the vial and the volume of diluent added. The result tells you how many micrograms (mcg) of peptide are present per unit of volume, which is useful for laboratory preparation and measurement planning.

## Typical Research Context

IGF-1 LR3 is a modified analog of insulin-like growth factor 1 with reduced binding protein affinity, extending its half-life in research settings. It is typically supplied as a lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder and requires reconstitution with a suitable diluent before use in vitro or in experimental protocols.

| Parameter | Common Research Values |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Typical vial sizes | 0.1 mg, 1 mg |
| Common diluent | Bacteriostatic water |
| Typical diluent volume | 1 mL |

## How to Use the Calculator

1. **Enter the vial amount** in milligrams (mg). Check the label or certificate of analysis for the exact mass.
2. **Enter the diluent volume** in milliliters (mL). This is the amount of bacteriostatic water or sterile water you plan to add.
3. **Enter your desired unit dose** in micrograms (mcg) if you want to see how much volume to draw for a specific amount.
4. The calculator returns:
   - **Concentration**: mg/mL or mcg/mL
   - **Volume per dose**: how many units (or mL) contain your desired mcg amount

## Important Notes

- **This is not a dosing tool.** We do not provide, suggest, or validate any dose. The calculator only converts between mass and volume based on your inputs.
- **Unit consistency matters.** The calculator assumes standard metric units. Double-check your vial label for whether the mass is listed in mg or mcg.
- **Stability after reconstitution.** Reconstituted peptides degrade faster than lyophilized powder. Store reconstituted solutions according to manufacturer or protocol guidelines, typically refrigerated and used within days to weeks.
