The mechanism of mRNA-4157 is based on personalized neoantigen presentation through lipid nanoparticle-encapsulated mRNA delivery, validated in Phase 2b clinical trials.

### How It Works (Simplified)

mRNA-4157 functions as a personalized cancer vaccine that trains the immune system to recognize and attack tumor-specific neoantigens:

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 not-prose my-8">
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Tumor Sequencing</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Patient tumor DNA is sequenced to identify up to 34 unique neoantigens (mutated proteins) that distinguish cancer cells from normal cells.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">mRNA Delivery</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Personalized mRNA encoding all neoantigens is encapsulated in lipid nanoparticles and injected, where it enters antigen-presenting cells.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">T Cell Activation</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Cells express neoantigens on MHC molecules, activating CD8+ killer T cells and CD4+ helper T cells specific to the patient's tumor.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Checkpoint Synergy</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Combined with pembrolizumab (PD-1 inhibitor) to release immune brakes, allowing activated T cells to attack residual cancer cells at full capacity.</p>
  </div>
</div>

**Key Research:** Weber JS et al. (Lancet, 2024) demonstrated 44% reduction in recurrence or death with combination therapy. [PMID:38246194](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38246194/)

### Important Limitations

- Investigational product; not approved for any indication
- Phase 3 confirmatory trials (INTerpath) ongoing with results expected 2027-2029
- Requires approximately 6 weeks manufacturing time from tumor biopsy
- Available only through registered clinical trials
- Long-term efficacy and overall survival benefit not yet confirmed
