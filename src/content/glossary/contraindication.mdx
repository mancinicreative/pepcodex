## How Contraindications Work

### Types of Contraindications

| Type | Definition | Action |
|------|------------|--------|
| **Absolute** | Must never use | Do not prescribe |
| **Relative** | Use with extreme caution | Weigh risks vs benefits |

### What Creates a Contraindication
- Known severe adverse reactions
- Drug interactions causing harm
- Underlying conditions worsening
- Lack of safety data in population

## Relevance to Peptides

### GLP-1 Agonist Contraindications

**Absolute Contraindications**
- Personal history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC)
- Family history of MTC
- Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2)
- Known hypersensitivity to the drug

**Relative Contraindications/Precautions**
- History of pancreatitis
- Severe gastrointestinal disease
- Diabetic retinopathy (for rapid glucose improvement)
- Pregnancy/breastfeeding

## Understanding the Distinction

### Absolute Contraindication Example
A patient with personal history of medullary thyroid carcinoma cannot receive semaglutide under any circumstances. The potential risk of tumor recurrence or growth outweighs any potential benefit.

### Relative Contraindication Example
A patient with history of pancreatitis might still receive GLP-1 therapy if:
- Benefits significantly outweigh risks
- Close monitoring is implemented
- Alternative treatments are inadequate
- Patient provides informed consent

## Checking for Contraindications

### Before Starting Peptide Therapy

| Step | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Medical history review | Identify absolute contraindications |
| Family history | Screen for hereditary conditions |
| Current medications | Check for interactions |
| Allergy assessment | Prevent hypersensitivity reactions |

### Ongoing Monitoring
- Report new symptoms promptly
- Regular follow-up appointments
- Medication reconciliation
- Update medical history

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What happens if someone takes a contraindicated medication?

Depending on the contraindication, consequences range from mild to severe. Absolute contraindications exist because serious harm is likely. If you've taken a contraindicated medication, contact your healthcare provider immediately for assessment and monitoring.

### Can contraindications change over time?

Yes. As new data emerges, FDA may add or remove contraindications. Additionally, individual circumstances change - someone might develop a condition that creates a new contraindication, or treatment for an underlying condition might resolve a previous concern.
