## Overview

Tirzepatide is FDA-approved while retatrutide is investigational. Retatrutide adds glucagon receptor agonism to the GIP/GLP-1 combination.

## Mechanism Comparison

| Aspect | Tirzepatide | Retatrutide |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **Receptors** | GIP + GLP-1 | GIP + GLP-1 + Glucagon |
| **Class** | Dual agonist | Triple agonist |
| **Novel Element** | First dual agonist | Adds glucagon |

## Regulatory Status

| Aspect | Tirzepatide | Retatrutide |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **FDA Status** | Approved | Phase 3 |
| **Indications** | T2D, Weight | None yet |
| **Availability** | Prescription | Investigational |

## Glucagon Component Theory

Retatrutide's glucagon receptor agonism may:
- Increase energy expenditure
- Enhance fat oxidation
- Provide thermogenic effects
- Accelerate weight loss beyond satiety
