The mechanism of zelenectide pevedotin is well-characterized through extensive preclinical and clinical research. Human clinical data supports the proposed mechanism.

### How It Works (Simplified)

Zelenectide pevedotin functions as a precision "smart missile" targeting cancer cells:

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 not-prose my-8">
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Target Recognition</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Bicyclic peptide binds Nectin-4 protein overexpressed on cancer cells (60-90% in urothelial cancer) with sub-nanomolar affinity.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Rapid Penetration</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">Small size (~2-3 kDa vs ~150 kDa for ADCs) enables fast, deep tumor penetration within hours rather than days.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Payload Delivery</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">After internalization, lysosomal enzymes cleave the linker releasing MMAE cytotoxin inside the cancer cell.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-[color:var(--primary-soft)] rounded-lg p-5 border border-[color:var(--primary-border)]">
    <div class="font-semibold text-[color:var(--ink)] mb-2">Cell Death</div>
    <p class="text-sm text-[color:var(--ink-muted)]">MMAE blocks tubulin polymerization, arrests mitosis, and triggers apoptosis. Bystander effect kills neighboring tumor cells.</p>
  </div>
</div>

**Key Research:** Rigby M, Bennett G et al. (2022) characterized BT8009 as a small hydrophilic Bicycle Toxin Conjugate that rapidly diffuses through tissue to penetrate Nectin-4-expressing tumors, with antitumor activity superior or equivalent to an enfortumab vedotin analog in preclinical models. [PMID:36112771](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36112771/)

### Important Limitations

- Investigational drug not yet approved by FDA or other regulatory agencies
- Available only through clinical trial enrollment (NCT04561362, NCT05936528)
- No head-to-head trials vs enfortumab vedotin completed
- Long-term survival data still maturing (median follow-up ~2-3 years)
- Optimal sequencing with other Nectin-4 targeting agents unknown
