PT-141

Also known as: Bremelanotide, Vyleesi

An FDA-approved melanocortin receptor agonist for hypoactive sexual desire disorder (HSDD) in premenopausal women. Acts centrally rather than on vascular mechanisms. Phase 3 RECONNECT trials demonstrated efficacy.

Overview

Overview

Quick Summary

  • Melanocortin-4 receptor agonist that acts on central nervous system
  • FDA approved (2019) as Vyleesi for HSDD in premenopausal women
  • First CNS-acting approved treatment for female sexual dysfunction
  • On-demand dosing - taken before anticipated sexual activity
  • Derived from Melanotan II but without tanning effects at approved level

Evidence Strength: High - FDA approval based on Phase 3 trials; clear efficacy signal for indicated population.

Mechanism of Action

How PT-141 Works

Unlike Viagra and similar drugs that work by improving blood flow to genitals, PT-141 works in the brain. It activates receptors in a part of the brain called the hypothalamus that controls sexual desire and arousal. This is why PT-141 can increase desire itself, not just physical response.

Think of it this way: Viagra helps the body respond to desire that’s already there. PT-141 may help generate that desire in the first place by triggering the release of dopamine (the “wanting” chemical) in brain regions that control sexual motivation.

This brain-based mechanism is also why PT-141 is the first FDA-approved treatment for low sexual desire in women. Previous drugs that worked on blood flow had minimal effect on female sexual function.

Melanocortin Receptor Binding

PT-141 (bremelanotide) is a cyclic heptapeptide derived from alpha-MSH that activates multiple melanocortin receptors with the following potency order:

ReceptorRelative PotencyKey Tissue Expression
MC1RHighestSkin melanocytes (tanning)
MC4RHighHypothalamus (sexual function)
MC3RModerateHypothalamus, limbic system
MC5RLowerExocrine glands
MC2RLowestAdrenal cortex

MC4R is the most relevant at therapeutic doses for sexual function.

  • Oxytocinergic activation: Bonding and arousal pathways

Warnings and Precautions

  • Blood pressure: Transient increases observed
  • Nausea: Can be significant; limits use
  • Cardiovascular: Caution in uncontrolled hypertension
  • Focal hyperpigmentation: Possible with repeated use

Contraindications

  • Uncontrolled hypertension
  • Known cardiovascular disease
  • Concomitant use with naltrexone (interaction)
