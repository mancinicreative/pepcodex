Cerebrolysin is a porcine brain-derived peptide mixture used in 50+ countries for stroke, dementia, and traumatic brain injury — but it is **[not FDA-approved](/regulatory-tracker/)** in the United States. For regulatory context, see our [FDA approval glossary entry](/glossary/fda-approval/) and [regulatory tracker](/regulatory-tracker/).

<h2 id="mechanism-of-action">Mechanism of Action</h2>

Cerebrolysin is a complex mixture of porcine brain-derived peptide fragments and free amino acids. Its proposed mechanisms are supported by both preclinical studies and multiple Phase 3 clinical trials, though the exact active components remain to be fully characterized.

### How It Works (Simplified)

Cerebrolysin contains small protein fragments that mimic natural brain growth factors (neurotrophins), providing multimodal neuroprotective and neurorestorative support:

**Key Research:** CARS trial (Muresanu DF et al. 2015) demonstrated significant upper-limb motor recovery (day-90 Action Research Arm Test, Mann-Whitney 0.71) in stroke patients. [PMID:26564102](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26564102/). Alzheimer's meta-analysis (Gauthier S et al. 2015) showed OR 3.32 for global clinical improvement across 6 RCTs. [PMID:25832905](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25832905/)

### Important Limitations

- Largest stroke trial (CASTA, N=1070) **failed primary endpoint** - benefit only in severe subgroup [PMID:22282884]
- Cochrane reviews conclude "**no convincing evidence**" for mortality/dependence reduction in stroke
- Complex mixture composition makes identifying active components difficult
- **Not FDA-approved** despite 50+ country approvals - no NDA filed
- Most trials manufacturer-sponsored (EVER Neuro Pharma)
- Russian and Chinese primary literature not fully accessible in English
